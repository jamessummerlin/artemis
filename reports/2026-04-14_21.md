# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-14 21:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II remains in post-mission status with all four crew members undergoing rehabilitation and debriefs at Johnson Space Center ahead of their April 16 postflight news conference. New post-mission reporting this cycle reveals that ESA's European Service Module (ESM-2) exhibited higher-than-expected propellant valve leak rates during the flight, a finding that will require design modifications for ESM-3 and beyond. Concurrently, the Wyss Institute has confirmed that single-cell RNA sequencing of the returned AVATAR organ-chip samples is now underway, with results expected to provide the first direct look at how deep-space radiation and microgravity affect human bone marrow at the cellular level.

---

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — crew in structured post-flight reconditioning, medical evaluations, human performance assessments, and lunar science debriefs at Johnson Space Center
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC, Launch Complex 39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown:** April 10, 2026 at 8:07 p.m. EDT (April 11, 00:07 UTC), Pacific Ocean ~50–80 miles off San Diego; recovery vessel USS John P. Murtha (LPD 26)
- **Mission Duration:** ~9 days, 25.5 hours (~218 hours)
- **Total Distance Traveled:** 694,481 miles — new human spaceflight distance record
- **Farthest Distance from Earth:** 252,756 miles (lunar flyby, April 6)

  - Wyss Institute/Harvard AVATAR organ chip single-cell RNA sequencing confirmed underway
  - Houston Public Media published a detailed Artemis III/IV outlook (April 14)
  - Nature published analysis of European science contributions and ESA's strategic ambitions post-Artemis II

### Technical & Engineering

- **ESA/ESM-2 postflight assessment — valve leak anomaly identified:**
  ESA's postflight assessment of the European Service Module (ESM-2) revealed that valve leak rates during flight were higher than predicted by pre-mission models. ESA officials confirmed the ESM-2 performed all required mission functions nominally — propulsion, power, thermal, and life support — and that crew safety was not at risk. However, the leak rate exceedance will require valve system design modifications for ESM-3 (Artemis III) and subsequent modules. ESA is conducting detailed engineering analysis; a formal anomaly report is expected within 60 days.
  - [ESA — Artemis II (mission page)](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Orion/Artemis_II)
  - [ESA — Splashdown for Artemis II (press release)](https://www.esa.int/Newsroom/Press_Releases/Splashdown_for_Artemis_II)
  - [ESA — European Service Module engines powering Artemis II](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/European_Service_Module_engines_powering_Artemis_II)

- **Orion heat shield — 30-day inspection ongoing at KSC:**
  No updates to the heat shield inspection timeline. Orion is being transported to Kennedy Space Center for detailed material scans. The 30-day inspection report remains on schedule for mid-May 2026. Heat shield data will inform Orion manufacturing modifications for Artemis III and the re-entry trajectory approach for future crewed lunar-return missions.
  - [NASA Shares Orion Heat Shield Findings, Updates Artemis Moon Missions — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-shares-orion-heat-shield-findings-updates-artemis-moon-missions/)

- **Lockheed Martin post-mission statement:**
  Lockheed Martin published a formal post-splashdown statement on April 10 noting that Orion met all mission success criteria and that manufacturing lessons from Artemis II will be incorporated into Artemis III and IV spacecraft.
  - [Splashdown! Lockheed Martin-Built Orion Spacecraft Safely Returns Astronauts to Earth — Lockheed Martin](https://news.lockheedmartin.com/2026-04-10-Splashdown-Lockheed-Martin-Built-Orion-Spacecraft-Safely-Returns-Astronauts-to-Earth,-Completing-NASAs-Artemis-II-Mission)

- **Artemis III architecture — LEO lander demonstration confirmed for 2027:**
  Multiple sources now confirm that Artemis III will be a crewed mission to low Earth orbit (LEO) conducted in mid-2027, designed to test integrated operations between Orion and one or both commercial Human Landing System (HLS) vehicles — SpaceX Starship HLS and Blue Origin Blue Moon. NASA has not yet determined whether one or both landers will be docked with Orion during Artemis III; that decision is pending lander readiness assessments. Artemis IV (~2028) remains the first crewed lunar landing target.
  - [What comes after Artemis II? — Houston Public Media (April 14, 2026)](https://www.houstonpublicmedia.org/articles/news/nasa/2026/04/14/548978/artemis-3-moon-space-nasa-houston/)

  - [NASA Strengthens Artemis: Adds Mission, Refines Overall Architecture — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/directorates/esdmd/nasa-strengthens-artemis-adds-mission-refines-overall-architecture/)

### Science & Instruments

- **AVATAR — Wyss Institute begins single-cell RNA sequencing:**
  The Harvard Wyss Institute / Emulate Inc. team has confirmed that bone marrow organ chips recovered from Orion are now undergoing single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) — a technique that measures gene expression changes within individual cells. This will be compared against ground-control chips that were kept under identical conditions except for spaceflight exposure, and against pre- and post-flight astronaut blood samples. Results will provide the first detailed cellular-level characterization of how deep-space radiation and microgravity together affect human hematopoietic (blood-forming) stem cells. Publication in a peer-reviewed journal is expected later in 2026.
  - [Wyss Institute Technologies Enable Breakthrough in Astronaut Health Research Aboard NASA's Artemis II Mission — GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/09/3271182/0/en/Wyss-Institute-Technologies-Enable-Breakthrough-in-Astronaut-Health-Research-Aboard-NASA-s-Artemis-II-Mission.html)
  - [Wyss Institute — Artemis II AVATAR](https://wyss.harvard.edu/news/wyss-institute-technologies-enable-breakthrough-in-astronaut-health-research-aboard-nasas-artemis-ii-mission/)
  - [NASA Artemis II Science | AVATAR — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/biological-physical/investigations/avatar/)

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO) — Earthset image analysis and cultural significance:**
  Post-mission coverage has highlighted the Earthset image captured at 6:41 p.m. EDT on April 6 as the Artemis II crew passed behind the lunar far side, showing a crescent Earth with Australia and Oceania visible. Live Science published crew first-person recollections (April 14) of the Earthset experience — specifically the 30-minute communications blackout during which Earth was completely invisible, described by crew members as disorienting and profound. The Planetary Society published the Vavilov Crater image as a standalone science spotlight.
  - ['Human minds shouldn't have to go through this': Artemis II crew recalls Earthset moment — Live Science](https://www.livescience.com/space/human-minds-shouldnt-have-to-go-through-this-artemis-ii-crew-recalls-unreal-moment-when-earth-disappeared-space-photo-of-the-week)
  - [Shadows Across Vavilov Crater — The Planetary Society](https://www.planetary.org/space-images/shadows-across-vavilov-crater)
  - [Earthset From the Lunar Far Side — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/earth-observatory/earthset-from-the-lunar-far-side/)
  - [NASA's Artemis II Crew Beams Official Moon Flyby Photos to Earth — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasas-artemis-ii-crew-beams-official-moon-flyby-photos-to-earth/)

- **AVATAR and Planetary Radio coverage:**
