# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-06 19:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is now 15 minutes into the lunar flyby observation window (opened 18:45 UTC), with the crew actively documenting 30 pre-selected geological targets on both the near and far sides of the Moon. At 17:57 UTC, Orion broke the 56-year-old Apollo 13 crew distance record, reaching beyond 248,655 miles from Earth; maximum distance of ~252,760 miles will occur at approximately 23:07 UTC. Closest lunar approach of ~4,070 miles is expected at 23:02 UTC following a planned ~40-minute communications blackout beginning at 22:44 UTC.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Active crewed mission — Flight Day 6 of 10; lunar flyby observation window underway (opened 18:45 UTC)
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC from Launch Complex 39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026 ~00:07 UTC (~8:07 p.m. EDT April 9), Pacific Ocean ~50 miles off San Diego; recovery by USS San Diego
- **Notable Changes Since Last Report (14:00 UTC):**
  - Apollo 13 crew distance record broken at **17:57 UTC** (~1:57 p.m. EDT) — Orion surpassed 248,655 miles from Earth
  - NASA live flyby coverage is now live (began 17:00 UTC) on NASA+, Amazon Prime, Apple TV, Hulu, Netflix, HBO Max, Roku, and NASA YouTube
  - Lunar observation window opened at **18:45 UTC** — crew actively conducting CLO science

### Technical & Engineering

- **Distance record broken:** At **17:57 UTC (1:57 p.m. EDT)**, Orion surpassed the Apollo 13 record of 248,655 miles — the farthest any humans had traveled from Earth since April 1970. Maximum distance of ~**252,760 miles** (~406,870 km) will be reached at approximately 23:07 UTC during closest approach.
  - [NPR: Watch live: Artemis II crew has now gone farther from Earth than any humans before](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/06/nx-s1-5773187/nasa-artemis-ii-moon-lunar-flyby)
  - [NBC Miami: It's time for Artemis II to break Apollo 13's distance record](https://www.nbcmiami.com/news/national-international/artemis-ii-break-apollo-13-distance-record-moon-flyby/3791209/)
- **Upcoming trajectory events:**
  - 22:44 UTC — Planned ~40-minute communications blackout begins as Orion passes behind the Moon
  - 23:02 UTC — Closest lunar approach: ~4,070 miles (~6,550 km) above the far-side surface
  - 23:07 UTC — Maximum distance from Earth: ~252,760 miles
  - 23:25 UTC — Comms restored; "Earthrise" moment; return leg begins
- **Spacecraft systems:** All systems nominal. No new anomalies reported on Flight Day 6.
- **Orion cabin:** Ground support noted that cabin lights inside Orion are dimmed and lunar maps are deployed to maximize crew efficiency during the observation window (CBS News, ground astronaut Randy Bresnik commentary).
- **Entry velocity:** Orion will re-enter Earth's atmosphere at approximately 25,000 mph on April 10 — the fastest crewed reentry ever attempted, validating the modified single-pass reentry angle implemented to mitigate the Artemis I heat shield anomaly.

### Science & Instruments

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO) — now underway:**
  - 30 geological targets finalized by the Artemis II Lunar Science Team and transmitted to the crew on April 5
  - Key targets include:
    - **Orientale Basin** — a nearly 600-mile-wide impact crater straddling the near and far sides, formed ~3.8 billion years ago; one of the Solar System's best-preserved multi-ring impact structures
    - Ancient mare basalts and tectonic ridges on the near side
    - Far-side regions never before seen directly by human eyes
  - Crew is capturing still photography and video using Orion's 32 cameras; working in pairs to provide real-time verbal descriptions to Mission Control science teams
  - [NASA Science: Artemis II Lunar Science Operations](https://science.nasa.gov/solar-system/nasas-artemis-ii-lunar-science-operations-to-inform-future-missions/)
  - [Artemis II Lunar Targeting Plan](https://science.nasa.gov/resource/artemis-ii-lunar-targeting-plan/)
- **Crater naming:** Space.com live updates report that the crew named a lunar crater during the flyby — described as a tribute to a loved one (Reid Wiseman). Hertzsprung basin on the far side is among the targets in scope.
  - [Space.com live: Artemis 2 updates April 6](https://www.space.com/news/live/artemis-2-nasa-moon-mission-updates-april-6-2026)
- **Upcoming solar eclipse observation:** At approximately 00:35 UTC (April 7), the Sun will pass behind the Moon from Orion's perspective; crew will image the **solar corona** for ~1 hour and attempt to observe meteoroid impact flashes on the lunar limb — a unique scientific opportunity not available to any prior human spaceflight
- **AVATAR investigation:** Organ-on-a-chip bone marrow devices continue operating; maximum deep-space radiation exposure is occurring now as the crew reaches peak distance from Earth's magnetosphere
- **O2O laser communications experiment:** Operating nominally in cislunar space
  - [NTRS: Optical Communications for Artemis II — O2O System (20250006514)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250006514)

### International Partners

- **ESA:** The Airbus-built European Service Module (ESM-2) continues performing nominally. All 33 engines and four solar array wings operating as expected throughout the flyby coast phase. ESA is framing the lunar flyby as a key validation milestone for European deep-space crewed hardware.
  - [ESA: Artemis II](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Orion/Artemis_II)
  - [ESA Orion Blog: Europe's engines powering Artemis II](https://blogs.esa.int/orion/2026/03/30/europes-engines-powering-artemis-ii/)
  - Note: ESA's Gateway hardware contributions (HALO, I-Hab) are in an uncertain state following the March 2026 Gateway cancellation
  - [Payload Space: With Artemis Changes, Europe Is Left Holding the Bag](https://payloadspace.com/with-artemis-changes-europe-is-left-holding-the-bag/)
- **CSA:** Jeremy Hansen is the first non-American to reach the lunar vicinity. CSA is livestreaming the flyby and publishing daily mission logbook entries.
  - [CSA: Scientific research during the mission](https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/missions/artemis-ii/scientific-research-during-mission.asp)
- **International CubeSats:** All four CubeSats (Germany/TACHELES, South Korea/K-Rad Cube, Saudi Arabia, Argentina/ATENEA) — deployed shortly after launch — continue operating in highly elliptical Earth orbit. All deploying nations are Artemis Accords signatories.
