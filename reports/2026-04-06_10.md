# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-06 10:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is on Flight Day 6, approximately 8.75 hours ahead of the lunar flyby observation window opening (18:45 UTC). Orion and the four-person crew are healthy and on a nominal free-return trajectory inside the Moon's gravitational sphere of influence. No new NASA blog updates have been published since the 09:00 UTC report; all major flyby milestones remain upcoming. Live multi-platform coverage begins at 17:00 UTC (1:00 p.m. EDT).

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Active crewed mission — Flight Day 6 of 10; inside lunar sphere of influence; pre-flyby approach (lunar flyby window opens 18:45 UTC)
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC from Launch Complex 39B, KSC
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026 ~00:07 UTC (5:07 p.m. PDT April 9) off San Diego coast; recovery by USS San Diego
- **Notable Changes (program-level):**
  - Lunar Gateway program canceled (March 2026)
  - Artemis III revised: no lunar landing; will perform LEO rendezvous/docking tests in 2027 with SpaceX Starship HLS and/or Blue Origin Blue Moon; AxEMU spacesuit testing included
  - Lunar landing deferred to Artemis IV (~early 2028)
  - SLS Exploration Upper Stage (EUS) canceled (February 2026); ULA Centaur V selected as alternative upper stage for Artemis IV+

### Technical & Engineering

- **Trajectory:** Orion is on a multi-trans-lunar injection (MTLI) free-return trajectory. The final outbound trajectory correction burn was completed at 11:03 p.m. EDT April 5 (03:03 UTC April 6). No further propulsive maneuvers are required until post-flyby return corrections.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/05/artemis-ii-flight-day-5-correction-burn-complete/
- **Communications blackout:** A planned ~40-minute Deep Space Network blackout is expected beginning at approximately 6:44 p.m. EDT (22:44 UTC) as Orion passes behind the Moon. Communications are expected to resume at approximately 7:25 p.m. EDT (23:25 UTC) at "Earthrise."
- **All spacecraft systems nominal.** No anomalies reported since the toilet issue resolved on Flight Day 2.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/02/artemis-ii-flight-update-crew-and-ground-teams-successfully-troubleshoot-orions-toilet/
- **Heat shield:** Modified reentry angle remains the planned mitigation for the Artemis I char loss anomaly; no additional hardware changes.

### Science & Instruments

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** The crew has been briefed on approximately 35 geological targets for the flyby window, including the Orientale basin (~600 miles wide, straddling the near and far sides), ancient lava flows, surface ridges, and brightness/color variations. Observations will be conducted in pairs with real-time verbal descriptions to Mission Control — the first systematic crewed lunar geological survey in over 50 years.
  - Source: https://science.nasa.gov/solar-system/nasas-artemis-ii-lunar-science-operations-to-inform-future-missions/
- **Solar corona observation:** At approximately 8:35 p.m. EDT (00:35 UTC April 7), the Sun will pass behind the Moon from Orion's perspective. The crew will image the solar corona for approximately one hour and monitor for meteoroid impact flashes on the lunar surface.
- **AVATAR investigation:** Organ-on-a-chip devices studying deep-space radiation and microgravity effects on human tissue are operating continuously.
- **ARCHeR investigation:** Real-time monitoring of crew performance, teamwork, sleep, and behavioral health via wrist monitors and saliva samples continues.
- **CubeSat secondary payloads:** Spacecraft from Germany (TACHELES), South Korea, Saudi Arabia, and Argentina deployed five hours after launch and remain operational.
  - Source: https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942

### International Partners

- **ESA (European Service Module):** The ESM continues performing nominally across all subsystems — propulsion (33 engines), power (4 solar arrays), thermal control, and life support. ESA engineers at ESTEC (Netherlands) and NASA JSC Mission Evaluation Room maintain 24/7 monitoring throughout the flyby.
  - Source: https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Artemis_II_mission_begins
  - Source: https://www.esa.int/Newsroom/Press_Releases/Europe_powers_Artemis_II_mission_to_the_Moon
- **CSA:** Jeremy Hansen is actively participating in all crew activities and lunar observations. CSA is publishing daily mission logbook entries. The Lunar Gateway cancellation has raised questions about a future dedicated Canadian astronaut mission slot originally tied to a Gateway mission.
  - Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/space-agency/news/2026/04/artemisii-mission-to-complete-historic-lunar-flyby.html
