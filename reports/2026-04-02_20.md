# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-02 20:00 UTC

## Summary

Orion *Integrity* and its four-person crew are in good health on Flight Day 2, completing the final pre-TLI period in high Earth orbit. The Trans-Lunar Injection (TLI) burn — the mission's most consequential maneuver — is scheduled for approximately 23:35 UTC tonight (~7:35 PM EDT), and flight controllers are conducting go/no-go polling. All spacecraft systems are nominal following the successful perigee raise burn at 11:06 UTC. No new anomalies have been reported since the 14:00 UTC update.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** In-flight — Flight Day 2, pre-TLI; awaiting go/no-go and burn execution (~23:35 UTC)
- **Launched:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35 UTC (6:35 PM EDT) from LC-39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Current Orbit:** High Earth checkout orbit; apogee ~43,760 miles (70,403 km) — highest crewed orbit since Apollo 17
- **TLI Burn:** Scheduled ~23:35 UTC April 2; ~6 min 5 sec ESM main engine firing; commits crew to free-return lunar trajectory
- **Splashdown Target:** ~April 10–11, 2026, Pacific Ocean off San Diego

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **TLI go/no-go underway:** Flight controllers at JSC Mission Control are conducting final systems reviews and go/no-go polling ahead of the TLI burn at ~23:35 UTC. All consumables, thermal, propulsion, and guidance systems are reported nominal. [NASA blog — Artemis II Mission](https://www.nasa.gov/mission/artemis-ii/)
- **Crew day 2 activities:** The crew completed a mid-day rest period, ate their first full meal aboard Orion, and conducted suit leak checks and emergency procedure reviews in preparation for the TLI burn. Commander Wiseman delivered a short video message from orbit relayed to media.
- **Deep Space Network handover:** NASA confirmed the Near Space Network to Deep Space Network (DSN) handover occurred successfully during the afternoon, marking the first crewed use of the DSN since the Apollo era. [NASA Artemis blog](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/01/live-artemis-ii-launch-day-updates/)
- **Toilet repair confirmed nominal:** The Orion waste management system has operated without issue since repairs completed earlier today. [NASA blog — Toilet Troubleshoot](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/02/artemis-ii-flight-update-crew-and-ground-teams-successfully-troubleshoot-orions-toilet/)

### Technical & Engineering

- **TLI burn profile:**
  - Engine: ESM orbital maneuvering system main engine (~6,000 lbs thrust)
  - Burn duration: ~6 minutes, 5 seconds
  - Delta-V: ~900 mph of added velocity to escape Earth orbit
  - Trajectory: Free-return figure-eight cislunar path; no Lunar Orbit Insertion required
  - Post-TLI: ~4-day coast to the Moon; DSN assumes primary comm; minor trajectory correction burns (TCBs) on Days 3–4

- **Orbital maneuvers to date (Flight Days 1–2):**
  - ICPS perigee raise (RL10 engine, Flight Day 1): raised apogee to initial checkout orbit
  - ICPS apogee raise burn (~18 min, RL10, Flight Day 1): raised apogee to ~43,760 miles
  - ESM perigee raise burn (43 sec, main engine, ~11:06 UTC April 2): completed orbital shaping for TLI setup
  - All burns performed within predicted tolerances

- **Heat shield reentry profile confirmed:** NASA has confirmed Flight Day 2 trajectory data remains within parameters for the steeper direct-entry profile replacing the Artemis I skip-entry. The change was driven by post-Artemis I thermal protection system analysis and was approved at the March 12 FRR. [Phys.org — Heat shield coverage](https://phys.org/news/2026-03-shield-safety-stakes-nasa-artemis.html)

- **SLS configuration update (program-level):** SLS Block 1B and Block 2 development were canceled February 26, 2026 by Administrator Isaacman. Artemis II through V will all use the Block 1 configuration. Mobile Launcher 2 development is canceled; a new second stage is under assessment for Artemis IV+. [NASASpaceFlight — Architecture report](https://www.nasaspaceflight.com/2026/03/artemis-course-correction-moonward-details/)

- **NTRS — Optical communications (O2O):** The Orion Artemis II Optical Communications payload (NTRS 20250010065, October 2025) enables up to 260 Mbps downlink, supporting near-real-time 4K video during and after the lunar flyby. [NTRS 20250010065](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065)

### Science & Instruments

- **Pre-TLI science status:** All seven science investigations are monitoring nominal. AVATAR (organ-on-a-chip devices) is collecting baseline data for comparison post-TLI when the crew passes through the outer Van Allen belts.
- **Radiation environment:** Radiation instruments inside and outside the Orion capsule are actively recording. Dose rates are expected to increase sharply during and after passage through the Van Allen belts during TLI. This data will feed directly into dosimetry models for Artemis III and IV crew planning.
- **CubeSat science:** The four 12U CubeSats (DLR TACHELES, KASA K-RadCube, Saudi Space Agency, CONAE ATENEA) deployed into highly elliptical Earth orbit on Flight Day 1 are transmitting science data to their respective ground stations. [NTRS 20250005942](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942)
- **Lunar far-side prep:** The crew reviewed NASA SVS flyby simulations and lunar observation checklists today in preparation for the April 6 closest approach (~6,513 km from far-side surface). [NASA SVS Artemis II Flight Path](https://svs.gsfc.nasa.gov/20412/)
- **ARCHER health monitoring:** Continuous physiological monitoring data and radiation dose measurements are being recorded and transmitted to the medical team at JSC.

- **Mission Specialist Christina Koch:** Reviewed post-TLI trajectory monitoring procedures. Conducting ongoing data collection for human research investigations.

## Academic & Research Papers

- **Title:** Optimized Trajectory Correction Burn Placement for the NASA Artemis II Mission
  - **Authors:** David Woffinden, Randy Eckman, Shane Robinson (NASA JSC)
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20230000223 (AAS 23-062)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20230000223)
  - **Relevance:** Directly applicable now; describes TCB placement methodology for the Artemis II free-return trajectory. Mission controllers will execute these burns on Days 3–4 post-TLI.

- **Title:** NASA's SLS Super Heavy-Lift Rocket in Process for Artemis II and Beyond
  - **Authors:** NASA MSFC SLS Team
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250011679 (December 22, 2025)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250011679)
  - **Relevance:** Describes hardware production status for Artemis II through V; RS-25 new production engine certification and EUS test article progress at MSFC.

- **Title:** Orion Artemis II Optical Communications Architecture, Implementation and Test Systems
  - **Authors:** NASA team
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250010065 (October 23, 2025)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065)
  - **Relevance:** Details the O2O payload providing up to 260 Mbps downlink — active mission capability supporting near-real-time 4K imagery.

- **Title:** Hitching a Ride on History: NASA's Space Launch System Artemis II CubeSats
  - **Authors:** NASA team
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250005942 (June 5, 2025)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942)
  - **Relevance:** Describes the four 12U international partner CubeSats now actively transmitting science data.

- **Title:** MoonAnything: A Vision Benchmark with Large-Scale Lunar Supervised Data
  - **Authors:** Clémentine Grethen, Yuang Shi, Simone Gasparini, et al.
  - **Source:** [arXiv:2604.00682v1](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00682v1) (submitted ~April 1, 2026)
  - **Relevance:** Learning-based visual perception benchmark for lunar surface navigation; supports autonomous systems development for future crewed Artemis surface missions.
