# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-02 08:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II remains in a nominal, quiet period approximately 9.5 hours into Flight Day 2, with the Trans-Lunar Injection (TLI) burn still ~15.5 hours away (scheduled ~23:35 UTC tonight). No new significant developments or anomalies have emerged since the 07:00 UTC report; all spacecraft systems continue to perform nominally. Flight controllers at JSC are conducting ongoing Day 2 system health reviews in preparation for the TLI go/no-go decision.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** In-flight — Flight Day 2; pre-TLI crew rest and ground systems review period
- **Launched:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35 UTC from LC-39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Flight Time:** ~9.5 hours as of report time
- **Current Orbit:** High Earth checkout orbit (~43,730 miles / ~70,400 km apogee) — highest human spaceflight since Apollo 17
- **TLI Burn Status:** On schedule for ~23:35 UTC tonight (~7:35 PM EDT); go/no-go poll pending; no adverse conditions reported
- **Splashdown Target:** April 11, 2026, Pacific Ocean off San Diego
- **Notable Changes:** None since 07:00 UTC report; waste management anomaly confirmed fully resolved (05:30 UTC resolution update); comm dropout resolved; no new anomalies

### Technical & Engineering

**Current Spacecraft Status (All Nominal):**
- Four European Service Module (ESM-2) solar array wings generating full power; power subsystem stable
- Environmental Control and Life Support System (ECLSS) delivering oxygen and drinking water from ESM tanks; first operational use with crew
- Deep Space Network (DSN) communications link stable following Near Space Network handover at apogee
- ESM main engine and auxiliary thrusters verified ready for TLI burn
- Orion Reaction Control System (RCS) thrusters healthy; confirmed post-proximity ops
- Guidance, Navigation & Control (GN&C) state vectors nominal; trajectory within expected bounds

**TLI Burn Profile (Scheduled — Not Yet Executed):**
- **Engine:** ESM orbital maneuvering system engine (~6,000 lbf thrust)
- **Duration:** ~6 minutes, 5 seconds
- **Delta-V:** ~900 mph added velocity
- **Trajectory type:** Multi-translunar injection (MTLI) — free-return figure-eight cislunar path (no lunar orbit insertion required)
- **Post-TLI:** ~4-day coast to the Moon; subsequent minor Trajectory Correction Burns (TCBs) on Days 3–4
- This is the **last major propulsive event** of the mission

**Post-TLI Trajectory Parameters:**
- Closest lunar approach (April 6): ~4,112 statute miles (~6,617 km) from the far-side surface
- Maximum distance from Earth: ~252,799 statute miles — expected to exceed Apollo 13 record (~248,655 miles)
- Reentry: ~25,000 mph; direct (steeper) descent angle to address Artemis I heat shield spalling findings

**Resolved Anomalies (No New Issues):**
- Communications dropout (~51 min post-launch, ~10 min duration): Confirmed resolved; cause identified as planned satellite handover transition
- Waste management system (toilet) controller fault: Confirmed resolved by ~05:30 UTC; system fully operational

**SLS Program Context:**
Per NTRS 20250011679 (December 2025), hardware for Artemis III, IV, and V is in production. Following the February 2026 cancellation of the Exploration Upper Stage, NASA awarded a sole-source contract to ULA in March 2026 for Centaur V upper stages for Artemis IV and V. [SpaceNews: NASA selects Centaur](https://spacenews.com/nasa-selects-centaur-for-new-sls-upper-stage/)

### Science & Instruments

No new instrument activations or data events this reporting window. All science systems remain in steady-state collection or checkout mode:

- **O2O Optical Communications System:** In checkout; planned 260 Mbps 4K video downlink demonstration scheduled for April 6 lunar flyby. [NTRS 20250010065](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065)
- **AVATAR (bone marrow organ-on-chip):** Active; cells currently transiting through the outer Van Allen belt, entering deep-space radiation environment.
- **ARCHeR Wristbands:** Active on all four crew members; continuously monitoring sleep quality, stress biomarkers, cognitive performance, and team coordination metrics.
- **Radiation Suite (HERA dosimeters + Crew Active Dosimeters):** All nominal; recording dose rates as spacecraft ascends toward cislunar space. Data informs real-time solar particle event response protocols.
- **CubeSats (all four):** TACHELES, K-RadCube, ATENEA, and SHMS remain operational in their elliptical deployment orbits; no anomalies. [NTRS 20250005942](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942)
- **Lunar flyby science prep:** Science Evaluation Room at JSC staffed; geology guidance team assembled for the April 6 flyby window (~21% of lunar far side sunlit at closest approach).

### Crew Updates

The crew is in a scheduled rest and preparation period ahead of tonight's TLI burn. No new public communications or crew video calls have taken place in this window.

- **Commander Reid Wiseman:** Leading mission operations; will give final crew "ready for TLI" concurrence to Mission Control before the burn window. Responsible for abort decision authority.
- **Pilot Victor Glover:** Resting; completed first-ever manual Orion piloting session during Day 1 proximity operations (~70 min, within ~30 ft of ICPS). Upon TLI, will become the first person of color to travel beyond low Earth orbit.
- **Mission Specialist Christina Koch:** Conducted ESM engine readiness checks earlier this morning. Upon TLI, will become the first woman to travel beyond low Earth orbit.
- **Mission Specialist Jeremy Hansen (CSA):** On his first spaceflight; first Canadian and first non-U.S. citizen to participate in a lunar mission. First scheduled public Q&A from space: April 4. [CSA event page](https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/events/2026/2026-04-04-artemis-ii-first-q-and-a-jeremy-hansen-live-from-space.asp)
- **First crew video call:** Expected post-TLI, likely early April 3 UTC.

## Academic & Research Papers

- **Title:** Optimized Trajectory Correction Burn Placement for the NASA Artemis II Mission
  - **Authors:** David Woffinden, Randy Eckman, Shane Robinson (NASA JSC)
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20230000223 (AAS 23-062)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20230000223)
  - **Relevance:** Methodology for the TCBs that will be executed on Days 3–4 post-TLI, accounting for GN&C performance margins, navigation uncertainty, crew schedule, and thruster selection.

- **Title:** Orion Artemis II Optical Communications Architecture, Implementation and Test Systems
  - **Authors:** NASA JSC / Glenn Research Center
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250010065](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065) — October 2025
  - **Relevance:** Detailed architecture of the O2O system (260 Mbps downlink, 20 Mbps uplink) currently in checkout; enables near-real-time 4K lunar video on April 6.

- **Title:** Hitching a Ride on History — Artemis II CubeSat Payloads
  - **Authors:** NASA MSFC / mission teams
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250005942](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942) — June 2025
  - **Relevance:** Documents the four deployed CubeSat payloads (TACHELES, K-RadCube, ATENEA, SHMS) and their science objectives; all confirmed active in orbit.

- **Title:** Deep Space Radiation Measurements and Crew Radiation Protection for the NASA Artemis Program
  - **Authors:** NASA JSC / Langley Research Center
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250002757](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250002757) — March 2025
  - **Relevance:** Crew radiation storm shelter protocols and dose benchmarks; informing real-time dosimetry interpretation as crew transits the outer magnetosphere.

- **Title:** NASA's SLS Super Heavy-Lift Rocket in Process for Artemis II and Beyond
  - **Authors:** NASA MSFC SLS Team
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250011679](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250011679) — December 22, 2025
  - **Relevance:** Hardware production status for Artemis II through V; confirms RS-25 engine production certification and provides context for the Centaur V transition.

- **Title:** Machine Learning-Based Radiation Forecasting for Deep Space Missions Using SDO Imagery
  - **Authors:** Gurav et al.
  - **Source:** [arXiv:2411.17703](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.17703) — November 2024
  - **Relevance:** Solar energetic particle event forecasting applicable during trans-lunar coast and the April 6 flyby.
