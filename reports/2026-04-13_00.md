# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-13 00:00 UTC

## Summary

The Artemis II mission concluded successfully on April 10, 2026, with Orion "Integrity" splashing down in the Pacific Ocean at 8:07 p.m. EDT (~00:07 UTC April 10), completing humanity's first crewed lunar flyby since Apollo 17 in 1972. All four crew members — Wiseman, Glover, Koch, and Hansen — returned to Houston on April 11 for a welcoming ceremony at Ellington Field. Post-mission activities are now underway, including crew reconditioning, Orion heat shield detailed inspection at KSC, and science data analysis; NASA Associate Administrator Kshatriya has confirmed the Artemis III crew will be announced "soon" and declared "the path to the surface is open now."

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — crew in post-flight reconditioning, medical evaluations, and lunar science debriefs at NASA Johnson Space Center
- **Mission Duration:** 10 days (April 1–10, 2026); launched April 1 at 22:35:12 UTC from LC-39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown:** April 10, 2026 at 8:07 p.m. EDT (00:07 UTC April 10), Pacific Ocean ~50–80 miles off San Diego; recovered by USS John P. Murtha (LPD 26)
- **Notable Changes:**
  - Mission complete with no anomalies
  - All crew records confirmed: farthest crewed spaceflight in 56 years (252,756 miles, surpassing Apollo 13 by 4,111 miles)
  - Orion capsule transported to KSC for detailed heat shield inspection
  - Artemis III mission architecture now confirmed as LEO HLS docking test (target mid-2027); Artemis IV designated as first crewed lunar landing (~2028)

### Technical & Engineering

- **Heat shield performance:** The redesigned AVCOAT heat shield with modified steeper reentry trajectory successfully protected the crew during lunar-return reentry. Immediate inspections were conducted by divers and onboard experts at the recovery ship; the capsule has since been transported to Kennedy Space Center for detailed CT scanning.
  - Background: The Artemis I heat shield exhibited unexpected char-layer ablation at 100+ locations; NASA redesigned the reentry trajectory (steeper descent angle) rather than replacing the heat shield.
  - [NASA Confident Artemis 2 Heat Shield Will Protect Crew During Re-Entry — Spaceflight Now](https://spaceflightnow.com/2026/04/10/nasa-confident-artemis-ii-heat-shield-will-protect-crew-during-re-entry/)
  - [NASA Shares Orion Heat Shield Findings, Updates Artemis Moon Missions — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-shares-orion-heat-shield-findings-updates-artemis-moon-missions/)
- **Manual piloting demonstrations:** Both planned demos completed nominally — Koch/Hansen on FD4 and Wiseman/Glover on FD8 (~01:59 UTC April 9). Results feed directly into Orion handling qualities data for future crewed missions.
- **Trajectory correction burns:** TCB-Return-1 (April 7, 15 sec, 1.6 ft/s ΔV) completed; TCB-Return-2 and -3 executed on schedule. All burns nominal.
- **Systems performance:** No anomalies reported across Orion's environmental control, power, communications, or navigation systems across the full 10-day mission.

### Science & Instruments

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** Crew documented impact craters, ancient lava flows, and surface fractures during the ~7-hour lunar flyby on April 6. Human color discrimination (browns, blues indicating mineral composition and age) provided data not achievable by robotic imagers. CLO imagery is being analyzed at JSC.
  - [NASA's Artemis II Crew Beams Official Moon Flyby Photos to Earth — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasas-artemis-ii-crew-beams-official-moon-flyby-photos to-earth/)
  - [Artemis II Lunar Flyby Gallery — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/gallery/lunar-flyby/)
- **Meteoroid impact flashes:** Six meteoroid impact flashes observed on the darkened lunar surface — data will contribute to impact hazard models for future surface operations.
- **Solar corona observations:** Crew captured images of the solar corona during lunar-eclipse geometry as the Moon blocked the Sun during flyby approach.
- **Earthset/Earthrise:** Multiple observations documented from lunar distance; first crewed Earthrise imagery since Apollo program.
- **AVATAR (biomedical):** Organ-on-a-chip devices flown to study effects of radiation and microgravity on crew health at lunar distances; hardware recovered with capsule and en route to JSC lab.
- **Radiation / SEP monitoring:** No significant solar energetic particle events during the mission; full dosimetry data from personal dosimeters and spacecraft radiation monitors being analyzed.
- **Blood and saliva samples:** Collected pre-, in-, and post-flight for immune system studies; samples with JSC biomedical teams.
