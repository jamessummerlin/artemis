# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-06 04:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is **currently in flight**, having launched successfully on April 1, 2026 at 22:35 UTC from Kennedy Space Center's Launch Complex 39B. The crew of four — Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Jeremy Hansen — completed the trans-lunar injection burn on April 2, becoming the first humans to leave Earth orbit since Apollo 17 in December 1972. Today (Flight Day 6), the spacecraft is conducting its historic **lunar flyby**, with closest approach of 4,066 miles from the lunar surface and splashdown planned for April 10 off San Diego.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Active crewed lunar flyby (Flight Day 6 of 10)
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 6:35 p.m. EDT (22:35:12 UTC)
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026, ~5:07 p.m. PDT, off San Diego coast
- **Notable Changes:** Mission had been delayed multiple times since the original September 2024 target due to Orion heat shield investigations and a liquid hydrogen leak during the wet dress rehearsal in February 2026. The Flight Readiness Review on March 12, 2026 gave a unanimous "go." The Lunar Gateway program was officially **canceled** in March 2026, representing a major shift in NASA's deep space architecture.

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **Launch:** SLS Block 1 / Orion ("Integrity") lifted off on April 1, 2026. All four Orion solar array wings deployed nominally. The crew conducted a 70-minute proximity operations demo with the ICPS upper stage, closing to within 9 meters.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/01/live-artemis-ii-launch-day-updates/
- **Leaving Earth orbit (Flight Day 2):** The European Service Module main engine fired a ~6-minute trans-lunar injection (TLI) burn on April 2, sending Orion on its free-return trajectory to the Moon. The crew became the first humans beyond low Earth orbit since 1972.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasas-artemis-ii-mission-leaves-earth-orbit-for-flight-around-moon/
- **Flight Day 3:** A trajectory correction burn was executed on April 3 and the crew received their Lunar Targeting Plan (LTP) assignments for observing the lunar surface.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/03/artemis-ii-flight-day-3-crew-prepares-for-first-correction-burn-readies-to-receive-lunar-observation-assignment/
- **Flight Day 4 (Manual Piloting):** Christina Koch and Jeremy Hansen alternated at Orion's controls for a 41-minute manual piloting demonstration, testing 6-DOF and 3-DOF thruster modes.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/04/artemis-ii-flight-day-4-crew-completes-manual-piloting-demonstration/
- **Flight Day 5:** Crew conducted suit demonstrations and completed an outbound trajectory correction burn at 11:03 p.m. EDT; lunar flyby preparations underway.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/05/artemis-ii-flight-day-5-crew-demos-suits-readies-for-lunar-flyby/
- **Flight Day 6 (today):** Orion entered the lunar sphere of influence at ~12:41 a.m. EDT. Lunar flyby window is 2:45–9:40 p.m. EDT today; closest approach is 4,066 miles from the lunar surface.

### Technical & Engineering

- **SLS Block 1:** 8.8 million pounds of liftoff thrust from 4 RS-25 engines and 2 solid rocket boosters (177 ft each). Boosters provided >75% of liftoff thrust for the first ~2 minutes.
- **Orion heat shield:** Post-Artemis I investigations revealed unexpected AVCOAT ablator char loss due to pressurized gas venting issues causing horizontal cracking and material shedding. NASA's resolution: modified the reentry descent angle to reduce time in the problematic thermal environment rather than replacing the heat shield. All objecting engineers were addressed prior to the March 12 FRR.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-shares-orion-heat-shield-findings-updates-artemis-moon-missions/
- **Distance record:** The crew reached 252,757 miles from Earth — approximately 4,102 miles farther than Apollo 13's record.
- **In-flight anomalies:** Minor waste management system issues (fan controller malfunction, frozen urine vent line) were resolved by the crew and Mission Control. A brief burning smell from the hygiene bay was investigated and cleared. No life-threatening issues have been reported.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/02/artemis-ii-flight-update-crew-and-ground-teams-successfully-troubleshoot-orions-toilet/
- **Trajectory:** Free-return profile from highly elliptical Earth orbit (apogee ~38,000 nmi), with ESM TLI burn on April 2; lunar gravity acts as a slingshot returning Orion to Earth without requiring a propulsive return burn.
  - Source: https://www.astronomy.com/space-exploration/how-artemis-2-will-fly-around-the-moon/

### Science & Instruments

- **Lunar observations:** Crew is executing the Crew Lunar Observations (CLO) science plan, making systematic observations of the lunar surface from cislunar space — the first crewed lunar observations in 50+ years.
  - Source: https://science.nasa.gov/artemis-ii-science/
- **O2O (Orion Artemis II Optical Communications System):** A 4-inch telescope with two gimbals demonstrating laser communications with ground stations in California and New Mexico; targets up to 260 Mbps downlink / 20 Mbps uplink.
  - NTRS: https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250006514
- **AVATAR (A Virtual Astronaut Tissue Analog Response):** Organ-on-a-chip devices flying aboard Orion to study the effects of deep-space radiation and microgravity on human tissue health.
- **CubeSats** (deployed from Orion's stage adapter):
  - **ATENEA** (Argentina / CONAE): Radiation shielding investigation, long-range communications, and radiation spectrum measurement.
    - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2025/05/20/nasa-signs-agreement-with-argentinas-space-agency-for-artemis-ii-cubesat/
  - **Space Weather CubeSat-1** (Saudi Space Agency): Measuring radiation, solar X-rays, solar energetic particles, and magnetic fields.
  - **TACHELES** (Germany / DLR): In-space technology demonstration including electrical components for future lunar logistics vehicles.
  - NTRS: https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942
- **32 cameras/imaging devices** aboard Orion for engineering, navigation, crew monitoring, lunar science, and public outreach.
- **Radiation studies:** Crew and embedded dosimeters are gathering first crewed deep-space radiation data to inform future long-duration missions.
  - NTRS: https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250002757

### Crew Updates

| Astronaut | Role | Agency | Historic First |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Reid Wiseman** | Commander | NASA | Oldest person to travel beyond LEO |
| **Victor Glover** | Pilot | NASA | First person of color beyond LEO |

All four astronauts are the first humans to leave Earth orbit since Apollo 17 (December 1972). The crew spoke with ABC News from space on April 3 and has conducted multiple live broadcasts. Crew quarantine began March 18, 2026.
- Source: https://abcnews.com/US/entire-globe-artemis-ii-astronauts-speak-abc-news/story?id=131674209

### International Partners

- **ESA (European Space Agency):** Provided the European Service Module (ESM), built by Airbus, which supplies Orion with propulsion, electrical power, thermal control, water, and oxygen. The ESM main engine performed the critical TLI burn.
  - Source: https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Orion/Artemis_II
- **CSA (Canadian Space Agency):** Jeremy Hansen, first Canadian to fly to the Moon, is conducting lunar surface observations and participated in the manual piloting demonstration. CSA is publishing a daily mission logbook.
  - Source: https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/missions/artemis-ii/daily-logbook.asp
- **Argentina (CONAE):** ATENEA CubeSat deployed as part of the Artemis Accords partnership.
- **Saudi Arabia:** Space Weather CubeSat-1 deployed.
- **Germany (DLR):** TACHELES CubeSat deployed.
- **Lunar Gateway (CANCELED):** The international Gateway lunar space station partnership was officially terminated in March 2026 by the current NASA administration, affecting previously planned contributions from ESA, CSA, JAXA, and others.
