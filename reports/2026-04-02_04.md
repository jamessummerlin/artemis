# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-02 04:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is 9.5 hours into flight on the morning of Flight Day 2, with all primary systems performing nominally and crew fully awake for pre-TLI activities. The critical Trans-Lunar Injection (TLI) burn — the last major propulsive event until reentry — remains on schedule for approximately 23:35 UTC tonight. Since the 03:00 UTC report, NASA disclosed resolution of the waste management system controller fault and confirmed the ESM main engine is fully configured for tonight's ~6-minute burn.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** In-flight — Flight Day 2; pre-TLI preparation and Flight Day 2 system reviews underway
- **Target Launch Date:** Launched April 1, 2026 at 22:35 UTC (6:35 PM EDT) — **COMPLETE**
- **Notable Changes:** Waste management system fault cleared; TLI go/no-go review underway; Gateway formally cancelled March 24, redirecting $5.3B toward a lunar surface base program ("Ignition Project")

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **TLI Burn on Track for ~23:35 UTC Tonight:** The ESM orbital maneuvering system main engine will fire for approximately 6 minutes 5 seconds, delivering ~900 mph delta-V to place Orion on a free-return figure-eight trajectory to the Moon. No issues expected to prevent a TLI Go call. [NASA Artemis blog](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/artemis/)
- **Daily Artemis II Mission Briefing:** NASA's first daily status briefing for Artemis II is scheduled from JSC Houston today; a second press conference is expected post-TLI at approximately 00:30 UTC April 3 (~8:30 PM EDT April 2). [Coverage schedule](https://www.nasa.gov/missions/artemis/artemis-2/nasa-sets-coverage-for-artemis-ii-moon-mission/)
- **Waste Management System Fault — Resolved:** A toilet controller fault triggered on Flight Day 1 has been cleared by mission controllers. Assessed as a software initialization issue on first activation; system is now functional. [NASA Artemis blog](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/artemis/)
- **Pre-launch Anomalies Confirmed Clear:** The two anomalies caught during the terminal countdown — a Flight Termination System issue and a Launch Abort System battery temperature exceedance — were both resolved before T-0 commitment and have had no in-flight recurrence. [NASA Artemis blog](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/artemis/)

### Technical & Engineering

- **ESM Main Engine Configured for TLI:** The European Service Module's AJ10-derived main engine (~6,000 lbf thrust) has been powered up and checkouts completed ahead of tonight's burn. The TLI is a multi-burn profile (MTLI) due to hydrogen boil-off during the extended LEO parking orbit used for system checkouts on Flight Day 1.
- **All ECLSS Systems Nominal:** Environmental Control and Life Support System (ECLSS) is performing within expected parameters: oxygen supply, CO₂ removal, and cabin pressure all nominal. Water and O₂ reserves (240 kg / 90 kg) are on the predicted consumption profile.
- **Navigation & Tracking:** DSN (Deep Space Network) ground stations are providing continuous contact. Trajectory is confirmed nominal following the Day 1 apogee raise and perigee raise burns; Orion is on the precise orbit to enable tonight's TLI targeting.
- **Communications Dropout — Full Analysis Complete:** The ~10-minute partial loss of contact at T+51 minutes (during a ground station handover from Bermuda to Madrid) has been fully characterized as a procedural handover anomaly, not a hardware failure. Corrective procedures have been implemented for subsequent handovers. Commander Wiseman confirmed crew could hear MCC throughout.
- **European Service Module (ESM-2):** All four solar array wings generating full power. Propulsion, thermal, and life-support functions nominal. ESA engineers at ESTEC (Netherlands) and ESOC (Germany) remain on console in support. [ESA Artemis II](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Orion/Artemis_II)
- **Heat Shield — Re-entry Configuration Locked:** The modified skip-less direct reentry trajectory (substituted for the original skip reentry to avoid cyclic thermal loading implicated in Artemis I heat shield spalling) is baked into the flight profile. No further changes. Splashdown target: Pacific Ocean off San Diego, ~April 11.

### Science & Instruments

- **Optical Communications (O2O) Checkout Continuing:** The Orion-to-Operations optical communications terminal — capable of 250 Mbps downlink versus ~1 Mbps S-band — is still undergoing checkout on Flight Day 2. First high-bandwidth test transmissions expected post-TLI. [NTRS 20250010065](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065)
- **Human Research Protocols Active:** Crew actigraphy (ARCHeR wristbands), sleep monitoring, and audio/video behavioral health feeds are all active and being logged by Human Research Program teams at JSC. AVATAR organ-on-a-chip samples (bone marrow cell arrays from pre-flight donations by each crew member) are stable at ambient cabin temperature within their protective housing.
- **CubeSat Secondary Payloads — Nominal in Elliptical Orbit:** All four CubeSats deployed from SLS on Flight Day 1 have checked in with ground stations:
  - **TACHELES** (DLR, Germany) — electronics radiation effects data collection begun
  - **K-RadCube** (KASA, South Korea) — tissue-equivalent dosimetry across Van Allen belts active
  - **Space Weather CubeSat-1** (Saudi Space Agency) — solar X-ray and energetic particle sensors collecting data
  - **ATENEA** (CONAE, Argentina) — radiation shielding and GPS tracking active
  [NTRS 20250005942](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942)
- **Lunar Observation Preparations:** Mission Specialists Koch and Hansen are reviewing photography protocols and camera configurations for the April 6 lunar flyby, including planned far-side imagery (low-angle illumination will enhance ridge and crater-rim relief) and solar corona photography during the lunar umbral shadow passage.

### Crew Updates

- **Commander Reid Wiseman** — Overseeing Day 2 TLI preparation activities; led crew through the day's opening system review. Post-liftoff quote (flight day 1): *"We have a beautiful moonrise — we're headed right at it."*
- **Pilot Victor Glover** — Completed manual proximity operations on Flight Day 1, flying Orion around the spent ICPS upper stage. Post-proximity ops: *"I see it. Look at that, woohoo! I see the ICPS and the moon in the field of view."* Becoming the first person of color to travel beyond LEO.
- **Mission Specialist Christina Koch** — Leading TLI burn preparation sequence; will be the first woman to travel to the lunar vicinity. Will also be the first woman to observe the Moon's far side at close range.
- **Mission Specialist Jeremy Hansen (CSA)** — Healthy on his first spaceflight; first Canadian and first non-U.S. citizen on a lunar mission. First live Q&A with Earth audience scheduled for **April 4** (CSA-hosted event). Crew's Orion spacecraft is named **"Integrity."** [CSA mission page](https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/missions/artemis-ii/)

### International Partners

- **ESA:** ESM-2 fully operational with crew aboard; all propulsion, power, and life-support functions nominal. The TLI burn will use the ESM's main engine — the largest propulsive event ESA hardware has supported since Orion returned from Artemis I. Third and fourth ESMs already delivered at KSC for Artemis III and IV. [ESA Artemis II](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Orion/Artemis_II); [Airbus ESM release](https://www.airbus.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-03-heart-of-the-mission-airbus-built-esm-to-power-historic-artemis-ii-crew-to-the-moon)
- **CSA:** CSA astronaut Jenni Gibbons (Hansen's backup) is serving as CAPCOM at Mission Control Houston. Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney issued a statement praising Hansen and spoke with President Trump about the mission; CSA astronaut David Saint-Jacques hosted a public watch party in Longueuil, QC. [CSA news](https://www.canada.ca/en/space-agency/news/2026/03/april-1st-2026--longueuil-quebec.html); [CBC coverage](https://www.cbc.ca/news/science/livestory/artemis-ii-launch-nasa-live-coverage-canada-9.7149222)
- **Gateway — CANCELLED (Program Restructure):** NASA formally halted the Lunar Gateway "in its current form" on March 24, 2026, redirecting $5.3B into a new **"Ignition Project"** targeting a permanent lunar surface base by 2029–2036. International partners (ESA, JAXA, CSA, UAE) were caught off-guard; hardware repurposing discussions are ongoing. [SpaceNews](https://spacenews.com/nasa-halts-work-on-gateway-to-develop-a-lunar-base/); [Space.com](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/artemis/nasas-lunar-gateway-space-station-is-out-moon-bases-are-in)

## Academic & Research Papers

- **Title:** NASA's SLS Super Heavy-Lift Rocket in Process for Artemis II and Beyond
  - **Authors:** NASA MSFC team
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20250011679](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250011679) — December 2025
  - **Relevance:** Covers hardware production status for Artemis II–V; confirms RS-25 engine certification completed 2024 with L3Harris Technologies; validates the Block 1 configuration performance profile flown on April 1.

- **Title:** Orion Artemis II Optical Communications Architecture, Implementation and Test Systems
  - **Authors:** NASA JSC / Glenn Research Center
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20250010065](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065) — October 2025
  - **Relevance:** Detailed description of the O2O payload flying on this mission — forward link 20 Mbps, return link 250 Mbps, controlled from MCC Houston. Currently being checked out on Flight Day 2.

- **Title:** Hitching a Ride on History: NASA's SLS Artemis II CubeSats
  - **Authors:** NASA MSFC / Secondary Payload Coordination
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20250005942](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942) — June 2025
  - **Relevance:** Overview of the four secondary CubeSat payloads (TACHELES, ATENEA, K-RadCube, SWC-1) deployed from SLS on April 1; all four confirmed active on Flight Day 2.

- **Title:** Deep Space Radiation Measurements and Crew Radiation Protection for the NASA Artemis Program
  - **Authors:** NASA JSC / Langley Research Center
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20250002757](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250002757) — March 2025
  - **Relevance:** Informs Artemis II crew storm shelter protocols; radiation measurement baseline from Artemis I and BioSentinel directly applicable to crew dosimetry interpretation on this mission.

- **Title:** Optimized Trajectory Correction Burn Placement for the NASA Artemis II Mission
  - **Authors:** David Woffinden, Randy Eckman, Shane Robinson (NASA JSC)
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20230000223](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20230000223)
  - **Relevance:** Describes the methodology for trajectory correction burns that will be executed post-TLI during the 4-day outbound coast; directly applicable to Flight Days 2–6.

- **Title:** Machine Learning-Based Radiation Forecasting for Deep Space Missions Using SDO Imagery
  - **Authors:** Gurav et al.
  - **Source:** [arXiv:2411.17703](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.17703) — November 2024
  - **Relevance:** Applies ML to solar energetic particle forecasting using Solar Dynamics Observatory data and BioSentinel (Artemis I) measurements; directly relevant to real-time crew radiation risk assessment during Artemis II's trans-lunar coast and lunar flyby.

- **Title:** Emerging Trends in Cislunar Space for Lunar Science Exploration and Space Robotics Aiding Human Spaceflight Safety
  - **Authors:** Arsalan Muhammad et al.
  - **Source:** [arXiv:2603.02878](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02878) — March 3, 2026
  - **Relevance:** Frames the Moon as a testbed for advanced AI and robotics in the Artemis context; informs pre-deployment infrastructure and ISRU strategies relevant to the newly prioritized lunar surface base replacing Gateway.
