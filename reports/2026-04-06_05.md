# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-06 05:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is on Flight Day 6, with Orion having entered the lunar sphere of influence at 12:41 a.m. EDT (04:41 UTC) today. The crew of four has already captured first glimpses of the Moon's far side — including the Orientale basin, never before seen directly by human eyes — as they approach the historic lunar flyby window (2:45–9:40 p.m. EDT). Separately, a significant program change was confirmed: Artemis III, currently targeted for 2027, will **no longer land on the Moon** and will instead conduct rendezvous and docking tests in low Earth orbit.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Active crewed lunar flyby — Flight Day 6 of 10 (in lunar sphere of influence)
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 6:35 p.m. EDT (22:35:12 UTC)
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026, ~5:07 p.m. PDT, off San Diego coast
- **Notable Changes:** Artemis III (targeted mid-2027) revised: will no longer attempt a lunar landing, and will instead test LEO rendezvous/docking with SpaceX Starship HLS and/or Blue Origin Blue Moon, along with the Axiom AxEMU spacesuit. Lunar Gateway program remains canceled (since March 2026).

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **Lunar sphere of influence entry:** Orion entered the lunar sphere of gravitational influence at approximately 12:41 a.m. EDT (04:41 UTC) on April 6, marking the formal transition from Earth-dominated to Moon-dominated gravity.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/05/artemis-ii-flight-day-5-crew-demos-suits-readies-for-lunar-flyby/
- **Flyby window:** The lunar flyby observation window opens at 2:45 p.m. EDT (18:45 UTC) and closes at 9:40 p.m. EDT (01:40 UTC April 7). Closest approach is 4,066–4,070 miles above the lunar surface at approximately 7:02 p.m. EDT (23:02 UTC).
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/missions/artemis/artemis-2/nasa-sets-coverage-for-artemis-ii-moon-mission/
- **Distance record:** The crew is expected to break the Apollo 13 distance record of 248,655 miles at 1:56 p.m. EDT (17:56 UTC), ultimately reaching a maximum of **252,757 miles** from Earth at approximately 7:07 p.m. EDT — 4,102 miles beyond the previous record.
  - Source: https://www.space.com/news/live/artemis-2-nasa-moon-mission-updates-april-5-2026
- **First far-side views:** The crew has begun capturing images of the lunar far side. NASA astronaut Christina Koch described seeing the Moon and noting: *"The darker parts just aren't quite in the right place"* — reflecting the unfamiliar view of the far side from close range.
  - Source: https://www.nbcnews.com/science/space/artemis-ii-astronauts-interview-space-moon-far-side-nasa-rcna266564
- **Coverage:** NASA TV and the NASA app are carrying live coverage of the flyby events.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/missions/artemis/artemis-2/nasa-sets-coverage-for-artemis-ii-moon-mission/

### Technical & Engineering

- **Trajectory:** Orion is on a "pinpoint trajectory" toward the Moon. The free-return profile requires no propulsive burn for the return journey; lunar gravity will slingshot the spacecraft back toward Earth.
  - Source: https://abcnews.com/Technology/live-updates/artemis-ii-live-updates-window-launch-opens-today/?id=131444306
- **Communications blackout:** As Orion passes behind the Moon, a planned ~40-minute communications blackout will occur, during which the lunar surface blocks Deep Space Network contact. No action is required by the crew.
- **Flight Day 5 burn complete:** A final outbound trajectory correction burn was completed at 11:03 p.m. EDT on April 5, confirming Orion is precisely aligned for flyby closest approach.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/05/artemis-ii-flight-day-5-correction-burn-complete/
- **Orion heat shield:** Modified reentry angle (increased descent steepness) remains the planned mitigation for the Artemis I char loss anomaly; no further hardware changes are required.

### Science & Instruments

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** The crew has received 30 specific lunar observation targets for the flyby window. Targets include impact craters, ancient lava flows, surface ridges, and differences in color, brightness, and texture to characterize surface composition — the first such systematic crewed observations in over 50 years.
  - Source: https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/what-will-nasas-artemis-ii-astronauts-see-on-the-moon/
- **Orientale basin:** The nearly 600-mile-wide impact crater straddling the near and far sides of the Moon will be visible in its entirety for the first time with human eyes during the flyby.
  - Source: https://www.lbc.co.uk/article/artemis-ii-astronauts-moon-far-side-never-seen-with-human-eyes-5HjdXLP_2/
- **Solar eclipse / corona observation:** At approximately 8:35 p.m. EDT (00:35 UTC April 7), the Sun will move behind the Moon from Orion's perspective, producing a total solar eclipse visible from the spacecraft for approximately one hour. The crew will image the **solar corona** — the Sun's outermost atmosphere — and monitor for meteoroid impact flashes on the lunar surface.
  - Source: https://www.wzzm13.com/article/news/nation-world/artemis-ii-moon-mission-crew-distance-record-apollo-orion-capsule-loop-total-solar-eclipse/507-b3457f3c-b9c8-44ca-9346-2a6305f9e08b
- **Far-side photography:** Crew members are splitting into pairs and alternating camera positions at the windows to maximize coverage during the observation window.
  - Source: https://www.nbcnews.com/science/space/artemis-ii-astronauts-interview-space-moon-far-side-nasa-rcna266564
- **O2O laser comm, AVATAR, and CubeSats** (ATENEA, Space Weather CubeSat-1, TACHELES) continue nominal operations (see earlier daily reports for details).

### International Partners

- **ESA (European Service Module):** ESM continues performing nominally; propulsion, power, thermal and life support systems operating as expected. ESM main engine not required during the lunar flyby itself.
  - Source: https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Orion/Artemis_II
- **CSA:** Jeremy Hansen is actively participating in lunar observations and has been speaking publicly about the mission. CSA is maintaining a daily mission logbook.
  - Source: https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/missions/artemis-ii/daily-logbook.asp
- **Artemis III revised architecture:** NASA's decision to restructure Artemis III eliminates the planned lunar landing in 2027. The mission will now focus on LEO rendezvous/docking with SpaceX Starship HLS and/or Blue Origin Blue Moon, plus AxEMU suit tests. This change primarily affects ESA, CSA, JAXA, and other partners who had planned contributions tied to lunar surface operations.
  - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artemis_III
