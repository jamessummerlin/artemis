# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-14 18:00 UTC

## Summary

The Artemis II crew is now in Day 5 of postflight reconditioning at NASA's Johnson Space Center, with new coverage providing detailed insight into the physiological recovery protocols underway. NASA's Artemis program architecture has been officially expanded with the addition of Artemis V (targeted late 2028) for a second crewed lunar landing and early Moon base construction activities. The IAU's review of the proposed "Carroll" and "Integrity" crater names submitted by the Artemis II crew is on track for a decision within approximately one month (~May 2026).

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — crew reconditioning (Day 5), Orion engineering inspections at KSC, science sample analysis
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026, 22:35:12 UTC from LC-39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown:** April 10, 2026, ~00:07 UTC (April 10, 5:07 p.m. PDT), Pacific Ocean SW of San Diego
- **Mission Duration:** ~9 days, 1 hour, 31 minutes
- **Notable Changes:**
  - Crew in Day 5 of standard postflight reconditioning at JSC; all four astronauts healthy
  - Orion heat shield formal 30-day inspection period continues; report due ~May 10, 2026
  - NASA formally added Artemis V to program architecture: crewed lunar landing + Moon base initiation, targeted late 2028
  - April 16 crew postflight news conference at JSC remains on schedule (18:30 UTC / 2:30 p.m. EDT)
  - TACHELES CubeSat (Germany/NEUROSPACE): communications not yet established; engineers continue troubleshooting

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **April 16 crew postflight news conference (upcoming — 18:30 UTC):** All four crew members (Wiseman, Glover, Koch, Hansen) will answer press questions at JSC. This is the first formal public opportunity for the full crew to discuss mission details since splashdown. Livestream on NASA TV and YouTube.
  - [NASA to Host Artemis II Crew Postflight News Conference — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-to-host-artemis-ii-crew-postflight-news-conference/)

- **NASA adds Artemis V to program architecture:** Following the Artemis II success, NASA officially announced the addition of a fifth Artemis mission (Artemis V), targeting late 2028 — to include the second crewed lunar landing and initiation of early Moon base construction activities. Artemis III (mid-2027) remains a crewed LEO test; Artemis IV (~early 2028) remains the planned first crewed lunar landing.
  - [NASA Adds Mission to Artemis Lunar Program, Updates Architecture — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-adds-mission-to-artemis-lunar-program-updates-architecture/)

- **Orion heat shield inspection underway at KSC:** Initial inspections after splashdown showed no unexpected conditions. The formal 30-day engineering inspection period is ongoing; a full report is due ~May 10, 2026. Heat shield data will be critical in determining Artemis IV schedule and any required redesign of the Avcoat permeability characteristics.
  - [NASA Shares Orion Heat Shield Findings, Updates Artemis Moon Missions — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-shares-orion-heat-shield-findings-updates-artemis-moon-missions/)

- **White House congratulations and visit pending:** President Trump called the crew on April 1 from the Oval Office and again after splashdown, inviting the crew to the White House. No date has been confirmed; President said the visit would happen "soon."
  - [ICYMI: President Trump Congratulates Artemis II Crew on Making History — The White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/releases/2026/04/icymi-president-trump-congratulates-artemis-ii-crew-on-making-history/)

### Technical & Engineering

- **Orion heat shield — inspection status:** Two heat shield experts embedded in the USS John P. Murtha recovery ship conducted initial visual inspections before the capsule was shipped from San Diego to KSC by road. Detailed thermal scans and structured material analysis are now underway in Florida. The data will directly inform whether an Avcoat permeability redesign is required for Artemis IV or only trajectory changes suffice. Report due ~May 10.
  - [Heat shield data from Artemis II could define Artemis IV timeline — Scripps News](https://www.scrippsnews.com/science-and-tech/space/heat-shield-data-from-artemis-ii-could-define-artemis-iv-timeline)

- **ESM helium pressurant valve — anomaly investigation ongoing:** ESA and Airbus Defence and Space root-cause investigation continues. Design modifications will be required for Artemis IV's more demanding propulsion profile.

- **TACHELES CubeSat (NEUROSPACE, Germany) — no contact:** Successfully deployed in high Earth orbit; ground stations have not established radio contact. Engineers continue troubleshooting. Satellite is rated for a 2-year orbital life.
  - [Tacheles (satellite) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tacheles_(satellite))

- **Navy recovery operations — USS John P. Murtha:** A detailed new USNI News article (April 12) and Navy fleet press release document the dive medical team and capsule recovery sequence for Artemis II, including underwater crew extraction, initial medical assessments aboard the well deck, and transfer by helicopter to the recovery ship's medical bay. Navy Divers from Explosive Ordnance Disposal Mobile Unit 11 led the extraction.
  - ['Integrity, Arriving': Navy Crew Recovers Astronauts After Artemis Mission — USNI News](https://news.usni.org/2026/04/12/integrity-arriving)
  - [First Contact: Meet the Dive Medical Recovery Team of Artemis II — U.S. Fleet Forces Command](https://www.usff.navy.mil/Press-Room/News-Stories/Article/4456440/first-contact-meet-the-dive-medical-recovery-team-of-artemis-ii/)

### Science & Instruments

- **Carroll Crater (proposed) — IAU timeline confirmed:** The crew proposed the name "Carroll" (after Commander Wiseman's late wife, Carroll Taylor Wiseman) for a bright far-side crater spotted during the April 6 flyby, and "Integrity" (after the Orion capsule's callsign) for another feature. Both names have been formally submitted to the International Astronomical Union following crew return. IAU's Ramasamy Venugopal stated a ruling is expected in "about a month" — consistent with the ~May 2026 estimate in previous reports.
  - [Artemis II Crew Name New Moon Crater After Astronaut's Late Wife — My Modern Met](https://mymodernmet.com/moon-crater-carroll-artemis-ii/)
  - [Carroll (crater) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carroll_(crater))
  - [Where is Carroll Crater? — Astronomy Magazine](https://www.astronomy.com/space-exploration/artemis-2-crew-shares-emotional-moment-as-they-name-crater-after-commanders-late-wife/)

- **Vavilov Crater geology — farside data processing:** Crew observation data and images of Vavilov Crater (located on the rim of Hertzsprung Basin) from the April 6 flyby are being analyzed by science teams. The close-up imagery shows the transition from smooth interior material to rugged ejecta — the first human-led visual geological survey of this region in over 50 years. Data confirms the farside crust is significantly thicker and has fewer volcanic maria than the nearside.
  - [Artemis II Science Data 2026: First Results from Vavilov Crater & Radiation Tests — College Simplified](https://www.collegesimplified.in/post/artemis-ii-science-data-downlink-flight-day-7-2026)

- **AVATAR organ-on-a-chip — RNA sequencing underway:** Bone marrow organ chips have been transported to the Harvard Wyss Institute / Emulate laboratories. Single-cell RNA sequencing is in progress, comparing deep-space exposed chips to simultaneous Earth-based control chips. Results expected in coming months; a peer-reviewed publication is in preparation.
  - [NASA Artemis II Science — AVATAR — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/biological-physical/investigations/avatar/)

- **DLR M-42 EXT radiation data — processing ongoing:** The four extended radiation detectors returned the highest-resolution deep-space radiation dataset ever collected on a crewed cislunar trajectory. Data downlink is complete; analysis by DLR and collaborating institutions is underway.
  - [Artemis II launches to the Moon with German and European tech on board — DLR](https://www.dlr.de/en/latest/news/2026/artemis-ii-launches-to-the-moon-with-german-and-european-tech-on-board)

### Crew Updates

- **Day 5 reconditioning — detailed protocols:** A new Orbital Today article (published April 14) provides detailed breakdown of the physiological recovery process underway at JSC:
  - **Musculoskeletal:** Muscle mass can drop up to 20% after two weeks in microgravity; back, neck, and calves are most affected. However, the ~10-day Artemis II mission is far shorter than a standard ISS rotation, so impacts are expected to be significantly milder.
  - **Cardiovascular:** Fluid shifts from the lower body that occurred in microgravity are reversing; heart rate and blood pressure regulation monitoring ongoing.
  - **Vestibular/neurological:** Balance and spatial orientation systems require re-adaptation; astronauts may initially need support walking. Typically resolves within days for short-duration missions.
  - Structured rehabilitation begins immediately upon return; progress is closely monitored by the human performance and medical teams.
  - [From Deepest Space to Doctor's Orders: What Happens to the Artemis II Crew Now — Orbital Today](https://orbitaltoday.com/2026/04/14/from-deepest-space-to-doctors-orders-what-happens-to-the-artemis-ii-crew-now/)
  - [Brutal recovery Artemis 2 astronauts face following return to Earth — UniLad Tech](https://www.uniladtech.com/science/space/nasa/brutal-recovery-artemis-2-astronauts-return-to-earth-654184-20260413)

- **Historic firsts:** Victor Glover (first person of color), Christina Koch (first woman), Jeremy Hansen (first non-U.S. citizen), and Reid Wiseman (oldest person) to travel beyond low Earth orbit to the vicinity of the Moon.

- **April 16 news conference (upcoming):** Full crew to hold postflight Q&A at 18:30 UTC / 2:30 p.m. EDT at JSC.

### International Partners

- **ESA — European Service Module:** ESM performed all primary functions during Artemis II. Helium pressurant valve anomaly root-cause investigation ongoing; expected to require hardware redesign for Artemis IV. ESA confirmed ongoing discussions with Airbus Defence and Space.
  - [ESA — Splashdown for Artemis II](https://www.esa.int/Newsroom/Press_Releases/Splashdown_for_Artemis_II)

- **CSA — Jeremy Hansen / Gateway:** Jeremy Hansen completed the mission as Mission Specialist, the first non-U.S. citizen to fly to the vicinity of the Moon. The CSA's Canadarm3 program, originally planned for the now-cancelled Lunar Gateway, is in renegotiation following NASA's pivot to a $20B lunar surface base program.
  - [The Artemis campaign — Canadian Space Agency](https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/astronomy/moon-exploration/artemis-missions.asp)

- **Lunar Gateway cancelled — architecture renegotiation:** NASA officially cancelled Gateway on March 24, 2026. PPE and HALO modules are being repurposed toward the surface base concept. International partner negotiations (ESA, JAXA, CSA) are ongoing.
  - [NASA halts work on Gateway to develop a lunar base — SpaceNews](https://spacenews.com/nasa-halts-work-on-gateway-to-develop-a-lunar-base/)
