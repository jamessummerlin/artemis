# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-06 01:00 UTC

## Summary

Orion confirmed entered the Moon's sphere of gravitational influence at 12:41 AM EDT (4:41 AM UTC) — the first humans in the Moon's gravity well since Apollo 17 in December 1972. A brief 14-second trajectory correction burn is planned ahead of the flyby observation window (2:45–9:40 PM EDT), which remains on schedule with no anomalies. New details emerged on NASA's first Artemis II Science Officers team, who are embedded in Mission Control supporting real-time lunar science from two dedicated back rooms.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** In-flight — Flight Day 6, pre-flyby transit
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026, 22:35:12 UTC, Launch Complex 39B, KSC
- **Lunar Sphere of Influence Entry:** 12:41 AM EDT April 6 (4:41 AM UTC) — **confirmed**
- **Pre-Flyby Trajectory Correction Burn:** 14-second burn planned; spacecraft is on pinpoint trajectory (first two scheduled correction burns were cancelled as unnecessary)
- **Flyby Observation Window:** 2:45–9:40 PM EDT April 6 (18:45 UTC – 01:40 UTC April 7)
- **Closest Approach:** ~7:02 PM EDT, ~4,066 miles above lunar surface
- **Maximum Distance from Earth:** ~7:07 PM EDT, 252,757 miles (406,773 km) — new crewed record
- **Communications Blackout:** ~5:47 PM EDT, ~40 minutes
- **Earthrise / Signal Re-acquisition:** ~7:25 PM EDT
- **Solar Eclipse Observation:** ~8:35 PM EDT, ~1 hour duration
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026, ~8:07 PM EDT, Pacific Ocean off San Diego, CA
- **Notable Changes:** No mission profile changes. Gateway cancellation (March 2026) and Artemis III restructuring (LEO demonstration) remain in effect.

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **Sphere of Influence Confirmed:** Orion officially crossed into the Moon's gravitational sphere of influence at 12:41 AM EDT, confirming the planned milestone. This makes the Artemis II crew the first humans in the Moon's gravity well since Apollo 17 on December 14, 1972.
  - Source: https://www.msn.com/en-us/news/insight/artemis-ii-crew-nears-moon-s-gravitational-pull-milestone/gm-GMFA5D6995

- **Pinpoint Trajectory:** Orion's trajectory is described by NASA as "pinpoint" — so precise that the first two outbound trajectory correction burns were cancelled. Only a brief 14-second burn is planned before the flyby window opens.
  - Source: https://abcnews.com/Technology/live-updates/artemis-ii-live-updates-window-launch-opens-today/?id=131444306

- **No Daily Status Briefing Today:** As previously announced, NASA is not holding its daily mission status briefing on April 6 due to lunar flyby activities. Briefings resume April 7.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/missions/artemis/artemis-2/nasa-sets-coverage-for-artemis-ii-moon-mission/

- **NASA+ Live Coverage:** Coverage of the flyby airs beginning 1:00 PM EDT on NASA+, Amazon Prime, Apple TV, Hulu, Netflix, HBO Max, and Roku.
  - Source: https://plus.nasa.gov/scheduled-video/nasas-artemis-ii-crew-flies-around-the-moon-official-broadcast/

### Technical & Engineering

**Orion ("Integrity") — Pre-Flyby Configuration**
- All four solar array wings deployed and nominal; ESM-2 providing full propulsion, power, and life support.
- Orion's trajectory is so precise that only one short 14-second trim burn is required before the flyby window, versus the three correction burns originally planned.
- Crew configured handheld cameras (80–400 mm and 14–24 mm lens complement) for the lunar observation session.
- Return trajectory to Earth is on track for April 10 splashdown.

**Deep Space Network (DSN)**
- DSN continues nominal two-way communications. A planned ~40-minute blackout begins at ~5:47 PM EDT when Orion passes behind the Moon. Signal re-acquisition expected at ~7:25 PM EDT.

**O2O Optical Communications**
- The Orion Artemis II Optical Communications laser payload continues nominal operation at up to 260 Mbps downlink.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/technology/space-comms/o2o/

### Science & Instruments

**Science Officers in Mission Control**
- NASA has certified three personnel as the agency's first-ever Artemis II Science Officers, a new dedicated flight controller console embedded in Mission Control:
  - **Kelsey Young** — NASA Goddard Space Flight Center (GSFC)
  - **Trevor Graff** — NASA Johnson Space Center (JSC)
  - **Angela Garcia** — NASA Johnson Space Center (JSC)
- The science officers completed months of flight controller training, certification simulations, geology training, and integrated simulations with the astronauts.
- During the flyby, crew imagery and recorded audio are downlinked in real-time to two science back rooms at JSC: the **Science Evaluation Room** and the **Science Mission Operations Room**. Experts in those rooms provide real-time analysis and strategic guidance to the science officer at the Mission Control console.
- This mission is explicitly a test of lunar science operations workflows that will be refined for Artemis III and beyond.
  - Source: https://science.nasa.gov/missions/artemis/meet-nasas-new-artemis-ii-science-officers/

**Lunar Targeting Plan (LTP) — 30 Targets**
- The 30-target observation list transmitted April 5 remains active. Key targets: Orientale Basin (~600 miles wide, near/far-side boundary), Hertzsprung Crater, and multiple far-side features invisible from Earth.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/gallery/artemis-ii-mission-science/

**AVATAR / ARCHeR / CubeSats**
- AVATAR organ-on-a-chip biology experiment, ARCHeR biometric wristbands, and all four CubeSats (K-RadCube, Space Weather CubeSat-1, TACHELES, ATENEA) remain nominal.

### Crew Updates

- All four crew members are healthy and preparations for the flyby are on schedule.
- **Commander Reid Wiseman (NASA)** — leading flyby observation session.
- **Pilot Victor Glover (NASA)** — first person of color beyond LEO.
- **Mission Specialist 1 Christina Koch (NASA)** — first woman beyond LEO.
- **Mission Specialist 2 Jeremy Hansen (CSA)** — first Canadian and first non-American beyond LEO.
- No new crew interviews or statements since the 00:00 UTC report. Previous quotes from Hansen ("Right away, you are humbled") and Koch ("The darker parts just aren't quite in the right place") remain the latest crew commentary.
  - Source: https://www.nbcnews.com/science/space/artemis-ii-astronauts-interview-space-moon-far-side-nasa-rcna266564

## Upcoming Milestones

- **April 6, 2026, ~1:00 PM EDT:** NASA+ live flyby broadcast begins.
- **April 6, 2026, ~2:45 PM EDT:** Flyby observation window opens; Orion's windows oriented toward the Moon.
- **April 6, 2026, ~5:47 PM EDT:** Communications blackout begins (~40 min).
- **April 6, 2026, ~7:02 PM EDT:** Closest lunar approach — ~4,066 miles above surface.
- **April 6, 2026, ~7:07 PM EDT:** Maximum distance from Earth — 252,757 miles — new crewed distance record.
- **April 6, 2026, ~7:25 PM EDT:** Earthrise; DSN re-acquires communications.
- **April 6, 2026, ~8:35 PM EDT:** Solar eclipse begins; ~1 hour. Solar corona and meteoroid flash observations.
- **April 6, 2026, ~9:40 PM EDT:** Flyby observation window closes; crew begins imagery transfer.
- **April 7, 2026:** Science team reviews flyby imagery; crew debrief. Mission status briefing resumes.
- **April 7–9, 2026:** Return transit to Earth; final trajectory correction burns.
- **April 10, 2026, ~8:07 PM EDT:** Splashdown, Pacific Ocean off San Diego, CA.
- **Post-mission (April/May 2026):** Orion heat shield post-flight inspection.
- **Mid-2027:** Artemis III — restructured as LEO mission (commercial HLS and Axiom suit demonstration). No crewed lunar landing.
- **~2028:** Artemis IV — first crewed lunar landing.
