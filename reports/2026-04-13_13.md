# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-13 13:00 UTC

## Summary

Two notable new developments since the 12:00 UTC report: Saturday Night Live's April 12 cold-open sketch parodied the Artemis II mission with host Colman Domingo, reflecting the mission's broad cultural impact; and new reporting confirms Boeing has issued internal layoff notices tied to the escalating uncertainty around the SLS rocket's future under the Trump NASA administration. Core mission status (Day 12 post-splashdown crew reconditioning, pending Artemis III crew announcement, heat shield inspection, ESM valve analysis) is unchanged.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — Day 12 crew post-flight reconditioning, medical/human-performance evaluations, and lunar science debriefs at NASA Johnson Space Center
- **Mission Duration:** 10 days (April 1–10, 2026); splashdown April 10 at 8:07 p.m. EDT (00:07 UTC April 11) in the Pacific Ocean ~96 km off San Diego; recovered by USS *John P. Murtha* (LPD 26)
- **Total distance flown:** 694,481 miles; maximum distance from Earth: 252,756 miles (406,771 km) — a new human spaceflight record
- **Notable Changes:**
  - **Boeing SLS layoff notices issued** — Boeing employees reportedly warned to prepare for potential end of SLS program as NASA finalizes post-Artemis II architecture decisions
  - ESM helium valve leak confirmed ~10× worse than ground-test levels; redesign required for Artemis IV
  - Lunar Gateway cancelled (March 2026); Artemis III redesignated as LEO rendezvous/docking test (mid-2027); Artemis IV (~early 2028) planned as first crewed lunar landing

### Technical & Engineering

**NEW — Boeing SLS layoffs:**
- Boeing has issued internal layoff notices to employees tied to the SLS program, per reporting from *Rocket City Now* and *Gizmodo* (April 11–13). The notices reflect growing uncertainty about SLS's future after NASA cancelled the Exploration Upper Stage (EUS/Block 1B) contract in February 2026 and is considering halting SLS production after Artemis V.
- NASA's Artemis architecture revisions potentially represent a direct truncation of Boeing's projected ~$82 billion, 20-launch SLS pipeline.

### Science & Instruments

*No new science results published since 12:00 UTC. Standing status:*

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** 7,000+ high-resolution images and video from the April 6 lunar flyby (closest approach 4,067 mi / 6,545 km from the far-side surface) being processed at JSC. Six meteoroid impact flashes recorded on the darkened lunar surface; hazard-modeling analysis ongoing.
- **Distance record:** 252,756 miles (406,771 km) from Earth on April 6, 2026, surpassing Apollo 13's 248,655 mi (1970) by more than 4,000 miles.
- **AVATAR Bone Marrow Organ Chips:** Hardware recovered post-splashdown; comparison analysis of flight chips vs. ground controls underway at Wyss Institute (Harvard).
- **ARCHeR (Artemis Research for Crew Health and Readiness):** Wearable sensors tracked crew heart rate, sleep, cognitive function, and team dynamics; actigraphy and audiovisual data being processed.
- **Radiation dosimetry (M-42 EXT detectors + personal dosimeters):** Preliminary readings within ~10% of predicted margins; full analysis ongoing.
- **International CubeSats:** CubeSats from Germany (*TACHELES*), Argentina (*ATENEA*), South Korea, and Saudi Arabia deployed in high Earth orbit; data analysis ongoing.
- **IAU crater naming:** NASA will formally submit proposals for *Carroll* and *Integrity* craters; IAU decision expected ~mid-May 2026.
