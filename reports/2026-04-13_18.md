# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-13 18:00 UTC

## Summary

No significant new developments in the two hours since the 16:00 UTC report. The Artemis II crew remains in Day 14 of their ~21-day post-flight reconditioning protocol at NASA Johnson Space Center in Houston. Space.com designated an April 13 crew celebration photograph as its "Space Photo of the Day," marking continued media attention to the mission's legacy. Artemis III crew announcement and the crew's White House visit both remain pending without confirmed dates.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — Day 14 of crew post-flight reconditioning, medical/human-performance evaluations, and lunar science debriefs at NASA Johnson Space Center
- **Mission Duration:** 10 days (April 1–10, 2026); splashdown April 10 at 8:07 p.m. EDT (00:07 UTC April 11) in the Pacific Ocean ~96 km off San Diego; recovered by USS *John P. Murtha* (LPD 26)
- **Total distance flown:** 694,481 miles; maximum distance from Earth: 252,756 miles (406,771 km) — a new human spaceflight record (surpassing Apollo 13's 248,655 mi, 1970)
- **Notable Changes:**
  - No new mission-status changes since the 16:00 UTC report
  - White House visit: still no confirmed date (crew invited by President Trump; timing remains TBD)
  - Artemis III crew announcement: still pending ("soon" per NASA AA Kshatriya, April 10; NASA Administrator Isaacman, April 11)
  - Boeing SLS layoff notices: no new updates since April 11–13 coverage (~200 workers in Huntsville, AL)
  - ESM helium valve leak confirmed ~10× worse than ground-test levels; ESA redesign required for Artemis IV
  - Lunar Gateway cancelled (March 2026); Artemis III redesignated as LEO rendezvous/docking test (mid-2027); Artemis IV (~early 2028) planned as first crewed lunar landing

### Science & Instruments

*No new science results published since 16:00 UTC. Full standing status:*

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** 7,000+ high-resolution images and video from the April 6 lunar flyby (closest approach 4,067 mi / 6,545 km from the far-side surface) being processed at JSC. Six meteoroid impact flashes recorded on the darkened lunar surface; hazard-modeling analysis ongoing.
  - [NASA's Artemis II Lunar Science Operations to Inform Future Missions — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/solar-system/nasas-artemis-ii-lunar-science-operations-to-inform-future-missions/)
  - [APOD: 2026 April 11 – Artemis II: Flight Day 6](https://apod.nasa.gov/apod/ap260411.html)
- **Distance record:** 252,756 miles (406,771 km) from Earth on April 6, 2026 — surpassing Apollo 13's 248,655 mi (1970) by more than 4,000 miles.
- **AVATAR Bone Marrow Organ Chips:** Hardware recovered post-splashdown; comparison analysis of flight chips vs. ground controls underway at Wyss Institute (Harvard).
  - [AVATAR investigation — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/biological-physical/investigations/avatar/)
- **ARCHeR (Artemis Research for Crew Health and Readiness):** Wearable sensors tracked crew heart rate, sleep, cognitive function, and team dynamics; actigraphy and audiovisual data being processed at JSC.
  - [NTRS 20260002430 — PROJECT ARCHeR: Artemis II Update](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20260002430)
- **Radiation dosimetry (M-42 EXT detectors + personal dosimeters):** Preliminary readings within ~10% of predicted margins; full analysis ongoing.
- **International CubeSats:** CubeSats from Germany (*TACHELES*), Argentina (*ATENEA*), South Korea, and Saudi Arabia deployed in high Earth orbit; data analysis ongoing.
- **IAU crater naming:** NASA will formally submit proposals for *Carroll* and *Integrity* craters (named by crew during flyby); IAU decision expected ~mid-May 2026.
