# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-03 07:00 UTC

## Summary
Artemis II is approximately 33 hours into its ~10-day mission, with Orion "Integrity" and its four-person crew well into translunar transit following a successful TLI burn late on April 2. Flight Day 3 begins with the crew healthy and all primary spacecraft systems nominal after a toilet fan anomaly was resolved on Flight Day 1. Lunar closest approach remains on track for ~April 6.

## Mission Status
- **Current Phase:** In-flight — translunar transit, Flight Day 3 (April 3, 2026)
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC (6:35 p.m. EDT) from Launch Complex 39B, KSC
- **Elapsed Mission Time:** ~33 hours
- **Splashdown Target:** ~April 10, 2026, Pacific Ocean
- **Notable Changes:** No new trajectory or schedule changes. Mission proceeding nominally following pre-launch delays caused by a liquid hydrogen leak (February wet dress rehearsal) and a helium flow issue that triggered a VAB rollback in March.

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates
- NASA is providing daily mission status briefings from Johnson Space Center beginning April 2, 2026, except for April 6 (lunar flyby day). Briefings air on the NASA YouTube channel; media may RSVP to jsccommu@mail.nasa.gov up to two hours before each briefing.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/missions/artemis/artemis-2/nasa-sets-coverage-for-artemis-ii-moon-mission/
- All major Flight Day 1 and 2 milestones completed successfully: apogee raise burn, proximity operations demonstration, perigee raise burn, and Translunar Injection burn.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/mission/artemis-ii/
- NASA confirmed a toilet fan controller anomaly was identified and resolved on Flight Day 1 (April 1). The crew and Mission Control troubleshot the issue; normal toilet operations were restored. The toilet is the first commode on any crewed deep-space mission.
  - Sources: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/02/artemis-ii-flight-update-crew-and-ground-teams-successfully-troubleshoot-orions-toilet/ | https://www.astronomy.com/space-exploration/artemis-2-crew-fixes-toilet-can-now-pee-in-it/

### Technical & Engineering

**Orion Spacecraft ("Integrity")**
- Toilet anomaly details: Immediately after launch, a fault in the toilet's fan controller disabled the urine collection function. For approximately six hours, at least one crew member used a NASA flight-approved contingency bag. Crew and Mission Control isolated the fault and restored full toilet functionality before end of Flight Day 1.
  - Source: https://theprint.in/science/artemis-ii-and-its-toilet-troubles-it-failed-after-takeoff/2894861/
- All four solar array wings confirmed generating nominal power throughout transit.
- Orion's ESM main engine fired for ~5 minutes 50 seconds on April 2 (~23:49 UTC) for TLI, placing the spacecraft on a free-return lunar trajectory.

**SLS Rocket (post-separation)**
- SLS core stage and ICPS separated nominally. All four international CubeSats were deployed from the SLS stage adapter approximately 5 hours after launch.

**Heat Shield**
- No new heat shield status updates. The pre-launch controversy centered on Artemis I's post-flight char loss findings; NASA's mitigations (redesigned Avcoat porosity, direct reentry trajectory) were reviewed and approved at the March 12, 2026 Flight Readiness Review.
- Post-mission heat shield inspection will be a critical data point for Artemis III planning.

**Trajectory**
- Free-return trajectory: no course correction burns have been publicly reported as required so far; trajectory remains nominal.
- Closest lunar approach (~4,000–6,000 miles from the surface): ~April 6, 2026, ~1:45 p.m. EDT.
- Distance record attempt: ~252,000 miles from Earth, exceeding Apollo 13's ~248,655-mile record, also targeted for ~April 6.

**Ground Systems**
- Johnson Space Center Mission Control continues 24/7 flight operations. Flight controllers rotating through shifts; daily status briefings scheduled through mission conclusion.

### Science & Instruments

**Crew Health Experiments (ongoing)**
- **AVATAR (A Virtual Astronaut Tissue Analog Response):** Organ-on-a-chip devices measuring effects of radiation and microgravity on human tissue. Blood and saliva sample collection underway.
- **ARCHeR:** Wearable monitoring device (watch-like) tracking crew activity levels, sleep patterns, and well-being throughout transit.
  - Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-00964-4

**CubeSat Science (post-deployment)**
- All four CubeSats confirmed deployed and operational after SLS stage adapter separation. No new operational data publicly released as of 07:00 UTC April 3.
- **K-RadCube (South Korea — KASA/KASI):** Measuring Van Allen Belt radiation levels.
- **Space Weather CubeSat-1 (Saudi Arabia — SSA):** Measuring radiation, solar X-rays, energetic particles, and magnetic fields.
- **TACHELES (Germany — DLR):** Demonstrating in-space technologies for future lunar logistics.
- **ATENEA (Argentina — CONAE):** Radiation shielding and long-range communications research.
  - Source: https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2026/04/02/satellite-cubesat/5711775174421/

**Upcoming Science Activities**
- Lunar far side photography: During ~April 6 flyby, crew will photograph the far side under oblique illumination, highlighting crater rims, ridges, and slopes. These images will support geological and future landing-site studies.
  - Source: https://science.nasa.gov/artemis-ii-science/

### Crew Updates
- **Commander Reid Wiseman (NASA):** Mission proceeding nominally; leading crew operations.
- **Pilot Victor Glover (NASA):** Successfully completed manual proximity operations demonstration on Flight Day 1; reported real-world vehicle performance exceeded simulator predictions. First Black astronaut traveling to the vicinity of the Moon.
- **Mission Specialist Christina Koch (NASA):** First woman to travel to the vicinity of the Moon.
- **Mission Specialist Jeremy Hansen (CSA):** First non-American to travel beyond low Earth orbit; first Canadian to travel to the Moon's vicinity.
- All four crew members reported healthy and performing nominal mission activities as of end of Flight Day 2 (April 2–3).
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/mission/artemis-ii/

## Upcoming Milestones
- **~April 6, 2026 (~1:45 p.m. EDT):** Orion reaches ~252,000 miles from Earth (distance record attempt); lunar closest approach (~4,000–6,000 miles from surface); crew photographs lunar far side. No daily status briefing from JSC on this date (flyby activities).
- **~April 10, 2026:** Planned splashdown in the Pacific Ocean; U.S. Navy recovery operations.
- **Post-mission:** Heat shield post-flight inspection — critical data for Artemis III reentry planning.
- **Mid-2027:** NASA plans to launch Human Landing System (HLS) vehicles to low Earth orbit for Artemis III rendezvous/docking tests.
- **~2027:** Artemis III crewed lunar landing mission (NET 2027).
- **Cancelled (March 2026):** Lunar Gateway space station program cancelled by NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman; components being redirected toward plans for a permanent lunar surface base.
