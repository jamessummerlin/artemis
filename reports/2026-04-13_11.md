# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-13 11:00 UTC

## Summary

No significant new developments have emerged in the two hours since the 09:00 UTC report. The Artemis II crew (Day 10 post-splashdown) continues post-flight reconditioning and medical evaluations at NASA's Johnson Space Center in Houston. All previously tracked pending items — Artemis III crew announcement, White House visit date, IAU crater naming decision, and Orion post-flight inspections — remain outstanding with no new information.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — Day 10 crew post-flight reconditioning, medical/human-performance evaluations, and lunar science debriefs at NASA Johnson Space Center
- **Mission Duration:** 10 days (April 1–10, 2026); splashdown April 10 at 8:07 p.m. EDT (00:07 UTC April 11) in the Pacific Ocean ~96 km off San Diego; recovered by USS *John P. Murtha* (LPD 26)
- **Notable Changes:**
  - No new schedule, architecture, or personnel changes since the 09:00 UTC report
  - ESM helium valve leak confirmed ~10× worse than ground-test levels; redesign required for Artemis IV
  - Lunar Gateway cancelled (March 2026); Artemis III redesignated as LEO rendezvous/docking test (mid-2027); Artemis IV (~early 2028) remains planned as first crewed lunar landing

  - [The Artemis 2 space toilet is actually working fine. But there is another problem — Space.com](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/artemis/the-artemis-2-space-toilet-is-actually-working-fine-but-there-is-another-problem)

- **SLS architecture update:** NASA selected ULA Centaur V (dual RL10 engines) to replace the ICPS/Exploration Upper Stage from Artemis IV onward; Mobile Launcher 2 development cancelled (Feb. 27, 2026).

### Science & Instruments

*No new science results published since 09:00 UTC. Standing status:*

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** 7,000+ high-resolution images/video from the April 6 flyby (closest approach 4,067 mi / 6,545 km) being processed at JSC. Six meteoroid impact flashes recorded on the darkened lunar surface; hazard-modeling analysis ongoing.
- **Distance record:** 252,756 miles (406,771 km) from Earth, surpassing Apollo 13's 248,655 mi (1970).
- **AVATAR Bone Marrow Organ Chips:** Hardware recovered; comparison analysis against ground controls underway at Wyss Institute (Harvard).
  - [Why NASA sent 'organ chips' of the Artemis II crew into space — CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/09/science/artemis-2-human-health-experiments)
- **Radiation dosimetry:** M-42 EXT detectors showing preliminary readings within ~10% of predicted margins; full analysis ongoing.
- **ARCHeR crew health data:** Actigraphy and audiovisual data being processed through machine-learning pipeline (see NTRS 20260002430 in previous reports).
- **IAU crater naming:** NASA will formally submit proposals for *Carroll* and *Integrity* craters; IAU decision expected "in about a month" (~mid-May 2026).
  - [Artemis II names moon crater Carroll after Reid Wiseman's late wife — NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/07/nx-s1-5776440/artemis-moon-crater-carroll-wiseman)
  - [Moon memorial: Artemis 2 astronauts name lunar 'bright spot' after mission commander's late wife — Space.com](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/artemis/moon-memorial-artemis-2-astronauts-name-lunar-bright-spot-after-mission-commanders-late-wife)
