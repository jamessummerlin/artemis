# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-02 06:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is approximately 7.5 hours into Flight Day 2 with all primary systems performing nominally. The mission is in a quiet pre-TLI hold period: no major flight events occur until the Trans-Lunar Injection burn scheduled for ~23:35 UTC tonight. The only notable development since the 05:00 UTC report is an official NASA blog post confirming the Orion waste management system (toilet) fault has been fully resolved by crew and ground teams.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** In-flight — Flight Day 2; pre-TLI system reviews, crew rest/preparation
- **Launched:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35 UTC from LC-39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Current Orbit:** High Earth checkout orbit (~43,730 miles / ~70,400 km apogee)
- **TLI Burn Status:** On schedule for ~23:35 UTC tonight (~7:35 PM EDT); go/no-go decision pending
- **Splashdown Target:** April 11, 2026, Pacific Ocean off San Diego
- **Notable Changes:** Waste management anomaly (minor) officially resolved per new NASA blog post

### Technical & Engineering

**Current Spacecraft Status (All Nominal):**
- Four solar array wings (SAWs) generating full power
- ECLSS life support systems active and providing crew oxygen/water from ESM tanks (first in-flight operational use with crew)
- Deep Space Network handover from Near Space Network verified at apogee; comm link stable
- All four main propulsion system engine checks complete ahead of TLI
- Orion reaction control system thrusters confirmed healthy post-proximity operations

**TLI Burn Profile (Scheduled — Not Yet Executed):**
- Engine: ESM orbital maneuvering system engine (~6,000 lbf thrust)
- Duration: ~6 minutes, 5 seconds
- Delta-V: ~900 mph added velocity
- Trajectory: Multi-translunar injection (MTLI) — free-return figure-eight cislunar path
- Post-TLI coast to Moon: ~4 days
- This is the **last major propulsive event** of the mission; subsequent course adjustments are minor Trajectory Correction Burns (TCBs) on Days 3–4

**Trajectory Correction Burns (Post-TLI, Days 3–4):**
Per NTRS paper 20230000223 (Woffinden, Eckman, Robinson), TCBs are optimized around GN&C performance margins, navigation uncertainty, crew schedule, and thruster selection. Mission controllers will execute these burns using the methodology described in that document. The free-return trajectory requires no Lunar Orbit Insertion; lunar gravity returns Orion to Earth autonomously.

**Free-Return Trajectory Key Parameters:**
- Closest lunar approach: ~4,112 statute miles (~6,617 km) from the far-side surface on April 6
- Maximum distance from Earth: ~252,799 statute miles — 4,144 miles beyond Apollo 13's record (~248,655 miles)
- Reentry speed: ~25,000 mph; modified to a direct (steeper) descent angle to avoid the two-pulse aerothermal environment implicated in Artemis I heat shield spalling

**SLS Program Status:**
An NTRS report (20250011679, December 2025) confirms hardware for Artemis III, IV, and V is in production. Following the February 2026 cancellation of the Exploration Upper Stage (EUS), NASA awarded a sole-source contract to ULA in March 2026 for Centaur V upper stages to fly on Artemis IV and V.

### Science & Instruments

No new science instrument activations or data releases in this reporting window. Key systems remain in checkout or standby mode:

- **O2O Optical Communications System:** In checkout; planned to transmit up to 260 Mbps 4K video during lunar flyby on April 6. Ground station handoff between NASA White Sands Complex and Table Mountain Facility tested at apogee.
- **AVATAR (bone marrow organ-on-chip):** Activated. Cells are being exposed to deep-space radiation beyond the Van Allen belts for the first time; data downlinked continuously.
- **ARCHeR Wristbands:** Active on all four crew members; monitoring sleep quality, stress biomarkers, cognitive performance, and team dynamics in real time.
- **Radiation Suite (HERA dosimeters + Crew Active Dosimeters):** All six HERA units nominal; crew active dosimeters recording trans-LEO dose rates.
- **CubeSats (all four):** TACHELES, K-RadCube, ATENEA, and SHMS confirmed in deployed elliptical orbits and collecting data. No anomalies reported.
- **Lunar flyby science preparation:** Science Evaluation Room at JSC staffed; real-time geology guidance team assembled for April 6 flyby window. ~21% of the lunar far side will be sunlit at closest approach; the crew will photograph regions never before directly observed by humans.

### Crew Updates

No new crew communications since the 05:00 UTC report. Crew is in a rest/preparation period ahead of tonight's TLI burn.

- **Commander Reid Wiseman:** Overseeing pre-TLI systems reviews; will give formal "ready for TLI" concurrence to Mission Control.
- **Pilot Victor Glover:** Completed first-ever manual Orion piloting session during Day 1 proximity operations (~70 minutes, closed to ~30 ft of ICPS). Resting ahead of TLI monitoring duties.
- **Mission Specialist Christina Koch:** Performing ESM engine system readiness checks. Will be the first woman to travel beyond LEO.
- **Mission Specialist Jeremy Hansen (CSA):** On his first spaceflight. First Canadian and first non-U.S. citizen ever to participate in a lunar mission. First scheduled public space-to-ground Q&A remains on **April 4**. [CSA event](https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/events/2026/2026-04-04-artemis-ii-first-q-and-a-jeremy-hansen-live-from-space.asp)

**Historical firsts this crew will achieve upon TLI:**
- Victor Glover — first person of color to travel beyond low Earth orbit
- Christina Koch — first woman to travel beyond low Earth orbit
- Jeremy Hansen — first non-U.S. citizen and first Canadian to travel beyond low Earth orbit

## Academic & Research Papers

- **Title:** Optimized Trajectory Correction Burn Placement for the NASA Artemis II Mission
  - **Authors:** David Woffinden, Randy Eckman, Shane Robinson (NASA JSC)
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20230000223 (AAS 23-062)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20230000223)
  - **Relevance:** Directly applicable in-flight; describes TCB optimization methodology being used by mission controllers on Days 3–4 post-TLI.

- **Title:** Orion Artemis II Optical Communications Architecture, Implementation and Test Systems
  - **Authors:** NASA JSC / Glenn Research Center
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250010065](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065) — October 2025
  - **Relevance:** Detailed architecture of the O2O system (260 Mbps downlink, 20 Mbps uplink) undergoing checkout; enables 4K video from the Moon.

- **Title:** Hitching a Ride on History — Artemis II CubeSat Payloads
  - **Authors:** NASA MSFC / mission teams
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250005942](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942) — June 2025
  - **Relevance:** Documents all four deployed CubeSat payloads (TACHELES, K-RadCube, ATENEA, SHMS) and their science objectives.

- **Title:** NASA's SLS Super Heavy-Lift Rocket in Process for Artemis II and Beyond
  - **Authors:** NASA MSFC SLS Team
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250011679](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250011679) — December 22, 2025
  - **Relevance:** Describes hardware production status for Artemis II through V; confirms RS-25 engine production certification and EUS cancellation context.

- **Title:** Deep Space Radiation Measurements and Crew Radiation Protection for the NASA Artemis Program
  - **Authors:** NASA JSC / Langley Research Center
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20250002757](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250002757) — March 2025
  - **Relevance:** Crew radiation storm shelter protocols and dose rate benchmarks; directly informing real-time dosimetry interpretation as crew exits Earth's magnetosphere.

- **Title:** Machine Learning-Based Radiation Forecasting for Deep Space Missions Using SDO Imagery
  - **Authors:** Gurav et al.
  - **Source:** [arXiv:2411.17703](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.17703) — November 2024
  - **Relevance:** Solar energetic particle forecasting methodology applicable during trans-lunar coast and lunar flyby.

- **Title:** Risk Characterization Research for Behavioral Medicine and Human Factors — Artemis II
  - **Authors:** NASA JSC Human Research Program
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20240011729](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20240011729) — September 2024
  - **Relevance:** Documents human system integration risks and crew behavioral health protocols for the first crewed deep-space mission since Apollo; ARCHeR wristband data will be assessed against these benchmarks.
