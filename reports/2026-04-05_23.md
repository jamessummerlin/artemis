# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-05 23:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is on Flight Day 5, en route to the Moon with all systems nominal. The crew completed spacesuit system demonstrations and received their final list of 30 lunar science observation targets today, ahead of the lunar flyby at closest approach (~4,000–6,000 miles from the surface) scheduled for Monday, April 6. The crew is set to enter the lunar sphere of gravitational influence at approximately 12:41 a.m. EDT on April 6, marking the first time humans have been in the Moon's gravity well since Apollo 17 in 1972.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** In-flight — translunar transit, Day 5 post-launch
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC (6:35:12 PM EDT), Launch Complex 39B, KSC
- **Lunar Flyby (Closest Approach):** ~April 6, 2026, ~1:45 PM EDT
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026, ~8:07 PM EDT, Pacific Ocean off San Diego, CA
- **Notable Changes:** No new mission profile changes since last report. Gateway cancellation (March 2026) and Artemis III restructuring (LEO demonstration, no crewed landing) remain in effect. Splashdown revised from April 11 to April 10 in updated scheduling.

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **Flight Day 5 (April 5, 2026):** The crew began the day with a full demonstration of the Orion Crew Survival System (OCSS) spacesuits — performing leak checks, simulating seat entry, and assessing mobility including the ability to eat and drink while suited.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/05/artemis-ii-flight-day-5-crew-starts-day-with-suit-demo/
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/05/artemis-ii-flight-day-5-crew-demos-suits-readies-for-lunar-flyby/
- The science team transmitted the final list of 30 lunar surface targets to the crew on the morning of April 5. Targets include the Orientale basin (a ~600-mile-wide crater straddling the near/far side boundary) and the Hertzsprung crater for comparative geological observations.
- **Flight Day 4 (April 4, 2026):** Crew completed a manual piloting demonstration — Pilot Victor Glover hand-flew Orion in deep space as a test of vehicle handling and crew interface, logging performance data that will inform future crewed lunar missions.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/04/artemis-ii-flight-day-4-crew-completes-manual-piloting-demonstration/
- Crew held a live interview with ABC News from aboard Orion on Flight Day 2; ongoing media engagements continue.
  - Source: https://abcnews.com/US/entire-globe-artemis-ii-astronauts-speak-abc-news/story?id=131674209
- NASA's Artemis II mission page and all live blog updates: https://www.nasa.gov/mission/artemis-ii/

### Technical & Engineering

**SLS Rocket**
- All SLS separation events performed nominally; no issues reported since launch. No new SLS-related engineering updates.
- SLS Block 1B and Block 2 upgrade programs remain formally canceled as of February 26, 2026. Only Block 1 configuration will fly future Artemis missions.

**Orion Spacecraft ("Integrity")**
- All four solar array wings remain deployed and generating power nominally throughout translunar transit.
- Trajectory correction burns completed successfully: outbound trajectory correction burn on Flight Day 3 (April 3) executed as planned.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/03/artemis-ii-flight-day-3-outbound-trajectory-correction-burn-update/
- A final outbound trajectory correction burn is planned ahead of the April 6 lunar flyby to finalize closest approach parameters.
- UWMS (toilet/urine collection) fan jam from Day 1 was resolved by the crew and remains fully operational.
- Minor email system issue (noted Day 1) fully resolved.

**Heat Shield & Reentry Profile**
- No new in-flight heat shield data; the modified steeper direct-reentry profile (eliminating the Artemis I skip maneuver) will be validated post-splashdown on April 10.
- Context: NASA chose this profile after discovering 100+ anomalous char-loss zones in the Avcoat ablative material during Artemis I post-flight inspections. Modeling and ground testing confirmed the steeper angle keeps thermal exposure within structural and thermal margins.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-shares-orion-heat-shield-findings-updates-artemis-moon-missions/
- Orion will re-enter at approximately 25,000 mph (~40,000 km/h), the fastest crewed reentry ever attempted. Eleven parachutes will slow the capsule to ~17 mph for splashdown.

**Optical Communications (O2O)**
- The Orion Artemis II Optical Communications (O2O) laser payload continues nominal operation, achieving up to 260 Mbps downlink and 20 Mbps uplink via laser link — the first operational crewed deep-space laser communications test.
- Ground terminals: White Sands, NM and Table Mountain, CA.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/technology/space-comms/o2o/

**Distance Record**
- Orion is on track to set a new crewed distance record of approximately 252,000 miles from Earth during the April 6 flyby, surpassing Apollo 13's record of ~248,655 miles.

### Science & Instruments

- **Lunar Observation Campaign:** The crew received their 30 prioritized observation targets from the Lunar Targeting Plan (LTP) software system. Key targets:
  - **Orientale Basin:** ~600-mile-wide impact crater straddling near/far-side boundary — first close human observations of this feature.
  - **Hertzsprung Crater:** Comparison with Orientale to study crater evolution over geologic timescales.
  - Far-side regions inaccessible from Earth-based observation form a significant portion of the target list.
  - Source: https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/05/artemis-ii-flight-day-5-crew-demos-suits-readies-for-lunar-flyby/
  - Source: https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20260001932

- **Camera Configuration:** The crew configured handheld cameras with 80–400 mm and 14–24 mm lenses for the lunar flyby photography session.

- **AVATAR Investigation (A Virtual Astronaut Tissue Analog Response):** Organ-on-a-chip devices studying effects of radiation and microgravity on human tissue — active throughout the mission.

- **ARCHeR (wearable health monitoring wristband):** Continuously collecting crew biometric and health data in the deep-space radiation environment.

- **O2O Laser Communications:** Actively operating in-flight as a science/technology demonstration (see Technical section).

- **CubeSats (deployed April 1, ~5 hours post-launch) — all confirmed operational:**
  - **K-RadCube** (South Korea/KASA/KASI): Tissue-mimicking radiation dosimetry through the Van Allen Belts.
  - **Space Weather CubeSat-1** (Saudi Arabia/Saudi Space Agency): Solar wind, X-ray, energetic particle, and magnetic field monitoring.
  - **TACHELES** (Germany/DLR): Electrical component testing for future lunar logistics vehicles.
  - **ATENEA** (Argentina/CONAE): Radiation shielding, orbital design, GPS, and long-range comms demonstration.
  - Source: https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942

### Crew Updates

- All four crew members are in good health and performing nominal mission activities on Flight Day 5.
- **Commander Reid Wiseman (NASA):** Leading all mission phases; oldest person to travel beyond low Earth orbit.
- **Pilot Victor Glover (NASA):** First person of color to travel beyond LEO; completed manual deep-space piloting demonstration on Flight Day 4 with performance exceeding simulator predictions.
- **Mission Specialist 1 Christina Koch (NASA):** First woman to travel beyond LEO.
- **Mission Specialist 2 Jeremy Hansen (CSA):** First Canadian and first non-American citizen to travel beyond LEO; held a live press availability from space for Canadian media.
  - Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/space-agency/news/2026/04/artemis-ii-jeremy-hansen-to-answer-questions-from-media-live-from-space.html
- Crew is preparing for the high-intensity lunar flyby observation period beginning April 6; suit demos today confirm readiness for any contingency requiring suited operations.
- CSA is maintaining a daily mission logbook: https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/missions/artemis-ii/daily-logbook.asp

### International Partners

**ESA (European Space Agency)**
- European Service Module (ESM-2), assembled by Airbus in Bremen with contributions from 13 ESA member states and over 100 European suppliers, continues to perform all propulsion, power, thermal control, and life-support functions nominally.
- ESM main engine executed the TLI burn; ESM solar arrays providing continuous power throughout translunar transit.
- ESA ground teams monitoring the mission from ESTEC (Netherlands), European Astronaut Centre (Germany), and NASA's Johnson Space Center (Houston).
- ESA's M-42 EXT radiation detectors are monitoring the deep-space radiation environment.
  - Sources: https://www.esa.int/Newsroom/Press_Releases/Europe_powers_Artemis_II_mission_to_the_Moon | https://www.dlr.de/en/latest/news/2026/artemis-ii-to-launch-for-the-moon-with-german-and-european-tech-on-board

**CSA (Canadian Space Agency)**
- Jeremy Hansen is fulfilling all Mission Specialist duties nominally.
- Canada was the first international partner to commit to the Lunar Gateway program (February 2019); with Gateway canceled in March 2026, CSA's long-term hardware commitment (Canadarm3) is under programmatic review.

## Upcoming Milestones

- **~April 6, 2026 at 12:41 a.m. EDT:** Crew enters the lunar sphere of gravitational influence — first humans in the Moon's gravity well since Apollo 17 (1972).
- **~April 6, 2026 at ~1:45 PM EDT:** Lunar closest approach (~4,000–6,000 miles from surface); 30-target observation and photography session of lunar near and far sides; potential crewed distance record of ~252,000 miles from Earth.
- **~April 7–9, 2026:** Return transit to Earth; final trajectory correction burns.
- **April 10, 2026 at ~8:07 PM EDT (5:07 PM PDT):** Splashdown off the coast of San Diego, CA; U.S. Navy recovery operations. Reentry at ~25,000 mph — fastest crewed reentry ever attempted.
- **Post-mission (April/May 2026):** Post-flight inspection of Orion heat shield — critical validation of the modified steeper reentry profile before Artemis IV planning.
- **Mid-2027:** Artemis III — restructured as a low Earth orbit mission to demonstrate commercial Human Landing System vehicles (SpaceX Starship HLS and/or Blue Origin Blue Moon) and Axiom Space EVA suits. No crewed lunar landing.
- **~2028:** Artemis IV — first crewed lunar landing (redesignated from original Artemis III objective).
- **Canceled:** Lunar Gateway space station (March 2026); SLS Block 1B/Block 2 upgrade programs (February 26, 2026).
