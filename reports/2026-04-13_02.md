# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-13 02:00 UTC

## Summary

No major new NASA press releases have been issued since the April 11 crew-return ceremony; post-mission activities remain the dominant story. Science data and hardware analysis are underway at JSC and KSC — detailed Orion heat shield CT-scan results and initial AVATAR organ-on-a-chip post-flight comparisons are both pending public release. The Artemis III crew announcement remains "soon" per NASA AA Kshatriya, and no White House visit date has been confirmed for the Artemis II crew.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — crew post-flight reconditioning, medical and human-performance evaluations, and lunar science debriefs at NASA Johnson Space Center (Day 3 post-return)
- **Mission Duration:** 10 days (April 1–10, 2026); splashdown April 10 at 8:07 p.m. EDT (00:07 UTC April 11) in the Pacific Ocean ~50–80 mi off San Diego; recovered by USS John P. Murtha (LPD 26)
- **Notable Changes:**
  - No new schedule or architecture updates since the April 10–11 post-splashdown period
  - All post-mission analysis workstreams are active; no anomalies reported from reentry
  - Artemis III (mid-2027, LEO HLS docking/AxEMU test) and Artemis IV (~2028, first crewed lunar landing) architecture confirmed; no further changes

### Technical & Engineering

- **Orion heat shield post-reentry inspection (ongoing):** Orion "Integrity" has been transported to Kennedy Space Center for detailed CT scanning and AVCOAT ablation analysis. Immediate post-splashdown visual inspection by recovery divers found no unexpected anomalies. Full CT-scan findings have not yet been publicly released — results will directly inform future Orion production batches, which are already being manufactured with improved AVCOAT permeability uniformity.
  - Background: The Artemis I heat shield showed unexpected char-layer ablation at 100+ locations; NASA redesigned the reentry trajectory (steeper descent angle) rather than replacing the heat shield for Artemis II. That modified trajectory was used for the April 10 reentry.
  - [NASA Shares Orion Heat Shield Findings, Updates Artemis Moon Missions — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-shares-orion-heat-shield-findings-updates-artemis-moon-missions/)
  - [NASA confident Artemis 2 heat shield will protect crew during re-entry — Spaceflight Now](https://spaceflightnow.com/2026/04/10/nasa-confident-artemis-ii-heat-shield-will-protect-crew-during-re-entry/)
- **Manual piloting data analysis (ongoing):** Data from both manual piloting demonstration sessions (Koch/Hansen on FD4; Wiseman/Glover on FD8, ~01:59 UTC April 9) is being analyzed for Orion handling qualities; results will feed into Artemis III and IV crew training for rendezvous/docking operations.
- **Artemis III mission profile (confirmed):** NASA will conduct an Orion rendezvous and docking test with SpaceX Starship HLS and/or Blue Origin Blue Moon in low Earth orbit (mid-2027), along with AxEMU spacesuit testing. A lunar landing is not included. Artemis IV (~2028) is now designated as the first crewed lunar landing.
  - [Artemis 3 and beyond: What's next for NASA after Artemis 2 moon success — Space.com](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/artemis/artemis-3-and-beyond-whats-next-for-nasa-after-artemis-2-moon-success)

### Science & Instruments

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO) — data analysis underway:** High-resolution imagery from the ~7-hour lunar flyby on April 6 (closest approach 4,067 miles) is being processed at JSC. The crew captured the Vavilov Crater on the far side within Hertzsprung Basin, revealing a transition between smooth interior materials and rugged ejecta. Human color discrimination of mineral composition (brown/blue hues) is being cross-referenced with existing orbital remote sensing datasets from LRO and Kaguya.
  - [Artemis II Lunar Flyby Gallery — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/gallery/lunar-flyby/)
  - [NASA Scientific Visualization Studio — Artemis II Lunar Flyby Simulation](https://svs.gsfc.nasa.gov/5633)
- **Radiation monitoring — preliminary data:** M-42 EXT European-designed radiation detectors (with a measurement range 6× greater than those on Artemis I) show preliminary readings suggesting Orion's shielding performed within ~10% of predicted safety margins. No significant solar energetic particle events occurred during the mission. Full dosimetry analysis is ongoing.
  - [Artemis II Mission Reveals Critical Environmental Data for Future Space Exploration — EnviroLink](https://www.envirolink.org/2026/04/10/artemis-ii-mission-reveals-critical-environmental-data-for-future-space-exploration/)
- **AVATAR organ-on-a-chip — post-flight analysis begun:** Hardware recovered with Orion has been transported to JSC. The Wyss Institute at Harvard will now compare the two sets of Bone Marrow Chips — flown in-flight with the crew vs. ground controls — to assess how deep-space radiation and microgravity affected bone-marrow blood-forming (hematopoietic) cells. Results are expected to take months of analysis; no preliminary findings released yet.
  - [Wyss Institute technologies enable breakthrough in astronaut health research aboard NASA's Artemis II mission — Wyss Institute](https://wyss.harvard.edu/news/wyss-institute-technologies-enable-breakthrough-in-astronaut-health-research-aboard-nasas-artemis-ii-mission/)
  - [How tiny chips with Artemis II astronaut cells could pave the way for living on the moon — Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/science/2026/04/09/avatar-astronauts-organ-on-a-chip-artemis/)
- **Meteoroid impact flashes:** Six distinct flashes observed on the darkened lunar surface during flyby; data is being analyzed to refine lunar impact hazard models relevant to future surface operations.
- **Blood and saliva samples:** Pre-, in-, and post-flight samples are with JSC biomedical teams for immune system and stress-response studies.
  - [Artemis II Science — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/artemis-ii-science/)

## Academic & Research Papers

- **Title:** Artemis II Lunar Science and Operations
  - **Authors:** A. N. Deutsch et al.
  - **Source:** 57th Lunar and Planetary Science Conference (LPSC 2026), The Woodlands, TX, March 16–20, 2026; Abstract #1681
  - **Relevance:** Describes the Artemis II Science Officers program, science objectives for the lunar flyby, and the framework for CLO (Crew Lunar Observations). Provides the scientific rationale for targeting specific surface features including crater rims, ejecta blankets, and the far-side crust during the April 6 flyby.
  - **URL:** [Artemis II Lunar Science and Operations — LPSC 2026 Abstract #1681](https://www.hou.usra.edu/meetings/lpsc2026/pdf/1681.pdf)

- **Title:** Artemis II Science Data Management and Archiving
  - **Authors:** A. N. Deutsch et al.
  - **Source:** 57th Lunar and Planetary Science Conference (LPSC 2026), Abstract #1583
  - **Relevance:** Describes the data management pipeline for Artemis II science data — CLO imagery, biomedical samples, radiation dosimetry — including how data will be archived, calibrated, and made accessible to the planetary science community for downstream peer-reviewed publications.
  - **URL:** [Artemis II Science Data Management — LPSC 2026 Abstract #1583](https://www.hou.usra.edu/meetings/lpsc2026/pdf/1583.pdf)

- **Title:** Artemis II Lunar Observations Support Software
  - **Authors:** CLO Team, NASA
  - **Source:** NASA Technical Reports Server (NTRS 20260001932) / LPSC 2026 poster
  - **Relevance:** Documents the Lunar Targeting Plan (LTP) software used by the crew during the April 6 flyby — integrating mission planning, target selection, simulated views, and in-flight observation prompts. This software enabled the scientifically targeted imaging of Vavilov Crater and other far-side features.
  - **URL:** [Artemis II Lunar Observations Support Software — NTRS 20260001932](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20260001932)

- **Title:** Wyss Institute Technologies Enable Breakthrough in Astronaut Health Research Aboard NASA's Artemis II Mission *(pre-mission announcement, results pending)*
  - **Authors:** Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering, Harvard University
  - **Source:** GlobeNewswire / Wyss Institute press release, April 9, 2026
  - **Relevance:** Announces deployment of Bone Marrow Organ Chips derived from each crew member's own cells, flown alongside the crew to study deep-space radiation and microgravity effects on hematopoiesis. Full comparative analysis vs. ground controls is now underway post-recovery.
  - **URL:** [Wyss Institute Breakthrough — GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/09/3271182/0/en/Wyss-Institute-Technologies-Enable-Breakthrough-in-Astronaut-Health-Research-Aboard-NASA-s-Artemis-II-Mission.html)
