# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-07 21:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is on Flight Day 7, well into its trans-Earth coast after completing the historic lunar flyby on April 6. Orion exited the lunar sphere of influence at ~17:25 UTC today (~41,072 miles from the Moon), and Return Trajectory Correction Burn 1 (TCB-1) is scheduled for approximately 01:03 UTC on April 8 (9:03 p.m. EDT tonight). A new NASA blog covering the crew's long-distance calls with family and the official start of the return phase was published this evening; an iconic "Earthset" photo captured by the crew is now public.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Active crewed mission — Flight Day 7 of 10; trans-Earth coast; lunar sphere of influence exit confirmed; TCB-1 imminent
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC, Launch Complex 39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026 at ~00:07 UTC (~8:07 p.m. EDT / 5:07 p.m. PDT April 9), Pacific Ocean ~50–80 miles off San Diego; recovery vessel **USS John P. Murtha (LPD 26)**
- **Notable Changes Since 20:00 UTC Report:**
  - TCB-1 timing clarified: scheduled for ~01:03 UTC April 8 (9:03 p.m. EDT), not yet executed as of 21:00 UTC
  - New NASA blog published: "Artemis II Flight Day 7: Crew Makes Long‑Distance Call, Begins Return"
  - Historic "Earthset" photo released — crew photographed Earth sinking below the lunar horizon
  - All systems nominal; crew currently in off-duty rest period ahead of TCB-1

### Technical & Engineering

- **Lunar sphere of influence exit — confirmed (~17:25 UTC, April 7):** Orion crossed the ~41,072-mile boundary from the Moon, transitioning to Earth-dominant gravity. All telemetry nominal.
- **Return Trajectory Correction Burn 1 (TCB-1) — scheduled ~01:03 UTC April 8 (9:03 p.m. EDT):** This is the first of up to three burns to fine-tune the return path. TCB-2 and TCB-3 are planned for April 8–9. Splashdown remains GO for April 10.
- **Total mission distance (as of Flight Day 7):** Orion is on a ~695,081-mile round-trip trajectory around the Moon and back. The crew broke the human spaceflight distance record on April 6 at 252,756 miles (406,771 km) from Earth, also reaching ~4,700 miles (7,600 km) beyond the Moon.
- **Entry interface (April 10):** Orion targeting reentry at ~25,000 mph (~40,000 km/h) — highest-velocity crewed reentry ever attempted. Post-splashdown heat shield inspection planned.
- **Wastewater/toilet system:** Partial workaround remains in effect (contingency collapsible urinal devices / CCUs). Non-mission-impacting.

### Science & Instruments

- **CLO science debrief complete (Flight Day 7 morning):** All 35 geological observations from the April 6 flyby — far-side feature identification, color variation mapping, and 57 minutes of solar corona imagery — have been downlinked and reviewed with JSC lunar science teams. Highlights include observations of the Orientale basin (~600-mile-wide, 3.8 billion-year-old impact crater straddling the near/far side boundary).
- **Solar corona imagery:** During the lunar eclipse transit on April 6, the crew imaged the solar corona and monitored for meteoroid impact flashes; data is now under analysis by scientists at Mission Control.
- **AVATAR (organ-on-a-chip):** Nominal; bone-marrow organ-chips from each crew member's own cells continue collecting deep-space radiation exposure data in the final phase of the mission as Orion approaches Earth's magnetosphere.
- **HERA/CAD dosimetry:** Six Hybrid Electronic Radiation Assessors and Crew Active Dosimeters continue recording accumulated dose; peak deep-space exposure phase now complete post-flyby. DLR M-42 EXT also nominal.
- **Space Weather AI model (University of Michigan):** AI-based solar radiation forecast model providing real-time crew radiation risk assessment; output informs Flight Day 8 radiation shelter drill planning.
  - [Providing the Artemis mission with solar radiation forecasts — Michigan Engineering News](https://news.engin.umich.edu/2026/04/providing-the-artemis-mission-with-solar-radiation-forecasts/)
- **CubeSats:** Space Weather CubeSat-1 (Saudi Arabia), ATENEA (Argentina/CONAE), TACHELES (Germany/DLR) — all operating nominally. K-RadCube (South Korea/KASA) — previously confirmed lost; no new status.

|---|---|---|---|

| **Victor Glover** | Pilot | NASA | First person of color to travel beyond LEO and near the Moon |

- All four crew members healthy; currently in off-duty rest period ahead of TCB-1 at ~01:03 UTC April 8.
- **Long-distance calls (Flight Day 7):** Crew made personal calls to family and friends during the return phase — the first such calls from beyond the Moon's sphere of influence since the Apollo era.
- **Trump presidential call (April 6, post-flyby):** President Trump congratulated the crew and invited all four to the White House/Oval Office upon their return.
  - [Artemis II crew caps historic lunar flyby with call from Trump — UPI](https://www.upi.com/Top_News/US/2026/04/07/Artemis-mission-Trump/3491775543573/)
- **Flight Day 8 (April 8):** Commander Wiseman and Pilot Glover second manual piloting demonstration; radiation storm shelter construction drill (first-ever with crew in deep space); Jeremy Hansen's third live CSA Q&A with Canadians.
