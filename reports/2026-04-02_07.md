# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-02 07:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is approximately 8.5 hours into flight with all primary spacecraft systems performing nominally. No new major developments have occurred since the 06:00 UTC report; the mission remains in a quiet pre-Trans-Lunar Injection (TLI) period. The previously reported toilet (waste management system) fault was fully resolved prior to this reporting window, and flight controllers continue their Day 2 system health reviews ahead of tonight's scheduled TLI burn.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** In-flight — Flight Day 2; pre-TLI system reviews and crew rest/preparation period
- **Launched:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35 UTC from LC-39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Current Orbit:** High Earth checkout orbit (~43,730 miles / ~70,400 km apogee)
- **TLI Burn Status:** On schedule for ~23:35 UTC tonight (~7:35 PM EDT); go/no-go decision by flight controllers pending
- **Splashdown Target:** April 11, 2026, Pacific Ocean off San Diego
- **Notable Changes:** No new changes since 06:00 UTC report; waste management anomaly confirmed resolved

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **Toilet fault confirmed resolved (prior to this window):** NASA published a blog update at ~05:30 UTC confirming crew and ground teams successfully troubleshot the Orion waste management system controller issue disclosed at the post-launch press conference. The system is fully operational. [NASA blog — April 2](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/02/artemis-ii-flight-update-crew-and-ground-teams-successfully-troubleshoot-orions-toilet/)

- **No new NASA blog posts** in the 06:00–07:00 UTC window. The Artemis blog and mission updates page show the toilet resolution post as the latest entry as of this report.

- **TLI go/no-go process ongoing:** Flight controllers at JSC continue reviewing system health data from Day 1 and the current checkout orbit phase. No "no-go" conditions have been reported. The formal go/no-go poll must occur before the burn window opens tonight.

- **Mission briefing schedule:** Daily mission status briefings from JSC resume following the TLI burn; the post-TLI press conference is expected around ~00:30 UTC on April 3. [NASA coverage page](https://www.nasa.gov/missions/artemis/artemis-2/nasa-sets-coverage-for-artemis-ii-moon-mission/)

### Technical & Engineering

**Current Spacecraft Status (All Nominal):**
- Four solar array wings (SAWs) generating full power; power subsystem nominal
- ECLSS life support systems delivering oxygen and drinking water from ESM tanks (first in-flight operational use with crew)
- Deep Space Network handover from Near Space Network completed at apogee; comm link stable
- All ESM main propulsion system engine checks complete ahead of TLI
- Orion reaction control system thrusters confirmed healthy post-proximity operations

**TLI Burn Profile (Scheduled — Not Yet Executed):**
- Engine: ESM orbital maneuvering system engine (~6,000 lbf thrust)
- Duration: ~6 minutes, 5 seconds
- Delta-V: ~900 mph added velocity
- Trajectory type: Multi-translunar injection (MTLI) — free-return figure-eight cislunar path
- Post-TLI coast to Moon: ~4 days
- This is the **last major propulsive event** of the mission; subsequent course corrections are minor Trajectory Correction Burns (TCBs) on Days 3–4

**Key Trajectory Parameters (Post-TLI):**
- Closest lunar approach (April 6): ~4,112 statute miles (~6,617 km) from the far-side surface
- Maximum distance from Earth: ~252,799 statute miles — exceeds Apollo 13's record of ~248,655 miles
- Reentry speed: ~25,000 mph; modified to a direct (steeper) descent angle to address Artemis I heat shield spalling findings

**SLS Program Status:**
Per NTRS 20250011679 (December 2025), hardware for Artemis III, IV, and V is in production. Following the February 2026 cancellation of the Exploration Upper Stage, NASA awarded a sole-source contract to ULA in March 2026 for Centaur V upper stages for Artemis IV and V.

### Science & Instruments

No new science instrument activations or data events in this reporting window. Systems remain in checkout or active data-collection mode:

- **O2O Optical Communications System:** In checkout; planned 260 Mbps 4K video downlink scheduled for April 6 lunar flyby. [NTRS 20250010065](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065)
- **AVATAR (bone marrow organ-on-chip):** Active; cells exposed to deep-space radiation beyond the Van Allen belts, with data downlinked continuously.
- **ARCHeR Wristbands:** Active on all four crew members; monitoring sleep, stress biomarkers, cognitive performance, and team dynamics.
- **Radiation Suite (HERA dosimeters + Crew Active Dosimeters):** All units nominal; recording trans-LEO dose rates as spacecraft ascends through the outer magnetosphere.
- **CubeSats (all four):** TACHELES, K-RadCube, ATENEA, and SHMS deployed and collecting data in elliptical orbits. No anomalies reported. [NTRS 20250005942](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942)
- **Lunar flyby science prep:** Science Evaluation Room at JSC staffed; geology guidance team assembled for April 6 flyby window. ~21% of the lunar far side will be sunlit at closest approach.

### Crew Updates

Crew is in a rest and preparation period ahead of the TLI burn. No new public communications since the previous reporting window.

- **Commander Reid Wiseman:** Overseeing pre-TLI systems reviews; will provide formal "ready for TLI" concurrence to Mission Control.
- **Pilot Victor Glover:** Completed first-ever manual Orion piloting session during Day 1 proximity operations (~70 min, to within ~30 ft of ICPS). Resting ahead of TLI monitoring duties. Will be first person of color to travel beyond low Earth orbit upon TLI.
- **Mission Specialist Christina Koch:** Performing ESM engine system readiness checks. Will be the first woman to travel beyond low Earth orbit.
- **Mission Specialist Jeremy Hansen (CSA):** On his first spaceflight; first Canadian and first non-U.S. citizen to participate in a lunar mission. First scheduled public Q&A from space remains on April 4. [CSA event](https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/events/2026/2026-04-04-artemis-ii-first-q-and-a-jeremy-hansen-live-from-space.asp)

### International Partners

**ESA:**
- ESM-2 propulsion, power, and life support all nominal. ESA engineers on console at ESTEC (Netherlands) and ESOC (Germany).
- Tonight's TLI burn will be the largest single propulsive event for ESA hardware since Artemis I reentry separation in December 2022.
- ESM life support (oxygen + water) is delivering crew consumables for the first time in the program's history.
- Future ESMs: ESM-3 delivered for Artemis III testing at KSC; ESM-4 at KSC; ESM-5 and ESM-6 in production at Airbus Bremen (planned delivery 2027–2028). [ESA Artemis II page](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Orion/Artemis_II)

**CSA:**
- CSA astronaut Jenni Gibbons (Hansen's backup) serving as CAPCOM at Mission Control Houston.
- Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney spoke with U.S. President Trump on launch day; official government statement published on Canada.ca.
- Three scheduled live crew video calls planned during the mission; dates to be confirmed post-TLI.

## Academic & Research Papers

- **Title:** Optimized Trajectory Correction Burn Placement for the NASA Artemis II Mission
  - **Authors:** David Woffinden, Randy Eckman, Shane Robinson (NASA JSC)
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20230000223 (AAS 23-062)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20230000223)
  - **Relevance:** Describes the TCB optimization methodology now being planned for execution on Days 3–4 post-TLI, accounting for GN&C performance margins, navigation uncertainty, crew schedule, and thruster selection.

- **Title:** Orion Artemis II Optical Communications Architecture, Implementation and Test Systems
  - **Authors:** NASA JSC / Glenn Research Center
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250010065](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065) — October 2025
  - **Relevance:** Detailed architecture of the O2O system (260 Mbps downlink, 20 Mbps uplink) currently in checkout; enables near-real-time 4K video from the Moon on April 6.

- **Title:** Hitching a Ride on History — Artemis II CubeSat Payloads
  - **Authors:** NASA MSFC / mission teams
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250005942](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942) — June 2025
  - **Relevance:** Documents the four deployed CubeSat payloads (TACHELES, K-RadCube, ATENEA, SHMS) and their science objectives; all confirmed active.

- **Title:** NASA's SLS Super Heavy-Lift Rocket in Process for Artemis II and Beyond
  - **Authors:** NASA MSFC SLS Team
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250011679](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250011679) — December 22, 2025
  - **Relevance:** Describes hardware production status for Artemis II through V; confirms RS-25 engine production certification and provides context for the EUS cancellation and Centaur V transition.

- **Title:** Deep Space Radiation Measurements and Crew Radiation Protection for the NASA Artemis Program
  - **Authors:** NASA JSC / Langley Research Center
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20250002757](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250002757) — March 2025
  - **Relevance:** Crew radiation storm shelter protocols and dose benchmarks; directly informing real-time dosimetry interpretation as crew ascends through the outer magnetosphere ahead of TLI.

- **Title:** Machine Learning-Based Radiation Forecasting for Deep Space Missions Using SDO Imagery
  - **Authors:** Gurav et al.
  - **Source:** [arXiv:2411.17703](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.17703) — November 2024
  - **Relevance:** Solar energetic particle forecasting applicable during trans-lunar coast and April 6 flyby.
