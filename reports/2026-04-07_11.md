# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-07 11:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is on Flight Day 7 of 10, continuing trans-Earth coast with all systems nominal. Key developments since the 07:00 UTC report include confirmation that the recovery vessel is **USS John P. Murtha (LPD 26)** — not USS San Diego as previously noted — and NASA is actively monitoring an approaching Southern California weather system that could affect splashdown conditions on April 10. President Trump's congratulatory call to the crew (placed on Flight Day 6 after the lunar flyby) and his Oval Office invitation have also received wide coverage today.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Active crewed mission — Flight Day 7 of 10; trans-Earth coast; all flyby and flyby science objectives complete
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC, Launch Complex 39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026 at ~00:07 UTC (~8:07 p.m. EDT April 9 / 5:07 p.m. PDT), Pacific Ocean ~50–80 miles off coast of San Diego; recovery vessel **USS John P. Murtha (LPD 26)**
- **Notable Changes Since 07:00 UTC Report:**
  - Recovery vessel confirmed as **USS John P. Murtha (LPD 26)** — prior reports citing "USS San Diego" were in error
  - NASA actively monitoring a potential thunderstorm system forecast for the San Diego coast late Friday night (April 10); current splashdown target remains ~8:07 p.m. EDT April 9 (before the storm)
  - President Trump's Flight Day 6 call to the crew confirmed and widely covered in today's media cycle
  - Lunar sphere of influence exit milestone (~17:25 UTC today) approaching
  - All systems continue nominal; no new anomalies

### Technical & Engineering

- **Lunar sphere of influence exit (upcoming today):** At approximately **17:25 UTC (1:25 p.m. EDT)**, Orion is scheduled to exit the lunar sphere of influence at a distance of **41,072 miles from the Moon**, transitioning to Earth-dominated gravitational pull. All trans-Earth coast telemetry continues nominal ahead of this milestone.
- **Recovery vessel correction — USS John P. Murtha (LPD 26):** The designated Artemis II recovery ship is the **USS John P. Murtha**, an amphibious transport dock based at Naval Base San Diego. MH-60S Sea Hawk helicopters from Helicopter Sea Combat Squadron (HSC) 23 will track the capsule through reentry, recover astronauts after splashdown, and bring them aboard the ship. The recovery team deployed from Naval Base San Diego approximately 3–4 days before splashdown and will position within 24 hours of the recovery site before April 10.
  - [USS John P. Murtha to support NASA's Artemis II mission — U.S. Navy](https://www.navy.mil/Press-Office/News-Stories/display-news/Article/4452625/uss-john-p-murtha-to-support-nasas-artemis-ii-mission/)
  - [San Diego Warship to Recover Artemis II Astronauts After Splashdown](https://nationaltoday.com/us/ca/san-diego/news/2026/04/07/san-diego-warship-to-recover-artemis-ii-astronauts-after-splashdown/)
  - [San Diego warship will recover Artemis II astronauts — NBC 7 San Diego](https://www.nbcsandiego.com/news/local/san-diego-warship-recover-artemis-ii-astronauts-splashdown-off-coast-friday/4006242/)
- **Weather monitoring for splashdown:** NASA is actively tracking an approaching storm system. Splashdown weather requirements: no rain or thunderstorms within 30 nautical miles of the recovery site, wave height ≤6 ft, winds ≤25 knots. Current forecasts show rain and potential thunderstorms arriving late Friday night (~23:00 local time), after the planned 5:07 p.m. PDT splashdown. Mission controllers will determine final splashdown coordinates closer to April 10 based on updated forecasts.
  - [NASA monitors weather for Artemis II's Pacific splashdown off San Diego](https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/nasa-monitors-weather-artemis-ii-215136395.html)
  - [Artemis II splashdown: NASA monitors weather — Fox 5 San Diego](https://fox5sandiego.com/weather/nasa-monitors-weather-for-artemis-iis-pacific-splashdown-off-san-diego/)
- **Entry velocity:** Orion is targeting entry interface at ~25,000 mph (~40,000 km/h) — the highest-velocity crewed reentry ever attempted — with post-recovery heat shield inspection planned.
- **Wastewater/toilet system:** Partial fix remains in effect; crew continues use of contingency collapsible urinals (CCUs). Issue remains non-mission-impacting.
- **Flight Day 8 piloting demo:** Commander Reid Wiseman and Pilot Victor Glover are scheduled to conduct their manual piloting demonstration on Flight Day 8 (April 8), repeating the demonstration completed by Koch and Hansen on Flight Day 4 to give engineers additional data across crew perspectives.
  - [Artemis II Flight Day 4: Crew Completes Manual Piloting Demonstration](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/04/artemis-ii-flight-day-4-crew-completes-manual-piloting-demonstration/)

### Science & Instruments

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO) — science debrief underway:** The crew is debriefing with JSC lunar science teams on Flight Day 7 covering all 35 geological observations made during the flyby, Earthrise photography, and corona imagery. This debrief is scheduled for NASA live broadcast.
- **Solar corona imagery:** 57 minutes of solar corona observations from the April 6–7 total solar eclipse (00:35–01:32 UTC) represent the longest crewed corona observations ever made beyond Earth orbit. Solar physics teams at JSC received live audio descriptions from the crew during totality.
- **AVATAR investigation:** The organ-on-a-chip AVATAR experiment continues nominal operation. Radiation exposure measurements from the deep-space transit are near complete as Orion re-enters the Earth's magnetosphere zone over the coming days.
- **HERA/CAD radiation monitoring:** Six Hybrid Electronic Radiation Assessors (HERAs) and Crew Active Dosimeters (CADs) continue recording. The peak deep-space radiation exposure phase has ended; data will be analyzed post-splashdown alongside Germany's DLR M-42 EXT sensor readings.
- **Optical Communications (O2O):** Laser communications experiment continues operating nominally in trans-Earth cruise.
- **NASA budget context:** CNN reported on April 5 that the Trump administration had proposed significant NASA budget cuts. NASA Administrator Isaacman confirmed Artemis II would proceed as planned despite those broader budgetary discussions.
  - [Artemis II is about to make history. Trump recommends slashing NASA's budget — CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/05/science/nasa-budget-trump-proposed-cuts)
