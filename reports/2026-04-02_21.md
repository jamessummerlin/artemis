# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-02 21:00 UTC

## Summary

Flight controllers at NASA Johnson Space Center have polled "Go" for the Trans-Lunar Injection (TLI) burn, now approximately 2.5 hours away (~23:35 UTC). Orion *Integrity* and all four crew members remain in good health in high Earth orbit (~43,760-mile apogee), and all spacecraft systems are confirmed nominal ahead of the mission's most critical maneuver. A newly published MIT Lincoln Laboratory article confirms the O2O laser communications terminal is performing as expected on orbit following deployment.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** In-flight — Flight Day 2, TLI confirmed "GO"; burn at ~23:35 UTC
- **Launched:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35 UTC (6:35 PM EDT) from LC-39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Current Orbit:** High Earth checkout orbit; apogee ~43,760 miles (70,403 km)
- **TLI Burn:** ~23:35 UTC tonight; ~6 min 5 sec ESM main engine firing; commits crew to free-return cislunar trajectory
- **Splashdown Target:** ~April 10–11, 2026, Pacific Ocean off San Diego
- **Notable Changes:** No changes from prior report; go/no-go poll has now formally concluded "GO"

### Technical & Engineering

- **TLI burn (imminent, ~23:35 UTC):**
  - Engine: ESM orbital maneuvering system main engine (~6,000 lbs thrust)
  - Burn duration: ~6 minutes, 5 seconds
  - Delta-V: ~900 mph added velocity; escapes Earth orbit and enters free-return figure-eight cislunar trajectory
  - No Lunar Orbit Insertion required; crew returns to Earth on free-return path
  - Post-TLI: ~4-day coast to the Moon; DSN assumes primary comm; minor trajectory correction burns (TCBs) planned for Days 3–4

- **Space Daily analysis:** "The Critical Burn: How Artemis 2's Translunar Injection Commits Four Astronauts to the Moon" — published April 2; notes the abort options that close after TLI and the significance of the free-return trajectory for crew safety. [SpaceDaily](https://spacedaily.com/sd-n-the-critical-burn-how-artemis-2s-translunar-injection-commits-four-astronauts-to-the-moon/)

- **Heat shield (direct-entry) profile:** Flight Day 2 trajectory data confirmed within parameters for the steeper direct-entry reentry replacing the Artemis I skip-entry. Approved at the March 12 Flight Readiness Review. [Phys.org](https://phys.org/news/2026-03-shield-safety-stakes-nasa-artemis.html)

- **SLS architecture (program-level):** SLS Block 1B and Block 2 development canceled February 26, 2026. Artemis II through V all use Block 1. Mobile Launcher 2 development canceled. A new second stage solution is under assessment for Artemis IV+. [NASASpaceFlight](https://www.nasaspaceflight.com/2026/03/artemis-course-correction-moonward-details/) | [SpaceNews](https://spacenews.com/nasa-revises-plans-for-future-artemis-missions-cancels-upgrades-to-sls/)

### Science & Instruments

- **O2O laser communications — MIT Lincoln Laboratory update (new, April 2, 2026):** MIT Lincoln Laboratory published a feature article confirming their laser communications terminal is aboard and operating on orbit. The O2O payload provides up to 260 Mbps downlink, enabling near-real-time 4K video streaming during and after the lunar flyby — a generational leap beyond the S-band radio systems of the Apollo era. The terminal was developed in collaboration with NASA's Space Communications and Navigation (SCaN) program. [MIT News — O2O launch feature](https://news.mit.edu/2026/lincoln-laboratory-laser-communications-terminal-launches-artemis-ii-0402) | [MIT Lincoln Laboratory](https://www.ll.mit.edu/news/lincoln-laboratory-laser-communications-terminal-launches-historic-artemis-ii-moon-mission)

- **AVATAR experiment status:** AVATAR (A Virtual Astronaut Tissue Analog Response) organ-chip devices — miniaturized bone marrow avatars grown from the crew's own cells — are recording baseline data pre-TLI. These chips will experience the same deep-space radiation and microgravity as the crew during the Van Allen belt passage and cislunar transit. The Planetary Society featured a podcast segment on AVATAR and a sungrazing comet coinciding with the mission. [NASA AVATAR](https://science.nasa.gov/biological-physical/investigations/avatar/) | [The Planetary Society](https://www.planetary.org/planetary-radio/2026-avatar-and-sungrazer)

- **Radiation instruments:** Van Allen belt dosimetry is expected to spike sharply during TLI. All radiation sensors nominal; data feeding directly into dosimetry models for Artemis III/IV crew planning.

- **CubeSat science:** Four international 12U CubeSats (DLR TACHELES, KASA K-RadCube, Saudi Space Agency, CONAE ATENEA) deployed Flight Day 1 and transmitting science data to their respective ground stations. [NTRS 20250005942](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942)

- **Lunar observation prep:** Crew reviewed lunar observation checklists for April 6 closest approach (~6,513 km from far-side surface), including geological survey targets identified in pre-mission science planning. [NASA SVS Artemis II Flight Path](https://svs.gsfc.nasa.gov/20412/)
