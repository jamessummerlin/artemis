# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-08 16:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is nominal on Flight Day 8, cruising through trans-Earth coast with splashdown on track for April 10 at ~00:07 UTC off San Diego. The most significant new technical finding this cycle is expanded coverage of the Orion Artemis II Optical Communications (O2O) laser link, which has downlinked over 100 GB of data — including first-ever 4K UHD video from deep space — at up to 260 Mbps, validated by two newly surfaced NTRS technical papers. Additional lunar flyby science details have also emerged: the crew observed six meteoroid impact flashes on the lunar surface and experienced a 54-minute total solar eclipse during the April 6 flyby. All major FD8 evening events (PM Carney/Hansen call, radiation shelter drill, crew media call, manual piloting demo) remain scheduled and have not yet occurred as of this report.

### Technical & Engineering

- **O2O Laser Communications System (new technical details this cycle):** The Orion Artemis II Optical Communications (O2O) system has surpassed all previous deep-space data rates:
  - **Downlink:** up to 260 Mbps (vs. ~0.8 Mbps for prior S-band radio links)
  - **Uplink:** up to 20 Mbps
  - **Volume:** over 100 GB downlinked by mission day 4
  - **Milestone:** first-ever 4K UHD video transmitted from deep space
  - **Hardware:** ~cat-sized terminal with 4-inch telescope and two-axis gimbal, modem, and flight computer interface; developed over 20+ years by NASA Goddard and MIT Lincoln Laboratory
  - **Ground stations:** NASA White Sands Complex (New Mexico) and Table Mountain Facility (California)
  - Two NTRS technical papers on O2O now confirmed (NTRS 20250009239, 20250009875)
  - [NASA's Artemis II laser communications system is beaming 4K video from the moon — Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/nasas-artemis-ii-laser-communications-system-is-beaming-4k-video-from-the/)
  - [Lincoln Laboratory laser communications terminal launches on Artemis II — MIT News](https://news.mit.edu/2026/lincoln-laboratory-laser-communications-terminal-launches-artemis-ii-0402)
  - [How O2O Lasercom is Redefining Deep-Space Data on Artemis II — R&D World](https://www.rdworldonline.com/the-photon-frontier-how-o2o-lasercom-is-redefining-deep-space-data-on-artemis-ii/)
- **Second bathroom issue (ongoing):** Urine overboard vent nozzle remains partially blocked (ice/chemical debris suspected); contingency collapsible urinal devices (CCUs) in use; non-critical.
- **ESM-2 (European Service Module):** Fully nominal; performance described as "above expectations." 33 engines and four 7-meter solar arrays operating without anomaly. ESA engineers on console at ESTEC (Netherlands) and EAC (Cologne) around the clock.
  - [Airbus — Artemis II lifts off: destination Moon with the Orion spacecraft](https://www.airbus.com/en/newsroom/stories/2026-04-artemis-ii-lifts-off-destination-moon-with-the-orion-spacecraft)
- **Splashdown weather (updated):** Favorable. Light rain not expected over recovery zone until ~11 PM PDT Friday (well after 5:07 PM PDT splashdown). Contingency site near Guadalupe Island (west of Baja California) on standby but not needed.
  - [Artemis II splashdown weather forecast — Fox Weather](https://www.foxweather.com/weather-news/artemis-ii-splashdown-light-rain-possible-recovery-zone)
- **TCB-2 and TCB-3:** Second and final trajectory correction burns scheduled April 9; no specific delta-V or times confirmed yet.

### Science & Instruments

- **Lunar flyby science — additional details confirmed:**
  - **Six meteoroid impact flashes** observed on the lunar surface during the April 6 flyby — the first time such flashes have been observed from a crewed spacecraft in lunar vicinity
  - **54-minute total solar eclipse:** Orion passed through Earth's umbra/penumbra alignment, providing a 54-minute solar eclipse from the crew's vantage point; crew captured the solar corona with Nikon D5 cameras
  - **Earthset / earthrise imagery:** Crew photographed Earth setting behind and rising above the lunar limb; imagery over Ohm crater (far side) is among the highest-resolution such photos from crewed flight
  - **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** All 35 planned flyby observations completed; post-mission analysis continuing at JSC; no new published results yet
  - [ScienceAlert — Artemis II crew sends back stunning images from far side of the Moon](https://www.sciencealert.com/the-artemis-ii-crew-has-sent-back-stunning-images-from-the-far-side-of-the-moon)
  - [PBS NewsHour — Photos: Artemis II captures Earthset from moon's far side](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/science/photos-boundary-breaking-artemis-ii-captures-view-of-earthset-from-moons-far-side)
- **Solar radiation forecasting:** University of Michigan / NASA machine-learning SEP (solar energetic particle) prediction models continue real-time validation; no solar particle events requiring action on April 8.
  - [Artemis II to test new models that predict solar particle storms — Phys.org](https://phys.org/news/2026-04-artemis-ii-solar-particle-storms.html)
- **AVATAR, ARCHER, HERA/CAD dosimetry:** All bio-medical and radiation monitoring experiments continuing nominal data collection through trans-Earth coast.
- **CubeSat status (clarification):** Four 12U CubeSats ejected from the Orion Stage Adapter into highly elliptical Earth orbit:
  - TACHELES (Germany) — operational
  - ATENEA (Argentina) — operational
  - SHAMS / Space Weather CubeSat-1 (Saudi Arabia) — operational
  - K-RadCube (South Korea) — **lost** post-deployment (contact not established)

## Academic & Research Papers

- **Title:** Orion Artemis II Optical Communications (O2O) — Technical Overview
  - **Authors:** NASA Goddard Space Flight Center / MIT Lincoln Laboratory team
  - **Source:** NASA Technical Reports Server (NTRS 20250009239; companion paper NTRS 20250009875)
  - **Relevance:** Documents the O2O laser communications terminal performance: 260 Mbps downlink, 20 Mbps uplink, 4K UHD video from deep space. First operational deep-space laser comms on a crewed mission. Foundation for future Artemis and lunar gateway communications architecture.
  - **URLs:** https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250009239 | https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250009875

- **Title:** Artemis II Lunar Observations Support Software (CLO Lunar Targeting Plan)
  - **Authors:** Crew Lunar Observations Team / Artemis II Lunar Science Team, NASA Goddard Space Flight Center
  - **Source:** NASA Technical Reports Server (NTRS 20260001932); LPSC 2026 poster
  - **Relevance:** Documents the ground-support software and structured observation plan enabling the 35 scientific lunar flyby observations; first systematic human lunar science targeting in 50+ years.
  - **URL:** https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20260001932

- **Title:** Solar Particle Storm Forecasting Models for Artemis II
  - **Authors:** University of Michigan Space Physics Research Lab / NASA collaboration
  - **Source:** Phys.org; University of Michigan news release (April 2026)
  - **Relevance:** Machine-learning and physics-based models predicting solar energetic particle events up to 24 hours ahead are being validated in real time — first live deep-space crewed validation. Directly relevant to crew radiation safety and future mission planning.
  - **URL:** https://phys.org/news/2026-04-artemis-ii-solar-particle-storms.html

- **Title:** NASA's SLS Super Heavy-Lift Rocket in Process for Artemis II and Beyond (NTRS 20250011679)
  - **Authors:** Bruce R. Askins, NASA SLS Program
  - **Source:** NASA Technical Reports Server; JANNAF 2025 proceedings
  - **Relevance:** Pre-launch SLS engineering status document; covers vehicle integration, Artemis II readiness, and Artemis III/IV production timelines.
  - **URL:** https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250011679

- **Title:** Hitching a Ride on History: NASA's SLS Artemis II CubeSats (NTRS 20250005942)
  - **Authors:** NASA Team
  - **Source:** NASA Technical Reports Server; USU SmallSat 2025 conference
  - **Relevance:** Documents four 12U CubeSats (TACHELES/Germany, ATENEA/Argentina, SHAMS/Saudi Arabia, K-RadCube/South Korea) deployed from Orion Stage Adapter into highly elliptical Earth orbit. K-RadCube contact lost.
  - **URL:** https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942

- **Title:** "The Unbearable Lightness of Artemis II and Our Future Among the Stars" (commentary)
  - **Authors:** Avi Loeb (Harvard University)
  - **Source:** Medium, April 8, 2026
  - **Relevance:** Academic commentary from a prominent astrophysicist on Artemis II's scientific and philosophical significance; not peer-reviewed but notable as scholarly public discourse.
  - **URL:** https://avi-loeb.medium.com/the-unbearable-lightness-of-artemis-ii-and-our-future-among-the-stars-f55c7326b82d
