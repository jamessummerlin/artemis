# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-14 16:00 UTC

## Summary

The primary new development this cycle is NASA's confirmation that the Artemis III SLS core stage (the top four-fifths of the rocket) will be rolled out of Michoud Assembly Facility on April 20, 2026, marking the first major Artemis III hardware milestone following Artemis II's successful conclusion. The Orion program manager has formally confirmed a 30-day post-splashdown heat shield inspection period culminating in a report, and NASA has now confirmed that heat shield data from Artemis II will directly influence the Artemis IV timeline. The April 16 postflight crew news conference at JSC (2:30 p.m. EDT) remains the most significant near-term public event.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — crew reconditioning (Day 4), engineering inspections, and science debriefs; Artemis III hardware integration underway
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC; Splashdown: April 10, 2026 at ~00:07 UTC
- **Mission Duration:** ~9 days, 1 hour, 31 minutes
- **Notable Changes:**
  - Crew is in Day 4 of standard postflight reconditioning at JSC
  - April 16 postflight crew news conference confirmed (2:30 p.m. EDT, JSC); NASA TV / YouTube livestream
  - Orion program manager confirms formal 30-day heat shield inspection period with final report; inspections began at recovery ship
  - Artemis III SLS core stage rollout from Michoud confirmed for April 20, 2026 — first major Artemis III production milestone
  - Lunar Gateway programme officially paused/cancelled in March 2026; focus shifted to $20 billion lunar surface base programme

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **Postflight news conference — April 16, 2:30 p.m. EDT:** All four crew members (Wiseman, Glover, Koch, Hansen) will speak at JSC; NASA TV / YouTube livestream. Media credentialing closed April 14.
  - [NASA to Host Artemis II Crew Postflight News Conference — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-to-host-artemis-ii-crew-postflight-news-conference/)
- **Artemis III SLS core stage rollout — April 20:** NASA will roll the top four-fifths of the Artemis III SLS core stage out of Michoud Assembly Facility (New Orleans) on April 20 and load it onto the Pegasus barge for delivery to KSC. This section contains the liquid hydrogen tank, liquid oxygen tank, intertank, and forward skirt. The four RS-25 engines are scheduled to ship from Stennis Space Center no later than July 2026 for integration into the engine section.
  - [NASA Invites Media to Rollout Event for Artemis III Moon Rocket Stage — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-invites-media-to-rollout-event-for-artemis-iii-moon-rocket-stage/)
  - [Experience the Rollout of SLS Hardware for Artemis III — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/general/experience-the-rollout-of-sls-hardware-for-artemis-iii/)
- **Artemis II news and updates hub** continues to aggregate postflight coverage.
  - [Artemis II News and Updates — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/artemis-ii-news-and-updates/)

### Technical & Engineering

- **Heat shield inspection — 30-day period confirmed:** The Orion program manager has confirmed a 30-day post-splashdown inspection period culminating in a formal engineering report on heat shield performance. Two NASA heat shield experts were aboard the recovery ship and began physical inspections before the capsule was transported to KSC. Divers photographed the heat shield in-water before recovery. Detailed thermal scans are planned at KSC. NASA states that the loft-reentry data will be "crucial in deciding future moon mission timelines" — directly linking Artemis IV's schedule to this inspection outcome.
  - [Heat shield data from Artemis II could define Artemis IV timeline — KTVQ](https://www.ktvq.com/science-and-tech/space/heat-shield-data-from-artemis-ii-could-define-artemis-iv-timeline)
  - [NASA confident Artemis 2 heat shield will protect crew during re-entry — Spaceflight Now](https://spaceflightnow.com/2026/04/10/nasa-confident-artemis-ii-heat-shield-will-protect-crew-during-re-entry/)
- **Artemis III hardware at KSC:** SRB aft skirt segments arrived at KSC rail yard on April 8 and are being transferred to the Rotation, Processing and Surge Facility (RPSF) for processing. The Artemis III Orion ESM engine nozzle is being installed inside the Neil A. Armstrong Operations and Checkout Building.
  - [Artemis III: More Hardware Starting To Arrive At KSC — TalkOfTitusville.com](https://talkoftitusville.com/2026/04/13/artemis-iii-more-hardware-starting-to-arrive-at-ksc/)
- **Artemis III mission profile — revised (background):** Confirmed as a mid-2027 crewed LEO mission to conduct Orion rendezvous and docking tests with SpaceX Starship HLS and/or Blue Origin Blue Moon; no lunar landing. SpaceX and Blue Origin are competing to have their landers human-rated for Artemis IV (~2028), the first crewed lunar landing.
  - [Artemis III will practice docking Orion with lunar landers in Earth orbit — Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/04/12/artemis-iii-docking-orion-lunar-landers-musk-starship-bezos-blue-moon-artemis-iv/)

### Science & Instruments

- **Meteoroid impact flash cross-referencing ongoing:** Ground scientists led by lunar science lead Kelsey Young continue matching the six crew-observed impact flash events (April 6) with data from lunar orbital satellites (likely LRO). Unistellar citizen-science network also mobilized telescopes during the flyby window to independently record any observable flashes from Earth.
  - [Lunar Flashes with Artemis II — Unistellar Citizen Science](https://science.unistellar.com/planetary-defense/missions/lunar-flashes-with-artemis-ii/)
  - [Impact Flash! — NASA Science citizen science program](https://science.nasa.gov/citizen-science/impact-flash/)
- **CLO image archive and far-side science:** Crew Lunar Observations (CLO) team continues processing 10,000+ images. Comparative analysis of Orientale Basin vs. Hertzsprung Basin is underway among planetary scientists.
- **ARCHeR and AVATAR bio-sample analysis:** Bio-behavioral and health data from the ARCHeR framework and crew biological samples (AVATAR program) are in active analysis at JSC.
  - [PROJECT ARCHeR: Artemis II Update — NTRS 20260002430](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20260002430)
  - [Artemis II Lunar Observations Support Software — NTRS 20260001932](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20260001932)

### Crew Updates

- **Day 4 postflight reconditioning:** Crew in standard JSC postflight protocols — cardiovascular, vestibular, and musculoskeletal evaluations; human performance assessments; science team debriefs. Expected 21–45 total days of reconditioning, with return to light duties in 2–3 weeks.
  - [From Deepest Space to Doctor's Orders: What Happens to the Artemis II Crew Now — Orbital Today](https://orbitaltoday.com/2026/04/14/from-deepest-space-to-doctors-orders-what-happens-to-the-artemis-ii-crew-now/)
- **White House visit:** President Trump's Oval Office invitation remains unconfirmed with no scheduled date. The prior Oval Office call during the mission (in lunar orbit) is documented on the White House website.
  - [From the Oval Office to Orbit — The White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/videos/from-the-oval-office-to-orbit-president-donald-j-trump-speaks-with-the-artemis-ii-astronauts/)
- **IAU crater names "Carroll" and "Integrity":** Proposed during flyby; broader international media coverage continues. IAU ruling anticipated ~May 2026.

### International Partners

- **ESA ESM postflight assessment:** ESA and Airbus Defence and Space continue compiling ESM performance data. Precision of the trans-lunar injection burn cancelled two planned trajectory correction burns. The helium pressurant valve leak rate was higher than expected in flight; NASA confirms design modifications will be required for Artemis IV's more demanding propulsion profile. The ESM for Artemis III is currently in hardware processing at KSC.
  - [ESA — Splashdown for Artemis II](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Artemis_II_splashdown)
  - [Artemis II relied on European science: what that means for the region's space ambitions — Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01013-w)
- **CSA — Jeremy Hansen coverage:** Canadian media continue following Hansen's reconditioning status and the CSA's Artemis II contribution.
- **Lunar Gateway cancelled — international partner impact:** The March 24 cancellation of the Lunar Gateway by NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman (confirmed at a NASA HQ event) has significant implications for ESA, JAXA, and CSA, who had hardware commitments to Gateway. NASA plans to repurpose Gateway hardware (Power and Propulsion Element, HALO) toward a $20 billion lunar surface base programme over seven years. ESA faces potential loss of its ESPRIT module investment.
  - [NASA halts work on Gateway to develop a lunar base — SpaceNews](https://spacenews.com/nasa-halts-work-on-gateway-to-develop-a-lunar-base/)
  - [NASA to spend $20 billion on moon base, cancel orbiting lunar station — CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/24/nasa-to-spend-20-billion-on-moon-base-cancel-orbiting-lunar-station.html)
  - [NASA's Pause on Gateway Lunar Orbiter Leaves Allies in Lurch — Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-04-01/nasa-s-pause-on-gateway-lunar-orbiter-leaves-allies-in-lurch/)
