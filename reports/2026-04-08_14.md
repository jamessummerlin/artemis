# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-08 14:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is in the early hours of Flight Day 8, with the crew aboard Orion "Integrity" cruising home on a nominal trans-Earth trajectory following the successful TCB-1 burn completed at 01:03 UTC April 8. All four crew members are healthy and active; the major Flight Day 8 milestones — radiation storm shelter construction drill, second manual piloting demonstration, JSC daily press conference (3:30 PM ET), and live crew media call (9:45 PM ET) — are all scheduled for later this afternoon/evening Eastern time, with splashdown remaining firmly on target for April 10 at approximately 00:07 UTC (~8:07 PM EDT / 5:07 PM PDT April 9) off San Diego.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Active crewed mission — Flight Day 8 of 10; nominal trans-Earth coast; TCB-1 complete; major FD8 activities upcoming
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC, Launch Complex 39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026 at ~00:07 UTC (~8:07 p.m. EDT / 5:07 p.m. PDT April 9), Pacific Ocean ~50–80 miles off San Diego; recovery vessel **USS John P. Murtha (LPD 26)**
- **Notable Changes Since 13:00 UTC Report:**
  - No anomalies reported; all systems nominal heading into Flight Day 8 activity block
  - 3:30 PM ET JSC daily press conference confirmed with NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman expected to participate
  - 9:45 PM ET crew media call confirmed — all four crew members will take questions live on NASA TV/YouTube
  - USS John P. Murtha confirmed en route to Pacific recovery zone; sailors trained on recovery procedures
  - CSA astronaut Jenni Gibbons' first official Artemis capcom shift confirmed; cited April 8 as "payoff day" watching crew make history

### Technical & Engineering

- **TCB-1 — COMPLETE (01:03 UTC April 8):** Confirmed nominal; 15-second thruster firing, 1.6 ft/s ΔV. Trajectory nominal for Earth reentry corridor.
- **Radiation storm shelter construction drill (scheduled ~00:15 UTC April 9 / ~8:15 PM ET April 8):** Crew will construct improvised radiation shelter using stowage and equipment around the central bay — barricading themselves between the heat shield and supply stacks — to assess emergency radiation-event procedures for future lunar surface missions. First-ever execution by a crew in deep space.
  - [Artemis II and the invisible hazard on the way to the Moon — ANSTO](https://www.ansto.gov.au/news/artemis-ii-and-invisible-hazard-on-way-to-moon-part-1)
  - [To Protect Artemis II Astronauts, NASA Experts Keep Eyes on Sun — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/missions/artemis/artemis-2/to-protect-artemis-ii-astronauts-nasa-experts-keep-eyes-on-sun/)
- **Second manual piloting demonstration (scheduled ~01:59 UTC April 9 / ~9:59 PM ET April 8):** Commander Wiseman and Pilot Glover to take manual control, center a designated target in Orion's windows, return to tail-to-Sun attitude, and compare 6 degree-of-freedom (6-DOF) vs. 3 degree-of-freedom (3-DOF) thruster control modes. Data will inform future Orion human-interface requirements. Previous demo on Flight Day 4 was fully nominal.
  - [Artemis II Flight Day 4: Crew Completes Manual Piloting Demonstration — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/04/artemis-ii-flight-day-4-crew-completes-manual-piloting-demonstration/)
- **TCB-2 (scheduled April 9):** Second of three return trajectory correction burns to refine Orion's reentry corridor.
- **TCB-3 (scheduled April 9–10):** Final trajectory correction ahead of entry interface.
- **Entry interface (April 10):** Orion targeting reentry at ~25,000 mph (~40,000 km/h) — highest-velocity crewed reentry ever attempted. Modified steeper entry profile in place to manage AVCOAT heat shield thermal environment. All heat shield systems nominal.
- **USS John P. Murtha recovery ship:** Confirmed departed Naval Base San Diego on April 7; en route to Pacific recovery zone ~50–80 miles off San Diego. MH-60S Sea Hawk helicopters from HSC-23 "Wildcats" (NAS North Island) will track Orion through reentry and retrieve crew. Navy divers will recover Orion capsule.
  - [USS John P. Murtha to support NASA's Artemis II mission — U.S. Navy](https://www.navy.mil/Press-Office/News-Stories/display-news/Article/4452625/uss-john-p-murtha-to-support-nasas-artemis-ii-mission/)
  - [What to know about Artemis II crew's return to Earth, splashdown off San Diego — Fox 5 San Diego](https://fox5sandiego.com/news/local-news/san-diego/what-to-know-about-artemis-ii-crews-return-to-earth-splashdown-off-san-diego/)
- **ESA European Service Module (ESM-2):** Operating nominally; providing propulsion, power, thermal control, and life support through trans-Earth coast.
- **Wastewater/toilet system:** Partial workaround (contingency collapsible urinal devices / CCUs) still in effect; non-mission-impacting.

### Science & Instruments

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** All 35 observations from April 6 flyby now under analysis at JSC. Far-side imagery of features never before seen by human eyes being processed. NASA Scientific Visualization Studio published simulation of the flyby.
  - [Simulating the Artemis II Lunar Flyby on April 6, 2026 — NASA SVS](https://svs.gsfc.nasa.gov/5633)
  - [Artemis II astronauts see parts of the moon rarely seen by humans — NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/06/nx-s1-5775639/artemis-astronauts-moon-observation-nasa-human-eye)
- **Solar eclipse (lunar flyby):** During the April 6 flyby, Orion dipped into the Moon's shadow for 53 minutes, giving the crew an unprecedented view of the solar corona. Imagery and spectral data collected for post-mission analysis.
  - [Why Artemis II's lunar flyby will offer unprecedented views — CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/06/science/artemis-2-lunar-flyby-geology)
- **AVATAR (organ-on-a-chip):** Nominal; bone-marrow organ-chips continue collecting deep-space radiation exposure data through trans-Earth coast.
  - [AVATAR Science Investigation — NASA](https://science.nasa.gov/biological-physical/investigations/avatar/)
- **ARCHER (actigraphy):** Crew wristband monitors continue collecting sleep, circadian rhythm, and activity data.
- **HERA/CAD dosimetry:** Six Hybrid Electronic Radiation Assessors and Crew Active Dosimeters recording accumulated dose; crew re-entering Earth's magnetosphere and radiation environment transitioning.
- **Space Weather / Solar monitoring:** University of Michigan AI model providing real-time solar radiation risk assessment; no solar energetic particle events flagged as of 14:00 UTC.
- **Radiation storm shelter drill (upcoming, ~00:15 UTC April 9):** Will generate first-ever crew-executed deep-space radiation shelter data to inform emergency protocols for future Artemis missions.
  - [Artemis II: Deep-Space Human Flight, Radiation Exposure — AeroMorning](https://aeromorning.com/en/artemis-ii-deep-space-human-flight-radiation-exposure/)

- **Crater naming proposal:** Crew proposed naming two newly-observed lunar far-side craters — "Integrity" (Orion spacecraft name) and a memorial crater for Commander Wiseman's late wife Carroll. Subject to post-mission IAU nomination.
- **President Trump call (Flight Day 7):** First presidential call to crewed astronauts beyond LEO in over 50 years; crew invited to visit the Oval Office post-mission.
