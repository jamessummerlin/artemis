# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-13 04:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II completed its historic 10-day crewed lunar flyby on April 10, 2026, with splashdown in the Pacific Ocean off San Diego; all four crew members are healthy and in post-flight reconditioning at JSC (Day 3). No major new developments have emerged since the 02:00 UTC report: the Artemis III crew announcement and White House crew visit remain pending, while post-mission hardware analysis (Orion heat shield CT scans, AVATAR organ chips, CLO imagery) continues at KSC and JSC. NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman echoed AA Kshatriya's statement that the Artemis III crew will be announced "soon."

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — crew post-flight reconditioning, medical/human-performance evaluations, and lunar science debriefs at NASA Johnson Space Center (Day 3 post-splashdown)
- **Mission Duration:** 10 days (April 1–10, 2026); splashdown April 10 at 8:07 p.m. EDT (00:07 UTC April 11) in the Pacific Ocean ~50–80 mi off San Diego; recovered by USS *John P. Murtha* (LPD 26)
- **Notable Changes:**
  - No new schedule or architecture changes since post-splashdown press conference
  - Artemis III revised mission (mid-2027, LEO HLS rendezvous/docking + AxEMU spacesuit test) and Artemis IV (~2028, first crewed lunar landing) remain confirmed

### Technical & Engineering

- **Orion heat shield analysis (ongoing):** Orion capsule "Integrity" is at Kennedy Space Center undergoing detailed CT scanning of the AVCOAT ablative heat shield. Post-splashdown diver inspection found no unexpected anomalies. Full results have not been publicly released; findings will inform future Orion production batches now being manufactured with improved AVCOAT permeability uniformity.
  - Background: Artemis II used a modified (steeper, shorter skip) reentry trajectory rather than a redesigned heat shield to address the char-loss problem discovered after Artemis I. Initial indications suggest the modified trajectory successfully mitigated the issue.
  - [NASA Shares Orion Heat Shield Findings, Updates Artemis Moon Missions — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-shares-orion-heat-shield-findings-updates-artemis-moon-missions/)
  - [Artemis II's Last Test: Will Its Heat Shield Work? — National Geographic](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/science/article/artemis-ii-heat-shield-nasa)
  - [There's an Issue with the Artemis II Heat Shield, but NASA Isn't Worried — Live Science](https://www.livescience.com/space/space-exploration/theres-an-issue-with-the-artemis-ii-heat-shield-but-nasa-isnt-worried-heres-why)
- **Manual piloting data analysis (ongoing):** Data from two manual piloting demonstration sessions is being analyzed to refine Orion handling qualities for crew rendezvous/docking training ahead of Artemis III and IV.
- **Artemis III mission profile (confirmed):** Mid-2027 LEO rendezvous and docking demonstration with SpaceX Starship HLS and/or Blue Origin Blue Moon; includes AxEMU spacesuit evaluation. No lunar landing. Artemis IV (~2028) is the first crewed lunar landing.
  - [Artemis 3 and Beyond: What's Next for NASA After Artemis 2 Moon Success — Space.com](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/artemis/artemis-3-and-beyond-whats-next-for-nasa-after-artemis-2-moon-success)
- **CubeSats deployed:** Four international CubeSats carried aboard Artemis II from Artemis Accords signatories:
  - *TACHELES* (Germany): studies effects of space conditions on electrical components used in lunar vehicles
  - *ATENEA* (Argentina): radiation shielding study, radiation environment mapping, GPS data collection, long-distance communications test

### Science & Instruments

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO) — analysis underway:** 7,000+ high-resolution images from the ~7-hour lunar flyby (April 6; closest approach 4,067 miles / 6,545 km from far-side surface) are being processed at JSC. Highlights include Vavilov Crater within Hertzsprung Basin showing the transition between smooth interior materials and rugged ejecta. Crew color discrimination reports (green/brown mineral hues) are being cross-referenced with LRO and Kaguya remote sensing datasets.
  - [Artemis II Flight Day 6: Crew Wraps Historic Lunar Flyby — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/06/artemis-ii-flight-day-6-crew-wraps-historic-lunar-flyby/)
  - [New Images from Artemis II's Historic Lunar Flyby Reveal 'Earthset' and Eclipse — CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/07/science/artemis-2-lunar-flyby-images-earthset)
  - [Artemis II Crew Saw Parts of the Moon Never Seen Before — NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/07/nx-s1-5776824/artemis-astronauts-moon-observation-nasa-human-eye)
- **Distance record set:** 252,756 miles (406,771 km) from Earth on April 6, surpassing Apollo 13's record of 248,655 miles (400,171 km) set in 1970.
  - [NASA's Artemis II Crew Eclipses Record for Farthest Human Spaceflight — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasas-artemis-ii-crew-eclipses-record-for-farthest-human-spaceflight/)
- **Solar corona observation:** Crew used the solar eclipse (Moon blocking the Sun as seen from Orion during flyby) to study the solar corona and observe meteoroid impact flashes on the darkened lunar surface. Six distinct flashes were recorded; data is being analyzed to refine lunar impact hazard models.
- **AVATAR organ-on-a-chip:** Hardware recovered; Bone Marrow Chips (derived from each crew member's own cells) now at Wyss Institute (Harvard) for comparison against ground controls. Full analysis expected to take months. Focuses on hematopoietic cell response to deep-space radiation and microgravity.
  - [Wyss Institute Technologies Enable Breakthrough in Astronaut Health Research — Wyss Institute](https://wyss.harvard.edu/news/wyss-institute-technologies-enable-breakthrough-in-astronaut-health-research-aboard-nasas-artemis-ii-mission/)
  - [Artemis II Science — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/artemis-ii-science/)
- **Radiation dosimetry:** M-42 EXT European-designed radiation detectors (6× range of Artemis I instruments) show preliminary readings within ~10% of predicted safety margins. No significant solar energetic particle events occurred during the mission. Full dosimetry analysis ongoing.
- **Blood and saliva samples:** Pre-, in-, and post-flight samples are with JSC biomedical teams for immune and stress-response studies.

### International Partners

- **ESA (European Space Agency):** The European Service Module (ESM) powered the mission throughout, providing propulsion, power (four solar arrays), thermal control, and life support (air, potable water) over more than 1 million km of deep-space travel. ESM was assembled by Airbus Defence and Space in Bremen with contributions from 13 European countries and 100+ suppliers. Post-mission ESM telemetry engineering review is underway; no public findings yet.
  - [ESA — Splashdown for Artemis II](https://www.esa.int/Newsroom/Press_Releases/Splashdown_for_Artemis_II)
  - [ESA — Artemis II](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Orion/Artemis_II)
- **CSA (Canadian Space Agency):** Jeremy Hansen continues post-flight reconditioning and debriefs alongside the rest of the crew at JSC. No new CSA press releases since April 11.
  - [Artemis II Crew to Be Welcomed in Houston — Canada.ca (CSA)](https://www.canada.ca/en/space-agency/news/2026/04/artemis-ii-crew-to-be-welcomed-in-houston-after-a-historic-mission-around-the-moon.html)
