# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-13 05:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II remains in the post-mission phase; the crew is at Day 4 of post-flight reconditioning at JSC with no new public appearances or schedule announcements. The most significant development since the 04:00 UTC report is confirmation that the European Service Module's helium propulsion valve leak was approximately 10× (an order of magnitude) worse in flight than measured during ground testing — a finding that will drive an extensive valve redesign for Artemis IV. No Artemis III crew announcement or White House visit date has been confirmed.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — Day 4 crew post-flight reconditioning, medical/human-performance evaluations, and lunar science debriefs at NASA Johnson Space Center
- **Mission Duration:** 10 days (April 1–10, 2026); splashdown April 10 at 8:07 p.m. EDT (00:07 UTC April 11) in the Pacific Ocean ~96 km off San Diego; recovered by USS *John P. Murtha* (LPD 26)
- **Notable Changes:**
  - No new schedule or architecture changes since post-splashdown press conference
  - ESM helium valve leak severity confirmed as 10× worse than ground-test levels; extensive redesign flagged for Artemis IV
  - Artemis III (mid-2027, LEO HLS rendezvous/docking + AxEMU suit test) and Artemis IV (~early 2028, first crewed lunar landing) remain confirmed

### Technical & Engineering

- **ESM helium propulsion valve leak — NEW DETAIL (since 04:00 UTC report):**
  - NASA confirmed that the helium leak in the European Service Module's propulsion pressurization valve system was approximately **10× (an order of magnitude) larger in flight** than measured during pre-flight ground testing.
  - The leak was internal (across valves, not venting to space) and did **not** pose a flight safety risk for Artemis II, which used a free-return trajectory and did not require full propulsion system pressurization.
  - Because the ESM is jettisoned and burns up during reentry, NASA teams prioritized in-flight data collection to fully characterize the leak before losing the hardware.
  - The findings will likely drive an **extensive redesign of the valve system** for Artemis IV, which will require full system pressurization for lunar orbit insertion and surface operations support.
  - [NASA Managers Outline Artemis 2 Reentry and Address Propulsion Issue — SpaceQ](https://spaceq.ca/nasa-managers-outline-artemis-2-reentry-and-address-propulsion-issue-ahead-of-splashdown/)
  - [Artemis II: Helium Leak 10× Worse Than Expected — 19FortyFive](https://www.19fortyfive.com/2026/04/artemis-ii-is-about-to-splash-down-theres-a-helium-leak-thats-10-times-worse-than-expected-nasa-says-its-fine/)
  - [Orion Helium Leak No Threat to Artemis II Reentry, But Will Require Redesign — Nsane Forums / General News](https://nsaneforums.com/news/general-news/orion-helium-leak-no-threat-to-artemis-ii-reentry-but-will-require-redesign-r34500)
  - [5 Takeaways from the Artemis II Mission — CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/11/science/moon-mission-takeaways-artemis-2)
- **Orion heat shield analysis (ongoing):** Orion capsule "Integrity" at KSC undergoing CT scanning of AVCOAT ablative heat shield. Modified reentry trajectory (steeper, shorter skip) successfully mitigated the char-loss anomaly seen after Artemis I. Results not yet publicly released.
  - [NASA Shares Orion Heat Shield Findings, Updates Artemis Moon Missions — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-shares-orion-heat-shield-findings-updates-artemis-moon-missions/)
- **Manual piloting data analysis (ongoing):** Data from two manual piloting demonstration sessions is being analyzed to refine Orion handling qualities for crew rendezvous/docking training ahead of Artemis III and IV.
- **Artemis III mission profile (confirmed):** Mid-2027 LEO rendezvous and docking demonstration with SpaceX Starship HLS and/or Blue Origin Blue Moon; includes AxEMU spacesuit evaluation. No lunar landing.
  - [Artemis 3 and Beyond: What's Next for NASA — Space.com](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/artemis/artemis-3-and-beyond-whats-next-for-nasa-after-artemis-2-moon-success)
- **Post-landing functional tests:** Crew is completing obstacle course runs and simulated spacewalks to assess adaptation to gravity post-mission, per standard ~21-day reconditioning protocol.

### Science & Instruments

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO) — analysis underway:** 7,000+ high-resolution images from the ~7-hour lunar flyby (April 6; closest approach 4,067 miles / 6,545 km above the far-side surface) are being processed at JSC. Targets included Vavilov Crater, Hertzsprung Basin, and the Orientale Basin (~600-mile-wide impact structure formed ~3.8 billion years ago). Crew color discrimination reports are being cross-referenced with LRO and Kaguya datasets.
  - [NASA's Artemis II Crew Beams Official Moon Flyby Photos to Earth — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasas-artemis-ii-crew-beams-official-moon-flyby-photos-to-earth/)
  - [Earthset From the Lunar Far Side — NASA Earth Observatory](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/earth-observatory/earthset-from-the-lunar-far-side/)
  - [The Stunning Photos from Artemis 2's Flyby of the Moon — Astronomy.com](https://www.astronomy.com/space-exploration/artemis-2-lunar-flyby-in-images/)
- **Distance record set:** 252,756 miles (406,771 km) from Earth on April 6, surpassing Apollo 13's record (248,655 mi / 400,171 km, 1970).
  - [NASA's Artemis II Crew Eclipses Record for Farthest Human Spaceflight — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasas-artemis-ii-crew-eclipses-record-for-farthest-human-spaceflight/)
- **Solar corona / meteoroid impacts:** Six distinct meteoroid impact flashes on the darkened lunar surface recorded; data being analyzed to refine lunar impact hazard models.
- **AVATAR organ-on-a-chip:** Hardware recovered; Bone Marrow Chips at Wyss Institute (Harvard) for comparison against ground controls. Analysis expected to take months.
  - [Wyss Institute Technologies Enable Breakthrough in Astronaut Health Research — Wyss Institute](https://wyss.harvard.edu/news/wyss-institute-technologies-enable-breakthrough-in-astronaut-health-research-aboard-nasas-artemis-ii-mission/)
  - [NASA AVATAR Investigation — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/biological-physical/investigations/avatar/)
- **Radiation dosimetry:** M-42 EXT detectors showing preliminary readings within ~10% of predicted margins. Full analysis ongoing.
