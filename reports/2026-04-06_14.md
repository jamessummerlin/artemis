# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-06 14:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is on Flight Day 6 of 10, approximately 4.75 hours before the lunar flyby observation window opens at 18:45 UTC. All spacecraft systems and crew remain healthy on a nominal free-return trajectory. NASA and major streaming platforms begin live flyby coverage at 17:00 UTC; closest lunar approach (~4,070 miles above the far-side surface) is expected at ~23:02 UTC, at which point Orion will also break the Apollo 13 crew distance record (~252,757 miles from Earth).

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Active crewed mission — Flight Day 6 of 10; inside lunar sphere of influence; pre-flyby coast
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC from Launch Complex 39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026 ~00:07 UTC (5:07 p.m. PDT April 9) off the coast of San Diego; recovery by USS San Diego
- **Notable Program-Level Changes:**
  - Lunar Gateway paused/canceled (March 2026); resources redirected toward an Artemis Base Camp on the lunar surface (~$20B initiative announced March 2026)
  - Artemis III revised: no lunar landing; will perform LEO rendezvous/docking tests in 2027 with SpaceX Starship HLS and/or Blue Origin Blue Moon, including AxEMU spacesuit testing
  - Lunar landing deferred to Artemis IV (~early 2028)
  - SLS Exploration Upper Stage (EUS) canceled (February 2026); ULA Centaur V selected as alternative upper stage for Artemis IV+

### Technical & Engineering

- **Trajectory:** Orion is on a multi-trans-lunar injection (MTLI) hybrid free-return trajectory. The final outbound trajectory correction burn completed at 03:03 UTC April 6 (11:03 p.m. EDT April 5). No further propulsive maneuvers are required until post-flyby return corrections.
  - [Artemis II Flight Day 5: Correction Burn Complete](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/05/artemis-ii-flight-day-5-correction-burn-complete/)
- **Flyby parameters:**
  - Closest approach: ~4,070 miles (6,550 km) above the far-side surface at ~23:02 UTC
  - Maximum distance from Earth: ~252,757 miles (406,870 km) at ~23:07 UTC — breaking the Apollo 13 crew record of 248,655 miles set in April 1970 by 4,102 miles
  - Distance record break expected at ~17:56 UTC (approximately 2 hours after live coverage begins)
- **Communications blackout:** Planned ~40-minute DSN blackout begins ~22:44 UTC as Orion passes behind the Moon; communications expected to resume ~23:25 UTC at "Earthrise"
- **Spacecraft systems:** All systems nominal. No anomalies since the Universal Waste Management System (toilet) was successfully troubleshot on Flight Day 2.
  - [Crew and Ground Teams Successfully Troubleshoot Orion's Toilet](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/02/artemis-ii-flight-update-crew-and-ground-teams-successfully-troubleshoot-orions-toilet/)
- **Heat shield:** Orion's modified reentry angle (direct steeper single-pass descent) remains the approved mitigation for the Artemis I heat shield char loss anomaly; no additional hardware changes.
- **ESA European Service Module (ESM-2):** All 33 engines, four solar array wings, thermal control, and life support systems continue performing nominally. ESA engineers at ESTEC and NASA JSC maintain 24/7 monitoring.
  - [From Europe to the Moon: ESM-2's journey — ESA Orion blog](https://blogs.esa.int/orion/2026/03/25/from-europe-to-the-moon-esm-2s-journey/)

### Science & Instruments

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** During the flyby observation window (18:45–01:40 UTC), the crew will document ~35 geological targets following a Lunar Targeting Plan (LTP) developed by the Artemis II Lunar Science Team. Key targets include:
  - **Orientale Basin** — ~600-mile-wide impact basin straddling the near and far sides
  - Ancient mare basalts (lava flows) on the near side
  - Tectonic ridges and scarps from crustal cooling
  - Color and brightness variations indicating mineralogical composition
  - Far-side regions never before seen directly by human eyes
  - Crew work in pairs, providing real-time verbal descriptions to Mission Control science teams — the first systematic crewed lunar geological survey in over 50 years
  - [NASA Artemis II Lunar Science Operations](https://science.nasa.gov/solar-system/nasas-artemis-ii-lunar-science-operations-to-inform-future-missions/)
  - [Artemis II Lunar Targeting Plan](https://science.nasa.gov/resource/artemis-ii-lunar-targeting-plan/)
- **Solar corona and meteoroid observations:** At ~00:35 UTC April 7, the Sun will pass behind the Moon from Orion's perspective; the crew will image the solar corona for ~1 hour and observe for meteoroid impact flashes on the lunar surface
- **AVATAR investigation (A Virtual Astronaut Tissue Analog Response):** Organ-on-a-chip devices containing bone marrow cells derived from crew blood samples are operating continuously, studying deep-space radiation and microgravity effects on the human immune system
  - [Avatars for Astronaut Health to Fly on NASA's Artemis II](https://science.nasa.gov/directorates/smd/avatars-for-astronaut-health-nasa-artemis-ii/)
- **ARCHeR investigation:** Continuous real-time monitoring of crew performance, teamwork, sleep, and behavioral health via wrist monitors and saliva samples
- **Orion-to-Ground Optical Communications (O2O):** Laser communications experiment operating in cislunar space during the mission
  - [NTRS: Optical Communications for Artemis II Crewed Moon Mission — O2O System (20250006514)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250006514)
- **CubeSat secondary payloads:** Four international CubeSats (Germany/TACHELES, South Korea, Saudi Arabia, Argentina) deployed five hours after launch, continuing operations in cislunar space
  - [NTRS: Hitching a Ride on History: NASA's SLS Artemis II CubeSats (20250005942)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942)

### International Partners

- **ESA:** The Airbus-built European Service Module is performing nominally and is providing propulsion, power, thermal control, and life support for the mission. ESA frames today's flyby as a key validation milestone for European deep-space crewed mission systems. ESM-4 (for Artemis IV) is in final integration at KSC; ESM-5 and ESM-6 are in production in Bremen with shipments planned for 2027–2028.
  - [ESA — Artemis II](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Orion/Artemis_II)
  - [Heart of the mission: Airbus-built ESM — Airbus](https://www.airbus.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-03-heart-of-the-mission-airbus-built-esm-to-power-historic-artemis-ii-crew-to-the-moon)
- **CSA:** Jeremy Hansen is the first Canadian — and first non-American — to participate in a lunar mission. CSA is publishing daily mission logbook entries. The Gateway cancellation has raised questions about the future Canadian astronaut mission slot originally tied to a Gateway crewed mission commitment.
  - [Artemis II mission to complete historic lunar flyby — Canada.ca](https://www.canada.ca/en/space-agency/news/2026/04/artemisii-mission-to-complete-historic-lunar-flyby.html)
