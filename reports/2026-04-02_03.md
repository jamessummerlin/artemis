# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-02 03:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is in its 10th hour of flight on Day 2, with the Trans-Lunar Injection (TLI) burn now the critical next event, scheduled for approximately 23:35 UTC this evening (~7:30 PM EDT). A post-launch press conference revealed two minor anomalies — a brief communications dropout and a waste management system controller fault — both assessed as non-critical. Flight controllers are reviewing all Day 1 system health data today before issuing a TLI go/no-go.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** In-flight — Day 2; crew rested and preparing for TLI burn
- **Target Launch Date:** Launched April 1, 2026 at 22:35 UTC (6:35 PM EDT) — **COMPLETE**
- **Notable Changes:** TLI burn pending ~23:35 UTC April 2; two minor anomalies disclosed at post-launch press conference (communications dropout, toilet controller fault), both non-critical

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **Daily Mission Briefings Begin:** The first of NASA's daily Artemis II status briefings is scheduled for today, April 2, from JSC Houston. A second, post-TLI press conference is expected at approximately 8:30 PM EDT (~00:30 UTC April 3). [Mission coverage schedule](https://www.nasa.gov/missions/artemis/artemis-2/nasa-sets-coverage-for-artemis-ii-moon-mission/)
- **Post-Launch Press Conference — Anomalies Disclosed:** At a post-launch presser on April 1, NASA disclosed:
  1. **Communications dropout** (~51 minutes into flight): A ~10-minute partial loss of contact during a planned ground station handover. Mission Control spoke "in the blind." Commander Wiseman confirmed the crew could hear MCC throughout. Now resolved; not a safety concern.
  2. **Waste management system controller fault:** A controller error when the toilet was first activated on-orbit. Expected to take a few hours to troubleshoot. Classified as a minor crew comfort issue, not a safety concern.
- **Pre-launch anomalies resolved before liftoff:** A Flight Termination System issue and a Launch Abort System battery temperature exceedance (assessed as an instrumentation fault) were both cleared before the T-0 commitment. [NASA Artemis blog](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/artemis/)

### Technical & Engineering

- **TLI Burn (Planned ~23:35 UTC, April 2):** The ESM orbital maneuvering system main engine (~6,000 lbs thrust) will fire for approximately 6 minutes 5 seconds, delivering a ~900 mph velocity increase to send Orion on its free-return figure-eight trajectory. This is the last major propulsive event of the mission until the final de-orbit/entry. Mission Specialist Christina Koch is leading burn preparations.
- **Multi-Translunar Injection (MTLI) profile:** Due to extended time in the LEO parking orbit (allowing system checkouts), hydrogen boil-off necessitates a multi-burn TLI profile rather than a single large burn.
- **Go/No-Go Review:** Flight controllers at JSC are conducting a comprehensive review of all Day 1 system performance data (solar arrays, propulsion, navigation, ECLSS, communications) before committing the crew to the lunar trajectory. No issues expected to prevent the TLI go.
- **European Service Module (ESM-2):** Performing nominally in all functions — propulsion, power, and life support (90 kg oxygen, 240 kg water aboard). First time the ESM is active with crew; engineers on console at ESTEC, ESOC, and JSC. [ESA Artemis II page](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Orion/Artemis_II)
- **Trajectory:** After TLI, Orion begins a 4-day coast to the Moon on a free-return trajectory. No Lunar Orbit Insertion planned; closest lunar approach ~6,513 km from the far-side surface on April 6. Expected to surpass the Apollo 13 distance record (~400,171 km from Earth).

### Science & Instruments

- **Artemis II CubeSats:** Four 12U CubeSats were deployed from SLS into highly elliptical Earth orbit during ascent. These secondary payloads — independent of the crewed mission — are conducting scientific investigations:
  - **TACHELES** (Germany) — effects of space radiation on electrical components
  - **ATENEA** (Argentina) — radiation shielding performance and space environment mapping
  - **K-RadCube** (South Korea) — tissue-equivalent dosimetry for crew radiation risk
  - **Space Weather CubeSat-1** (Saudi Arabia) — space weather environment characterization
  [NTRS 20250005942](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942)
- **Optical Communications (O2O payload):** The Orion-to-Operations (O2O) optical terminal, operated from Mission Control Houston, is being checked out. Supports up to 250 Mbps downlink (vs. ~1 Mbps on S-band), enabling real-time HD video from deep space. [NTRS 20250010065](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065)
- **Human Research:** Crew medical monitoring and human-factors data collection under way, including actigraphy and audiovisual feeds, supporting risk characterization for deep-space behavioral health. [NTRS 20240011729](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20240011729)

### Crew Updates

- **Commander Reid Wiseman** — Post-liftoff quote: *"We have a beautiful moonrise — we're headed right at it."* Overseeing spacecraft operations and the pre-TLI system review.
- **Pilot Victor Glover** — Successfully completed manual proximity operations on Flight Day 1; exclaimed: *"I see it. Look at that, woohoo! I see the ICPS and the moon in the field of view."* Will become the first person of color to travel to the lunar vicinity.
- **Mission Specialist Christina Koch** — Leading TLI burn preparation sequence on Flight Day 2. Will be the first woman to travel to the lunar vicinity.
- **Mission Specialist Jeremy Hansen (CSA)** — Healthy on his first spaceflight; first Canadian and first non-U.S. citizen on a lunar mission. First live space-to-Earth Q&A scheduled for **April 4**. [CSA mission page](https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/missions/artemis-ii/)
- **Crew sleep/wake cycle:** The crew completed two 4-hour sleep periods overnight and was awoken this morning for Flight Day 2 activities.

## Academic & Research Papers

- **Title:** NASA's SLS Super Heavy-Lift Rocket in Process for Artemis II and Beyond
  - **Authors:** NASA MSFC team
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20250011679](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250011679) — December 2025
  - **Relevance:** Covers hardware production status for Artemis II–V; confirms RS-25 engine certification completed 2024 with L3Harris Technologies; validates the Block 1 configuration performance profile flown on April 1.

- **Title:** Orion Artemis II Optical Communications Architecture, Implementation and Test Systems
  - **Authors:** NASA JSC / Glenn Research Center
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20250010065](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065) — October 2025
  - **Relevance:** Detailed description of the O2O payload flying on this mission — forward link 20 Mbps, return link 250 Mbps, controlled from MCC Houston. Currently being checked out on Day 2.

- **Title:** Hitching a Ride on History: NASA's SLS Artemis II CubeSats
  - **Authors:** NASA MSFC / Secondary Payload Coordination
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20250005942](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942) — June 2025
  - **Relevance:** Overview of the four secondary CubeSat payloads (TACHELES, ATENEA, K-RadCube, SWC-1) deployed from SLS on April 1; now conducting independent science missions in elliptical Earth orbit.

- **Title:** Deep Space Radiation Measurements and Crew Radiation Protection for the NASA Artemis Program
  - **Authors:** NASA JSC / Langley Research Center
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20250002757](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250002757) — March 2025
  - **Relevance:** Reports radiation measurements from Artemis I and BioSentinel; directly informs Artemis II crew storm shelter protocols and EVA radiation operations for future missions.

- **Title:** Emerging Trends in Cislunar Space for Lunar Science Exploration and Space Robotics Aiding Human Spaceflight Safety
  - **Authors:** Arsalan Muhammad et al.
  - **Source:** [arXiv:2603.02878](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02878) — March 3, 2026
  - **Relevance:** Most recent directly relevant arXiv preprint; frames the Moon as a testbed for advanced AI and robotics in the Artemis context; informs pre-deployment infrastructure and ISRU strategies for Artemis III and beyond.

- **Title:** Ground-based Cislunar Space Surveillance Demonstrations at Los Alamos National Laboratory
  - **Authors:** Los Alamos National Laboratory
  - **Source:** [arXiv:2412.03339](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.03339) — December 2024
  - **Relevance:** Reports direct ground-based observation of the Artemis I Orion vehicle at ~440,000 km range (g-band magnitude 19.57); demonstrates independent tracking capability for crewed cislunar missions including Artemis II.

- **Title:** Optimized Trajectory Correction Burn Placement for the NASA Artemis II Mission *(previously cited; directly in-flight applicable)*
  - **Authors:** David Woffinden, Randy Eckman, Shane Robinson (NASA JSC)
  - **Source:** [NTRS 20230000223](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20230000223)
  - **Relevance:** Trajectory correction burns are being executed now; this paper describes the methodology.
