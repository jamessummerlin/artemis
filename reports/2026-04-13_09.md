# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-13 09:00 UTC

## Summary

Minor new items have emerged in the hour since the 08:00 UTC report. A new NASA NTRS technical report on the PROJECT ARCHeR crew health study (NTRS 20260002430) was recently posted, and post-flight analysis of the Orion UWMS toilet now points to a chemical-reaction-driven debris clog as the probable failure mode. No new NASA press releases, crew public appearances, or a White House visit date have been announced.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — Day 8 crew post-flight reconditioning, medical/human-performance evaluations, and lunar science debriefs at NASA Johnson Space Center
- **Mission Duration:** 10 days (April 1–10, 2026); splashdown April 10 at 8:07 p.m. EDT (00:07 UTC April 11) in the Pacific Ocean ~96 km off San Diego; recovered by USS *John P. Murtha* (LPD 26)
- **Notable Changes:**
  - No new schedule or architecture changes since post-splashdown press conference
  - ESM helium valve leak confirmed as ~10× worse than ground-test levels; extensive redesign needed for Artemis IV
  - Lunar Gateway cancelled (March 2026); Artemis III redesignated as LEO rendezvous/docking test (mid-2027); Artemis IV (~early 2028) remains planned as first crewed lunar landing

### Technical & Engineering

- **UWMS toilet post-flight investigation — new leading theory:**
  The post-flight analysis has surfaced a probable failure mode for the Orion UWMS (Universal Waste Management System) vent issue: a chemical reaction within the urine-processing chemistry is generating debris that clogs a filter in the vent line. This is distinct from the Day 1 urine-collection fan/controller fault (which was resolved in flight). The filter-clog theory has emerged from ground-team telemetry review and will be confirmed once Orion is accessed at KSC's processing facility. Engineers note there is limited time before Artemis III to implement a fix, as the next mission is slated for mid-2027.
  - [Let's Talk About the Artemis II Toilet Problems — PJ Media](https://pjmedia.com/rick-moran/2026/04/12/lets-talk-about-the-artemis-ii-toilet-problems-n4951706) *(April 12)*
  - [The Artemis 2 space toilet is actually working fine. But there is another problem — Space.com](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/artemis/the-artemis-2-space-toilet-is-actually-working-fine-but-there-is-another-problem)
  - [Artemis II Flight Update: Crew and Ground Teams Successfully Troubleshoot Orion's Toilet — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/02/artemis-ii-flight-update-crew-and-ground-teams-successfully-troubleshoot-orions-toilet/)
- **ESM helium propulsion valve leak (ongoing analysis):**
  Confirmed ~10× larger in flight than ground-test levels; internal (not venting to space). No flight-safety risk for Artemis II free-return. Redesign required for Artemis IV lunar orbit insertion.
- **Orion heat shield (30-day inspection underway):**
  Orion capsule "Integrity" at KSC undergoing CT scanning of AVCOAT ablative heat shield; full report expected ~May 10, 2026.
- **Lunar crater naming — IAU decision timeline confirmed:**
  The crew proposed two crater names during the April 6 flyby: *Carroll* (for Commander Reid Wiseman's late wife, Carroll Taylor Wiseman, who died of cancer in 2020) and *Integrity* (after the Orion spacecraft). IAU representative Ramasamy Venugopal confirmed a formal decision is expected "in about a month," consistent with standard timelines for straightforward naming requests. This follows the Apollo tradition of crew-proposed crater names (e.g., Mount Marilyn, proposed Apollo 8, formally accepted 2017).
  - [Artemis II names moon crater Carroll after Reid Wiseman's late wife — NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/07/nx-s1-5776440/artemis-moon-crater-carroll-wiseman)
  - [Carroll Crater — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carroll_(crater))
  - [Lunar crater naming by Artemis II astronauts follows Apollo tradition — WDBO/AP](https://www.wdbo.com/news/science/artemis-ii/6X2ZXCTQN4427GYQFU7ZTWBGFQ/)

### Science & Instruments

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO) — analysis underway:** 7,000+ high-resolution images and video from the ~7-hour lunar flyby (April 6; closest approach 4,067 miles / 6,545 km) being processed at JSC. Crew proposed names for two far-side craters (see above). Six meteoroid impact flashes recorded on the darkened lunar surface; data being analyzed for hazard modeling.
- **Distance record set:** 252,756 miles (406,771 km) from Earth on April 6, surpassing Apollo 13's record (248,655 mi / 400,171 km, 1970).
- **AVATAR organ-on-a-chip:** Hardware recovered; Bone Marrow Chips at Wyss Institute (Harvard) for comparison against ground controls.
- **Radiation dosimetry:** M-42 EXT detectors showing preliminary readings within ~10% of predicted margins.
- **ARCHeR (Artemis Research for Crew Health & Readiness):** Actigraphy devices worn by select crew members tracked sleep, movement, and activity throughout the mission. Post-mission data is now being analyzed; see new NTRS report below.

## Academic & Research Papers

- **Title:** PROJECT ARCHeR: Artemis II Update — Artemis Research for Crew Health and Readiness

  - **Source:** NASA Technical Reports Server, NTRS 20260002430 (posted ~April 2026)
