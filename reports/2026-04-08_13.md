# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-08 13:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is well into Flight Day 8, with TCB-1 (Return Trajectory Correction Burn 1) confirmed successfully completed at 8:03 PM EDT on April 7 (01:03 UTC April 8) — a 15-second thruster firing producing a 1.6 ft/s velocity change. The crew is cruising toward Earth on nominal trajectory, splashdown remains firmly on track for April 10 at approximately 8:07 PM EDT off San Diego, and Flight Day 8 activities are underway including the second manual piloting demonstration and Jeremy Hansen's third CSA Q&A with Canadians.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Active crewed mission — Flight Day 8 of 10; nominal trans-Earth coast; TCB-1 complete
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC, Launch Complex 39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026 at ~00:07 UTC (~8:07 p.m. EDT / 5:07 p.m. PDT April 9), Pacific Ocean ~50–80 miles off San Diego; recovery vessel **USS John P. Murtha (LPD 26)**
- **Notable Changes Since 22:00 UTC April 7 Report:**
  - TCB-1 confirmed complete: 8:03 PM EDT April 7 (01:03 UTC April 8), 15-second burn, 1.6 ft/s ΔV — NASA published dedicated confirmation blog post
  - Space.com April 8 headline: "Astronauts cruise toward Earth during Flight Day 8" — crew on nominal return trajectory
  - USS John P. Murtha has departed port and is en route to Pacific recovery site
  - Weather forecast for splashdown: mild, monitoring for rain only; no range safety concerns flagged
  - Jeremy Hansen's third CSA Q&A with Canadians (including youth) completed Flight Day 8
  - CSA astronaut Jenni Gibbons served first official capcom shift from Mission Control Houston

### Technical & Engineering

- **TCB-1 — COMPLETE (8:03 PM EDT April 7 / 01:03 UTC April 8):** Orion's auxiliary thrusters fired for 15 seconds, delivering a 1.6 ft/s velocity change. This is the first of three planned return trajectory correction burns (TCB-1, TCB-2, TCB-3). Mission control confirmed nominal execution; all post-burn parameters within expected ranges.
- **TCB-2 — scheduled April 9:** Second return trajectory correction burn to progressively refine the re-entry corridor. Nominal scheduling maintained.
- **TCB-3 — scheduled April 9–10:** Final trajectory fine-tuning burn before entry interface on April 10.
- **Entry interface (April 10):** Orion targeting reentry at ~25,000 mph (~40,000 km/h) — highest-velocity crewed reentry ever attempted. Modified steeper entry profile (replacing originally planned skip reentry) instituted to manage AVCOAT heat shield thermal environment. All heat shield systems nominal.
- **Second manual piloting demonstration (Flight Day 8, ~10:59 PM ET April 8):** Commander Wiseman and Pilot Glover scheduled to repeat the manual piloting demonstration. Tasks include centering a target in the windows, returning to tail-to-Sun attitude, and comparing six degree-of-freedom (6-DOF) vs. three degree-of-freedom (3-DOF) control modes — data feeds directly into future Orion human-interface requirements.
- **Wastewater/toilet system:** Partial workaround (contingency collapsible urinal devices / CCUs) remains in effect; non-mission-impacting.
- **Total mission distance:** Approximately 695,081 miles (~1,118,624 km) round trip around the Moon and back to Earth.
- **ESA European Service Module (ESM-2):** Operating nominally through trans-Earth coast. Providing propulsion, power, air/water life support, and thermal control.

### Science & Instruments

- **Radiation Storm Shelter Drill (Flight Day 8, ~8:15 PM ET April 8):** Crew constructed a temporary radiation shelter inside Orion — first-ever execution by a crew in deep space. Procedure: central stowage bay contents were relocated to create a lower-dose region in the capsule using mass shielding between the crew and Orion's heat shield; ESM ran at peak capacity to scrub air and regulate temperature during the drill. Drill data will inform emergency protocols for future Artemis and lunar surface missions.
- **AVATAR (organ-on-a-chip):** Nominal; bone-marrow organ-chips continue collecting deep-space and return-phase radiation exposure data. Saliva sample collection ongoing.
- **ARCHER (actigraphy):** Crew wristband monitors continue collecting sleep, circadian rhythm, and activity data through the return phase.
- **HERA/CAD dosimetry:** Six Hybrid Electronic Radiation Assessors and Crew Active Dosimeters recording accumulated dose; crew now re-entering Earth's magnetosphere.
- **Space Weather / Solar monitoring:** University of Michigan AI model providing real-time solar radiation risk assessment; no solar energetic particle events flagged.
  - [To Protect Artemis II Astronauts, NASA Experts Keep Eyes on Sun — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/missions/artemis/artemis-2/to-protect-artemis-ii-astronauts-nasa-experts-keep-eyes-on-sun/)
- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** All 35 observations from April 6 flyby in analysis at JSC. Far-side imagery of features "never before seen by human eyes" being processed.
  - [Artemis II astronauts see parts of the moon rarely seen by humans — NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/06/nx-s1-5775639/artemis-astronauts-moon-observation-nasa-human-eye)
