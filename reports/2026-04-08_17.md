# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-08 17:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II remains nominal on Flight Day 8 at 17:14 UTC, cruising inbound on trans-Earth coast with splashdown unchanged for April 10 at ~00:07 UTC off San Diego. No new NASA blog post or anomaly has been reported since the 16:00 UTC cycle; all FD8 evening events (Carney/Hansen CSA call at 23:20 UTC, crew media call at 01:45 UTC April 9, Wiseman/Glover manual piloting demo at ~01:59 UTC April 9) remain upcoming. A second return trajectory correction burn (TCB-Return-2) is listed as conditional on the FD8 schedule and has not yet been confirmed. Additional details surfaced this cycle on the CSA call protocol and the ISS ship-to-ship call participants from April 7, and a newly confirmed AIAA technical papers collection on Artemis II provides a broader academic resource base.

- **Current Phase:** Active crewed mission — Flight Day 8 of 10; trans-Earth coast; nominal, no anomalies

## Academic & Research Papers

- **Title:** AIAA Artemis II Technical Papers Collection (2024–2026)
  - **Authors:** Multiple; contributors from Lockheed Martin (Orion), Aerojet Rocketdyne / Boeing / Northrop Grumman / Teledyne Brown (SLS), Jacobs (Exploration Ground Systems)
  - **Source:** AIAA Aerospace Research Central (ARC); Journal of Spacecraft and Rockets; AIAA SciTech Forum 2024–2026
  - **Relevance:** AIAA has assembled and made complimentary a dedicated collection of technical papers on Artemis II systems across Orion, SLS, and ground systems — the most comprehensive single peer-reviewed/conference-paper aggregation related to the mission. Access via AIAA's digital library.
  - **URL:** [AIAA Anticipates Artemis II Launch with Collection of Technical Papers — Aerospace America](https://aerospaceamerica.aiaa.org/institute/aiaa-anticipates-artemis-ii-launch-with-collection-of-technical-papers/)

- **Title:** Human-Centered Design of Next-Generation Telemetry Displays for NASA's Moon and Mars Exploration Missions (NTRS 20250011659)
  - **Authors:** NASA team
  - **Source:** NASA Technical Reports Server; published January 31, 2026
  - **Relevance:** Addresses the design of mission control telemetry displays for Artemis crewed lunar missions — directly applicable to MCC-H (Mission Control Center Houston) support of Artemis II and future missions.
  - **URL:** https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250011659

- **Title:** Groundwork for the Moon: TEST3 Supports NASA's Artemis III Mission (NTRS 20250011613)
  - **Authors:** NASA contractor team
  - **Source:** NASA Technical Reports Server; acquired December 19, 2025
  - **Relevance:** Documents the TEST3 systems/procedures supporting Artemis III surface mission planning — contextualizes the Artemis II flight readiness in the broader program progression toward crewed lunar landing.
  - **URL:** https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250011613

- **Title:** HLS Program Status — IAC 2025 (NTRS 20250008728)
  - **Authors:** Steve Creech, Acting Program Manager, Human Landing System (HLS) Program, NASA
  - **Source:** NASA Technical Reports Server; IAC 2025 (International Astronautical Congress)
  - **Relevance:** Pre-Artemis II launch status of the HLS program (Starship HLS / Blue Moon), covering readiness milestones for Artemis III and IV — key context for post-Artemis II mission planning.
  - **URL:** https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250008728
