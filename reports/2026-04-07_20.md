# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-07 20:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II has completed Flight Day 7's primary milestones: Orion exited the lunar sphere of influence at ~17:25 UTC (1:25 p.m. EDT) at 41,072 miles from the Moon, and the first of three return trajectory correction burns was completed in the afternoon, putting the crew firmly on course for April 10 splashdown off San Diego. All four crew members are healthy; Flight Day 8 activities — radiation storm shelter drill, manual piloting demonstration, and a third CSA live Q&A — are next up. No new anomalies; all systems remain nominal.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Active crewed mission — Flight Day 7 of 10 complete; trans-Earth coast; first return TCB complete; under Earth-dominant gravity
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC, Launch Complex 39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026 at ~00:07 UTC (~8:07 p.m. EDT / 5:07 p.m. PDT April 9), Pacific Ocean ~50–80 miles off San Diego; recovery vessel **USS John P. Murtha (LPD 26)**
- **Notable Changes Since 15:00 UTC Report:**
  - Lunar sphere of influence exit confirmed at ~17:25 UTC — Orion now under Earth-dominant gravity
  - Return Trajectory Correction Burn 1 (TCB-1) completed in the second half of Flight Day 7
  - Flight Day 7 science debrief and ISS crew call both complete; remainder of day designated off-duty rest
  - Splashdown weather forecast: favorable conditions through Friday afternoon; rain/thunderstorms expected late Friday night (after splashdown)
  - San Diego Air and Space Museum hosting public splashdown watch party

### Technical & Engineering

- **Lunar sphere of influence exit — confirmed (17:25 UTC, April 7):** Orion crossed the lunar sphere of influence boundary at approximately 41,072 miles from the Moon, transitioning from Moon-dominant to Earth-dominant gravity. All telemetry nominal through this milestone.
- **Return Trajectory Correction Burn 1 (TCB-1) — completed Flight Day 7 afternoon:** The first of up to three correction burns refined Orion's return trajectory. Two additional burns (TCB-2 and TCB-3) are planned for April 8–9 to fine-tune the April 10 splashdown corridor.
- **ULA Centaur V selected for Artemis IV/V SLS upper stage (March 2026, context):** NASA formally selected ULA's Centaur V (the upper stage currently flying on ULA's Vulcan rocket) to replace the delayed Exploration Upper Stage (EUS) on SLS Block 1B for Artemis IV and V, both targeted for 2028. The sole-source contract requires ULA to deliver two flight units and one flight spare, with the first unit at KSC no later than 9 months before Artemis IV launch.
  - [NASA ditches delayed SLS upper stage for ULA's Centaur V — The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/03/09/nasa_centaur_v_sls/)
  - [NASA Formally Selects ULA's Centaur V For Artemis IV and V — Talk of Titusville](https://talkoftitusville.com/2026/03/06/nasa-formally-selects-ulas-centaur-v-for-artemis-iv-and-v/)
  - [NASA selects Centaur for new SLS upper stage — SpaceNews](https://spacenews.com/nasa-selects-centaur-for-new-sls-upper-stage/)
- **Entry interface (April 10):** Orion targeting reentry at ~25,000 mph (~40,000 km/h) — highest-velocity crewed reentry ever attempted. Post-splashdown heat shield inspection planned.
- **Wastewater/toilet system:** Partial fix remains in effect; crew using contingency collapsible urinal devices (CCUs). Non-mission-impacting.
- **Splashdown weather:** Fox 5 San Diego reports current forecast shows favorable conditions through Friday afternoon (splashdown window); rain and thunderstorms expected late Friday night (~11 p.m. local), well after recovery operations conclude.
  - [NASA monitors weather for Artemis II's Pacific splashdown off San Diego — Fox 5 San Diego](https://fox5sandiego.com/weather/nasa-monitors-weather-for-artemis-iis-pacific-splashdown-off-san-diego/)

### Science & Instruments

- **CLO debrief complete (Flight Day 7 morning):** All 35 geological observations from the April 6 flyby — including far-side feature identification, color variation mapping, and 57 minutes of solar corona imagery — have been downlinked and reviewed with JSC lunar science teams.
- **AVATAR (organ-on-a-chip):** Nominal; radiation exposure data collection entering final phase as Orion approaches Earth's magnetosphere.
- **HERA/CAD dosimetry:** Six Hybrid Electronic Radiation Assessors and Crew Active Dosimeters continue recording; deep-space peak exposure phase complete post-flyby. DLR M-42 EXT also operating nominally.
- **Space Weather CubeSat-1 (Saudi Arabia), ATENEA (Argentina/CONAE), TACHELES (Germany/DLR):** All three CubeSats continue operating nominally.
- **K-RadCube (South Korea/KASA):** Previously confirmed lost; no new status.
