# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-07 05:00 UTC

## Summary

The Artemis II lunar flyby is complete. Orion emerged from behind the Moon at approximately 23:25 UTC on April 6 after a planned 41-minute communications blackout, having reached a record-breaking maximum distance of 252,757 miles from Earth (surpassing Apollo 13 by ~4,111 miles). The crew subsequently observed a 57-minute total solar eclipse and captured unprecedented solar corona imagery; Flight Day 7 is now underway with Orion on trans-Earth cruise and all systems nominal.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Active crewed mission — Flight Day 7 of 10; trans-Earth cruise; lunar flyby complete
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC from Launch Complex 39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026 ~00:07 UTC (~8:07 p.m. EDT April 9), Pacific Ocean ~50 miles off San Diego; recovery by USS San Diego
- **Notable Changes Since Last Report (19:00 UTC April 6):**
  - Communications blackout completed (22:44–23:25 UTC, April 6) — all comms nominal upon restoration
  - Maximum distance record confirmed: **252,757 miles** (406,772 km) at ~23:07 UTC
  - Closest lunar approach confirmed: **~4,070 miles** above the far-side surface at ~23:02 UTC
  - Earthrise captured on camera as Orion emerged from behind the Moon at 23:25 UTC
  - Total solar eclipse observed: 57 minutes of totality (~00:35–01:32 UTC April 7)
  - Lunar observation window closed at ~01:40 UTC April 7
  - President Trump called the crew post-flyby; invited crew to the Oval Office
  - Day 7: Orion exiting lunar sphere of influence at ~17:25 UTC, 41,072 miles from Moon

### Technical & Engineering

- **Communications blackout (22:44–23:25 UTC April 6):** Planned 41-minute blackout occurred as Orion transited behind the Moon. Contact restored exactly on schedule at 23:25 UTC. No anomalies detected.
- **Maximum distance from Earth confirmed:** At ~23:07 UTC April 6, Orion reached **252,757 miles (406,772 km)** — a new human spaceflight distance record, surpassing the Apollo 13 record of 248,655 miles by approximately **4,111 miles (~6,614 km)**.
- **Closest lunar approach confirmed:** ~**4,070 miles (6,550 km)** above the far-side surface at ~23:02 UTC. Closest approach executed without trajectory correction — no burns required.
- **Earthrise moment:** As Orion emerged from behind the Moon at 23:25 UTC, the crew captured "Earthrise" — Earth rising above the lunar limb. Expected to be among the mission's most significant photographic products.
  - [National Geographic: Artemis II crew recreates 'Earthrise'](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/science/article/artemis-ii-nasa-earthrise-photo)
- **Day 7 trajectory:** Orion is exiting the lunar sphere of influence at approximately **17:25 UTC** (1:25 p.m. EDT), at a distance of **41,072 miles from the Moon**. Trans-Earth cruise is underway.
- **Spacecraft systems:** All systems remain nominal. No new anomalies reported on Flight Day 7. Entry velocity for April 10 splashdown remains ~25,000 mph — the fastest crewed reentry ever attempted.
  - [NPR: Artemis II astronauts heading home after historic moon flyby](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/06/nx-s1-5773187/nasa-artemis-ii-moon-lunar-flyby)

### Science & Instruments

- **Solar eclipse / corona observations (00:35–01:32 UTC April 7):**
  - Totality lasted **57 minutes** (8:35–9:32 p.m. EDT April 6) — the longest total solar eclipse ever observed by humans beyond Earth's orbit
  - From Orion's vantage point beyond the Moon, the crew viewed the Sun disappear behind the lunar limb and observed the **solar corona** glowing around the Moon's edge for nearly an hour
  - Scientists provided prompts for the crew to verbally describe corona features in real time for solar physics teams on the ground — data expected to complement observations from orbiting spacecraft
  - The crew also searched for **zodiacal light** (sunlight reflected off interplanetary dust) during totality
  - Crew reaction: *"Humans probably have not evolved to see what we're seeing. It is truly hard to describe. It is amazing."*
  - [Scientific American: Artemis II crew experience total solar eclipse from space](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/nasas-artemis-ii-crew-experience-total-solar-eclipse-from-space/)
  - [Space.com: Artemis 2 LIVE — Solar eclipse ends for astronauts at the moon](https://www.space.com/news/live/artemis-2-nasa-moon-mission-updates-april-6-2026)
- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO) — window closed ~01:40 UTC April 7:**
  - Final targets observed during the trailing edge of the flyby. All 30 pre-selected geological targets were addressed; crew imagery and verbal descriptions downlinked to the science team.
  - Post-mission analysis of photography and video from Orion's 32 cameras will begin after splashdown.
- **Earthrise photography:** Crew captured new Earthrise images as Orion emerged from behind the Moon at 23:25 UTC. Images will be compared with the iconic Apollo 8 Earthrise and used for public engagement and science communication.
  - [Space.com: Artemis 2 moon astronauts to try to recreate Apollo 8's historic 'Earthrise' photo](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/artemis/artemis-2-moon-astronauts-will-try-to-recreate-apollo-8s-historic-earthrise-photo-during-april-6-flyby)
- **AVATAR investigation:** Organ-on-a-chip bone marrow devices continue operating. Peak deep-space radiation exposure phase now passing as Orion moves back toward Earth's magnetosphere.
- **O2O laser communications:** Continues operating nominally in cislunar space.

|---|---|---|---|

- All four crew members are reported healthy and in good spirits on Flight Day 7. No medical concerns.
- **President Trump called the crew** following the successful flyby. Speaking via remote link-up from the White House, with NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman present at Johnson Space Center, Trump told the crew: *"Today you've made history and made all America really proud, incredibly proud."* He called them "modern-day pioneers" and extended an invitation to visit the Oval Office upon return. A brief audio silence (~1 minute) mid-call was resolved when the crew requested a comms check and Trump confirmed he was still on the line.
