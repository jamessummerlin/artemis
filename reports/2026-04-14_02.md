# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-14 02:00 UTC

## Summary

No significant new developments in the approximately four hours since the 2026-04-13 22:00 UTC report. The Artemis II crew is now on Day 16 of post-flight reconditioning at NASA Johnson Space Center in Houston. All standing open items — the Artemis III crew announcement, the White House visit date, the Orion heat shield 30-day CT-scan, the ESM helium valve characterization report, the Boeing SLS layoff situation, and ongoing science data analysis — remain unchanged.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — Day 16 of crew post-flight reconditioning, medical/human-performance evaluations, and lunar science debriefs at NASA Johnson Space Center
- **Mission Duration:** 10 days (April 1–10, 2026); splashdown April 10 at 8:07 p.m. EDT (00:07 UTC April 11) in the Pacific Ocean ~96 km off San Diego; recovered by USS *John P. Murtha* (LPD 26)
- **Total distance flown:** 694,481 miles; maximum distance from Earth: 252,756 miles (406,771 km) — a new human spaceflight record (surpassing Apollo 13's 248,655 mi, 1970)
- **Notable Changes:**
  - No new mission-status changes since the 2026-04-13 22:00 UTC report
  - White House visit: still no confirmed date (crew invited by President Trump during April 7 in-orbit call)
  - Artemis III crew announcement: still pending ("soon" per NASA AA Kshatriya, April 10; NASA Administrator Isaacman, April 11–12)
  - Boeing SLS layoff notices: no new updates (~200 workers in Huntsville, AL; from ~400 original notices)
  - ESM helium valve leak confirmed ~10× worse than ground-test levels; ESA redesign required for Artemis IV
  - Lunar Gateway cancelled (March 2026); Artemis III redesignated as LEO rendezvous/docking test (mid-2027); Artemis IV (~early 2028) planned as first crewed lunar landing

Inspection mapping every square inch of the Avcoat surface using visual documentation, ultrasonic, and X-ray equipment to detect internal cracking and measure char depths. Full report expected ~May 10, 2026. NASA used a novel re-entry protocol (shorter skip, sustained higher temperature) to prevent trapped gas from fracturing the ablator. No new findings as of this report.

### Science & Instruments

*No new science results published since the 2026-04-13 22:00 UTC report. Full standing status:*

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** 10,000+ high-resolution images and video from the April 6 lunar flyby (closest approach 4,067 mi / 6,545 km from the far-side surface) being processed at JSC. Observations covered 30 designated surface features including the Orientale basin (~600 mi wide). Six meteoroid impact flashes recorded on the darkened lunar surface; hazard-modeling analysis ongoing. During the solar eclipse phase, crew studied the solar corona.
  - [NASA's Artemis II Lunar Science Operations to Inform Future Missions — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/solar-system/nasas-artemis-ii-lunar-science-operations-to-inform-future-missions/)
  - [NASA's Artemis II Crew Beams Official Moon Flyby Photos to Earth — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasas-artemis-ii-crew-beams-official-moon-flyby-photos-to-earth/)
- **Distance record:** 252,756 miles (406,771 km) from Earth on April 6, 2026 — surpassing Apollo 13's 248,655 mi (1970) by more than 4,000 miles.
- **AVATAR Bone Marrow Organ Chips:** Hardware recovered post-splashdown; comparison analysis of flight chips vs. ground controls underway at Wyss Institute (Harvard).
  - [AVATAR investigation — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/biological-physical/investigations/avatar/)
- **ARCHeR (Artemis Research for Crew Health and Readiness):** Wearable sensors tracked crew heart rate, sleep, cognitive function, and team dynamics; actigraphy and audiovisual data being processed at JSC.
  - [NTRS 20260002430 — PROJECT ARCHeR: Artemis II Update](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20260002430)
- **Radiation dosimetry (M-42 EXT detectors + personal dosimeters):** Preliminary readings within ~10% of predicted margins; full analysis ongoing.
- **International CubeSats:** CubeSats from Germany (*TACHELES*), Argentina (*ATENEA*), South Korea, and Saudi Arabia deployed in high Earth orbit; data analysis ongoing.
- **IAU crater naming:** NASA will formally submit proposals for *Carroll* and *Integrity* craters (named by crew during flyby); IAU decision expected ~mid-May 2026.
- **NASA SVS lunar flyby simulation:** NASA Scientific Visualization Studio published simulation of the April 6 lunar flyby sequence.
  - [Simulating the Artemis II Lunar Flyby on April 6, 2026 — NASA SVS](https://svs.gsfc.nasa.gov/5633/)
