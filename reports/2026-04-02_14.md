# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-02 14:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is in Flight Day 2, completing final orbital adjustments in high Earth orbit before the Trans-Lunar Injection (TLI) burn scheduled for approximately 23:35 UTC tonight (7:30–8:00 PM EDT). The perigee raise burn was completed successfully at approximately 11:06 UTC (7:06 AM EDT), and the Orion toilet issue flagged on Flight Day 1 has been resolved. The crew and spacecraft are in good health ahead of the most critical maneuver of the mission. In parallel, NASA has formalized a significant architectural update: the Lunar Gateway program has been cancelled, Artemis III has been redesigned as a low-Earth-orbit integrated systems test, and Artemis IV (early 2028) is now the first planned crewed lunar landing.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** In-flight — Flight Day 2; awaiting TLI burn (scheduled ~23:35 UTC April 2)
- **Launched:** April 1, 2026 at 6:35 PM EDT (22:35 UTC) from LC-39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Current Orbit:** High Earth checkout orbit, apogee ~43,760 miles (70,403 km)
- **TLI Burn Status:** Scheduled ~23:35 UTC April 2 (~7:30 PM EDT); flight controllers conducting final go/no-go review
- **Splashdown Target:** ~April 10–11, 2026, Pacific Ocean off San Diego

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **Perigee raise burn complete:** The ESM main engine fired for 43 seconds at approximately 11:06 UTC (7:06 AM EDT), raising the lowest point of Orion's orbit and completing the orbital shaping sequence begun by the apogee raise burn on Flight Day 1. Ground crew woke the crew at 7:06 AM EDT with the song *"Sleepyhead"* by Young and Sick. [NASA blog — Perigee Raise Burn Complete](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/02/artemis-ii-flight-update-perigee-raise-burn-complete/)
- **Toilet issue resolved:** Working closely with mission control, the crew restored the Orion spacecraft's waste management system to normal operations following the proximity operations demonstration. [NASA blog — Toilet Troubleshoot](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/02/artemis-ii-flight-update-crew-and-ground-teams-successfully-troubleshoot-orions-toilet/)
- **Proximity operations complete (Flight Day 1):** The crew fully completed the proximity operations demonstration, approaching to within 30 feet of the spent ICPS stage. [NASA blog — Proximity Ops Complete](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/01/artemis-ii-flight-update-proximity-operations-complete-perigee-raise-burn-up-next/)
- **Artemis program architecture update:** NASA announced the cancellation of the Lunar Gateway and redesigned the Artemis III mission as a low-Earth-orbit integrated systems test; Artemis IV is now the first crewed lunar landing. [NASA Architecture Update](https://www.nasa.gov/directorates/esdmd/nasa-strengthens-artemis-adds-mission-refines-overall-architecture/)

### Technical & Engineering

- **TLI Burn Profile (Pending):**
  - Burn duration: ~6 minutes, 5 seconds
  - Engine: ESM orbital maneuvering system main engine (~6,000 lbs thrust)
  - Delta-V: ~900 mph added velocity to escape Earth orbit
  - Trajectory type: Free-return, figure-eight cislunar path (no Lunar Orbit Insertion required)
  - Profile: Multi-translunar injection (MTLI) accounting for hydrogen boil-off during extended LEO parking
  - Post-TLI: Orion begins a ~4-day coast to the Moon; DSN assumes primary comm responsibility

- **Orbital burns summary (all Flight Day 1–2):**
  - Perigee raise maneuver (ICPS RL10): raised apogee to initial checkout orbit
  - Apogee raise burn (ICPS RL10, ~18 min): raised apogee to ~43,760 miles — highest any astronaut has been since Apollo 17
  - Perigee raise burn (ESM main engine, 43 sec): completed orbital shaping for TLI setup

- **Heat shield — modified reentry trajectory confirmed:** For Artemis II reentry (~April 10–11), NASA is flying a steeper direct-entry profile (abandoning the skip-reentry used on Artemis I) to limit thermal exposure and reduce ablative char loss observed post-Artemis I. The Flight Readiness Review on March 12, 2026 produced a unanimous "go" vote. [Phys.org heat shield coverage](https://phys.org/news/2026-03-shield-safety-stakes-nasa-artemis.html)

- **Deep Space Network handover:** Planned switch from Near Space Network to Deep Space Network will complete ahead of TLI, marking the first time in 50 years a crewed spacecraft has traveled far enough from Earth to require the DSN.

- **SLS Block 1 configuration standardized:** NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman announced February 26, 2026 that development of SLS Block 1B (with Exploration Upper Stage) and Block 2 are cancelled. All future Artemis flights will use the Block 1 configuration. Mobile Launcher 2 development is also cancelled. A new second stage to replace the ICPS is under assessment for Artemis IV onward. [NASASpaceFlight.com architecture report](https://www.nasaspaceflight.com/2026/03/artemis-course-correction-moonward-details/)

- **NTRS — Optical communications (O2O):** The Orion Artemis II Optical Communications payload (NTRS 20250010065, published October 2025) enables up to 260 Mbps downlink, supporting near-real-time 4K video of the lunar flyby. [NTRS 20250010065](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065)

- **NTRS — CubeSats:** Four 12U CubeSats from international Artemis Accords partners deployed into highly elliptical Earth orbit from the SLS upper stage adapter. (NTRS 20250005942, June 2025). [NTRS 20250005942](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942)

### Science & Instruments

- **Primary science focus:** Systems validation — Orion life support, navigation, communications, and deep-space operational procedures with crew aboard for the first time.
- **Lunar far-side observations:** During the April 6 flyby (~6,513 km closest approach from the far-side surface), the crew will photograph and observe portions of the lunar far side that no human has directly seen. Partially illuminated far-side terrain will create shadows enhancing relief, ridges, slopes, and crater rims. NASA Scientific Visualization Studio has prepared detailed photorealistic flyby simulations for crew prep. [NASA SVS Artemis II Flight Path](https://svs.gsfc.nasa.gov/20412/)
- **Optical communications (O2O):** Up to 260 Mbps downlink capability enables high-fidelity near-real-time 4K lunar imagery.
- **Human research & radiation monitoring:** Crew conducting continuous physiological monitoring and radiation dose measurements throughout the mission. The ARCHER medical monitoring program is active.
- **Proximity operations navigation data:** Docking camera data from the Flight Day 1 ICPS maneuver will feed development of autonomous rendezvous algorithms for future Artemis missions.
- **CubeSat science:** Four 12U CubeSats from Artemis Accords international partners conducting technology demonstrations and scientific research in highly elliptical Earth orbit.
- **Lunar Science Operations:** Crew observations and imaging during the flyby will inform future Artemis III/IV surface science planning, per NASA Science directorate guidance. [NASA Science — Lunar Science Operations](https://science.nasa.gov/solar-system/nasas-artemis-ii-lunar-science-operations-to-inform-future-missions/)

### Crew Updates

- **Commander Reid Wiseman:** Overseeing all spacecraft systems and mission decision-making ahead of TLI. After launch stated, *"We have a beautiful moonrise — we're headed right at it."* Raising two children alone since the death of his wife in 2020; his personal motivation has been widely covered in mission profiles.
- **Pilot Victor Glover:** Successfully flew Orion manually during proximity operations on Flight Day 1; exclaimed, *"I see it. Look at that, woohoo! I see the ICPS and the moon in the field of view."* First person of color and first Black astronaut to travel to the vicinity of the Moon.
- **Mission Specialist Christina Koch:** Preparing systems for TLI on Flight Day 2. Holds the record for the longest single spaceflight by a woman (328 days). Will become the first woman to travel to the lunar vicinity.
- **Mission Specialist Jeremy Hansen (CSA):** On his first spaceflight. Will be the first Canadian and first non-U.S. citizen to participate in a lunar mission. Is carrying a moon pendant with his family's birthstones engraved with "moon and back." First live space-to-ground Q&A scheduled for April 4. [CSA event — Hansen Q&A](https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/events/2026/2026-04-04-artemis-ii-first-q-and-a-jeremy-hansen-live-from-space.asp)
- **Backup crew:** Andre Douglas (backup for NASA three); Jenni Gibbons (backup for Hansen) is serving as Artemis capcom from ground control.

### International Partners

**ESA:**
- The European Service Module 2 (ESM-2) — built by Airbus in Bremen, integrating contributions from 20+ companies across 10 ESA member states and 100+ suppliers — is performing nominally. This is the first time the ESM life support systems (90 kg O₂, 240 kg drinking water, thermal control) have been active with a crew aboard.
- ESM carries 33 engines total: 1 main OMS engine (heritage Space Shuttle, flew 6 missions 2000–2002), 8 auxiliary engines, 24 RCS thrusters. Four solar array wings generate ~11.2 kW, giving Orion *Integrity* a ~63-foot wingspan.
- Over 200 ESA, Airbus, Lockheed Martin, and NASA specialists are monitoring the mission from ESTEC (Netherlands), ESOC (Germany), and JSC Houston.
- ESM-3 delivered and undergoing testing for Artemis III; ESM-4 at KSC; ESM-5 and ESM-6 in production (planned delivery 2027–2028).
- [ESA Artemis II page](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Orion/Artemis_II) | [ESA — Europe powers Artemis II](https://www.esa.int/Newsroom/Press_Releases/Europe_powers_Artemis_II_mission_to_the_Moon) | [ESA blog — ESM engines](https://blogs.esa.int/orion/2026/03/30/europes-engines-powering-artemis-ii/)

**CSA:**
- Jeremy Hansen is in orbit on his first spaceflight. Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney issued a statement calling the launch *"a testament to Colonel Hansen's exceptional skill"* and described Canada as *"a nation of explorers, builders, and innovators."*
- CSA astronaut David Saint-Jacques hosted media at the John H. Chapman Space Centre in Longueuil on launch day; CSA astronaut Joshua Kutryk was on-site in Florida.
- CSA backup astronaut Jenni Gibbons is serving as capcom from ground control during the mission.
- Three live space-to-ground communications with Hansen are scheduled (Flight Days 4, 5, and 8), to be livestreamed on CSA's Facebook and YouTube.
- Canada's long-term Artemis contribution is Canadarm3, now targeted for the surface-focused post-Gateway architecture.

## Academic & Research Papers

- **Title:** Optimized Trajectory Correction Burn Placement for the NASA Artemis II Mission
  - **Authors:** David Woffinden, Randy Eckman, Shane Robinson (NASA JSC)
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20230000223 (AAS 23-062)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20230000223)
  - **Relevance:** Directly applicable in-flight; describes TCB placement methodology accounting for GN&C performance, navigation uncertainty, crew schedule, and thruster selection for the Artemis II free-return trajectory. Mission controllers are executing these burns on Days 3–4.

- **Title:** NASA's SLS Super Heavy-Lift Rocket in Process for Artemis II and Beyond
  - **Authors:** NASA MSFC SLS Team
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250011679 (December 22, 2025)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250011679)
  - **Relevance:** Describes hardware production status for Artemis II through V; confirms RS-25 new production engine certification in 2024 and EUS test article progress at MSFC.

- **Title:** Orion Artemis II Optical Communications Architecture, Implementation and Test Systems
  - **Authors:** NASA team
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250010065 (October 23, 2025)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250010065)
  - **Relevance:** Details the O2O optical communications payload providing up to 260 Mbps downlink for near-real-time 4K lunar imagery during the mission.

- **Title:** Hitching a Ride on History: NASA's Space Launch System Artemis II CubeSats
  - **Authors:** NASA team
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20250005942 (June 5, 2025)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20250005942)
  - **Relevance:** Describes the four 12U CubeSats from Artemis Accords international partners launched on SLS for science and technology demonstrations in highly elliptical Earth orbit.

- **Title:** NASA's Space Launch System: Powering Artemis and Enabling Discovery
  - **Authors:** NASA SLS Program
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20240014859 (November 20, 2024)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20240014859)
  - **Relevance:** Artemis I SLS performance data and lessons learned informing Artemis II configuration.

- **Title:** MoonAnything: A Vision Benchmark with Large-Scale Lunar Supervised Data
  - **Authors:** Clémentine Grethen, Yuang Shi, Simone Gasparini, et al.
  - **Source:** [arXiv:2604.00682v1](http://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00682v1) (submitted ~April 1, 2026)
  - **Relevance:** Learning-based visual perception benchmark for lunar surface navigation; supports autonomous systems development relevant to future Artemis surface missions.
