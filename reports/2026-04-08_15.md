# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-08 15:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is in mid-Flight Day 8, cruising home on a nominal trans-Earth coast following the successful lunar flyby on April 6. A second spacecraft bathroom issue emerged — a blocked urine overboard vent nozzle (possible ice or chemical debris) — requiring manual attitude maneuvers by the crew to point the vent toward the sun for a partial fix; the issue is non-critical and workarounds are in place. Two historic firsts were confirmed during Flight Day 7–8 coverage: the first ever "ship-to-ship" call between a crewed lunar spacecraft and the ISS, and Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney's live video connection with CSA astronaut Jeremy Hansen from space. Splashdown remains on target for April 10 at ~00:07 UTC off San Diego with a favorable weather forecast.

### Technical & Engineering

- **Second bathroom issue — urine vent nozzle blocked (Flight Day 7 into FD8):** A new plumbing problem developed separate from the earlier toilet pump malfunction (resolved April 2). Orion's urine/wastewater overboard vent nozzle became obstructed — possibly from ice buildup or a chemical reaction producing filter-clogging debris. Flight controllers directed the crew to manually maneuver Orion to point the vent nozzle toward the sun and use onboard heaters to attempt a thaw/clear. The fix was partially successful: crew dumped approximately half the urine tank before stopping. Crew was advised to continue using contingency collapsible urinal devices (CCUs) as a precaution. Issue assessed as non-critical and non-mission-impacting.
  - [Artemis II faces second bathroom issue with 'frozen urine' — Irish Star](https://www.irishstar.com/news/world-news/artemis-ii-bathroom-problems-urine-36972880)
  - [The Artemis 2 space toilet is actually working fine. But there is another problem — Space.com](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/artemis/the-artemis-2-space-toilet-is-actually-working-fine-but-there-is-another-problem)
  - [Artemis 2 crew fixes toilet, can now pee in it — Astronomy.com](https://www.astronomy.com/space-exploration/artemis-2-crew-fixes-toilet-can-now-pee-in-it/)
- **Radiation storm shelter construction drill (~00:15 UTC April 9 / ~8:15 PM ET April 8):** Confirmed as a scheduled test objective regardless of actual solar weather. Chief Flight Director Emily Nelson stated: "One of our test objectives is actually to set up the radiation shelter, so we'll be doing that anyway, even without a radiation event." Drill involves converting central stowage bays into a temporary shelter within 30 minutes. No actual solar event reported requiring unplanned shelter use on April 8.
- **Second manual piloting demonstration (~01:59 UTC April 9 / ~9:59 PM ET April 8):** Wiseman and Glover scheduled to manually pilot Orion in 6-DOF and 3-DOF thruster modes. Notably, the crew also performed unplanned manual attitude maneuvering earlier in FD8 to orient the vent nozzle toward the sun — providing additional operational data ahead of the formal test.
- **TCB-2 (April 9):** Second return trajectory correction burn upcoming; no specific burn time or delta-v values yet confirmed in indexed sources.
- **TCB-3 (April 9–10):** Final trajectory correction before entry interface; still on schedule.
- **Splashdown weather forecast (April 9 PDT / April 10 UTC):** Favorable. Rain not expected until approximately 11 PM PDT Friday night, well after the 5:07 PM PDT splashdown window. Contingency splashdown location near Guadalupe Island (west of Baja California) on standby but not currently needed.

### Science & Instruments

- **Solar radiation forecasting validation:** University of Michigan / NASA collaboration confirmed that machine-learning and physics-based models predicting solar energetic particle (SEP) storms up to 24 hours ahead are being validated in real time during Artemis II. No SEP events requiring action were reported on April 8. This represents a first live validation of these predictive models on a crewed deep-space mission.
  - [Artemis II to test new models that predict solar particle storms — Phys.org](https://phys.org/news/2026-04-artemis-ii-solar-particle-storms.html)
  - [Providing the Artemis mission with solar radiation forecasts — University of Michigan](https://news.umich.edu/providing-the-artemis-mission-with-solar-radiation-forecasts/)
- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** All 35 flyby observations from April 6 continue post-mission analysis at JSC; no new results yet published.
- **AVATAR (organ-on-a-chip), ARCHER (actigraphy), HERA/CAD dosimetry:** All continuing nominal data collection through trans-Earth coast.
