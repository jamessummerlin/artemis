# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-02 10:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II is on Flight Day 2, with the crew in a high Earth orbit checkout loop following yesterday's successful launch. The Orion spacecraft's waste management system (toilet) has been fully restored to normal operations after a brief controller fault. The critical Trans-Lunar Injection (TLI) burn is scheduled for **8:12 PM EDT tonight (00:12 UTC, April 3)**, with the mission management team convening this afternoon for the go/no-go decision that will commit the crew to a lunar trajectory.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** In-flight — Flight Day 2; TLI burn scheduled for tonight (8:12 PM EDT / 00:12 UTC April 3)
- **Launched:** April 1, 2026 at 6:35 PM EDT (22:35 UTC) from LC-39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Current Orbit:** High Earth checkout orbit, apogee ~43,730 miles (69,797 km)
- **TLI Burn Status:** NOT YET EXECUTED as of 10:00 UTC; go/no-go meeting scheduled for this afternoon
- **Splashdown Target:** April 10, 2026, Pacific Ocean off San Diego

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **Toilet issue resolved:** NASA published a Flight Day 2 blog post confirming the crew and ground teams at Johnson Space Center successfully troubleshot and restored Orion's waste management system to normal operations. The fault indicator (blinking light) that arose before the apogee raise burn has been cleared. [NASA Flight Update blog — April 2](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/02/artemis-ii-flight-update-crew-and-ground-teams-successfully-troubleshoot-orions-toilet/)
- **Go/no-go meeting scheduled:** The mission management team, led by acting associate administrator Lori Glaze (Exploration Systems Development Mission Directorate), will convene this afternoon to review Day 1 health data and issue a go/no-go recommendation for the TLI burn. Flight Operations Director Norm Knight noted: *"We're looking to make sure that the life-support systems work, the vehicle's healthy."*
- **Daily status briefings underway:** NASA JSC is holding daily mission status briefings through splashdown (except April 6, lunar flyby day). [NASA coverage page](https://www.nasa.gov/missions/artemis/artemis-2/nasa-sets-coverage-for-artemis-ii-moon-mission/)

### Technical & Engineering

- **TLI Burn Profile (Scheduled — Not Yet Complete):**
  - Scheduled time: 8:12 PM EDT / 00:12 UTC on April 3 (~25 hours 37 minutes after launch)
  - Burn duration: ~6 minutes, 5 seconds using the ESM orbital maneuvering system main engine
  - Delta-V: ~900 mph added velocity
  - Trajectory: Free-return figure-eight cislunar path; no Lunar Orbit Insertion required
  - This is the **last major engine firing** of the mission
  - If not executed, Artemis II goes no farther than Earth orbit

- **CubeSat Deployments Confirmed (Flight Day 1, ~5 hours after launch):**
  Four international CubeSats deployed from the Orion Stage Adapter ring in sequential one-minute intervals:
  - **TACHELES** (Germany / DLR): Studies how space affects electrical components for future lunar vehicles
  - **K-RadCube** (South Korea / Korea AeroSpace Administration): Contains humanlike tissue to measure radiation effects across the Van Allen belts
  - **Space Weather CubeSat-1** (Saudi Space Agency): Collects data on solar X-rays, energetic particles, and magnetic fields
  - **ATENEA** (Argentina / CONAE): 12U-class CubeSat testing radiation shielding and long-range communications; built in collaboration with the National University of La Plata, UNSAM, and University of Buenos Aires

  All four nations are Artemis Accords signatories; Argentina is the first Latin American country to contribute a CubeSat to an Artemis mission. [MercoPress](https://en.mercopress.com/2026/03/31/argentine-microsatellite-to-fly-on-nasa-s-artemis-2-moon-mission)

- **Orbital shaping complete:** Both the apogee raise burn (~18 minutes) and the perigee raise burn (Flight Day 2, following crew wake-up at 7 AM EDT) have been completed, setting up the final parking orbit ahead of TLI.

- **Deep Space Network handover:** Prior to TLI, Orion must complete a planned communications transition from NASA's Near Space Network to the Deep Space Network.

- **Free-return trajectory parameters:**
  - Closest lunar approach: ~6,513 km from lunar far-side surface (Day 6, April 6)
  - Maximum Earth distance: ~252,799 statute miles — expected to set a new human spaceflight record
  - Reentry speed: ~25,000 mph; steeper descent angle to mitigate heat shield spalling observed on Artemis I

### Science & Instruments

- **CubeSat science begins:** Four deployed CubeSats are now maneuvering to intended orbits and beginning data collection (radiation, space weather, component testing).
- **Optical communications (O2O):** The Orion Optical Communications System will transmit near-real-time 4K video of the Moon at up to 260 Mbps during the lunar flyby.
- **Human research:** Crew exercise protocol has begun. Mission Specialist Christina Koch and Jeremy Hansen are scheduled for 30-minute workouts on a super-light flywheel device (smaller than a carry-on suitcase) capable of rowing, squats, and deadlifts — the regimen will continue nearly every day.
- **Radiation monitoring:** Passive and active dosimeters are recording cumulative dose as Orion passes through the Van Allen belts toward cislunar space.

### Crew Updates

- **Commander Reid Wiseman:** Overseeing the TLI go/no-go assessment and all spacecraft operations.
- **Pilot Victor Glover:** Successfully completed the proximity operations demonstration on Day 1, manually flying Orion to within ~30 feet of the ICPS. Preparing for TLI burn execution.
- **Mission Specialist Christina Koch:** Dedicated much of the morning of April 2 to TLI preparation, including systems checks and control panel configuration. Will become the first woman to travel to the lunar vicinity.
- **Mission Specialist Jeremy Hansen (CSA):** On his first spaceflight, making him the first Canadian and first non-U.S. citizen to travel toward the Moon. A first live space-to-ground Q&A with Hansen is scheduled for April 4. [CSA event](https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/events/2026/2026-04-04-artemis-ii-first-q-and-a-jeremy-hansen-live-from-space.asp)

### International Partners

**ESA:**
- The European Service Module (ESM-2) continues to perform nominally. All 33 engines (1 main, 8 auxiliary, 24 RCS) are available; four solar array wings deployed and generating power; life support providing O₂ and H₂O to the crew — the first time the ESM's life support has been operated with crew aboard.
- ESA engineers are on console around the clock at ESTEC (Netherlands), ESOC (Germany), and JSC Houston. ESA issued a launch-day press release: ["Europe powers Artemis II mission to the Moon"](https://www.esa.int/Newsroom/Press_Releases/Europe_powers_Artemis_II_mission_to_the_Moon) and a dedicated mission-begun page. [ESA Artemis II mission begins](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Artemis_II_mission_begins)
- Post-mission, the ESM will separate from the crew module and burn up harmlessly in the atmosphere before splashdown.

**CSA:**
- Jeremy Hansen is healthy and active on Flight Day 2. Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney spoke with President Trump about the mission on launch day.
- CSA astronauts David Saint-Jacques and Joshua Kutryk served as public liaisons at the John H. Chapman Space Centre and KSC respectively during the launch.
- Argentina's ATENEA CubeSat reflects the broader Artemis Accords international coalition participating in the mission.

## Academic & Research Papers

- **Title:** Cislunar State and Uncertainty Propagation via the Modified Generalized Equinoctial Orbital Elements
  - **Authors:** (Authors listed at arXiv)
  - **Source:** [arXiv:2603.20110](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20110) (~March 2026)
  - **Relevance:** Addresses trajectory propagation and uncertainty characterization in the cislunar domain with full Earth–Moon–Sun perturbation models — directly applicable to Artemis II navigation as Orion prepares for TLI and the 4-day coast to the Moon.

- **Title:** An Open Benchmark of One Million High-Fidelity Cislunar Trajectories
  - **Authors:** (SSAPy team)
  - **Source:** [arXiv:2512.11064](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.11064) (December 2025)
  - **Relevance:** Open dataset of numerically propagated cislunar trajectories; supports navigation method development and space domain awareness tools applicable to Artemis operations.

- **Title:** Optimizing Earth-Moon Transfer and Cislunar Navigation: Integrating Low-Energy Trajectories, AI Techniques and GNSS-R Technologies
  - **Authors:** (listed at arXiv)
  - **Source:** [arXiv:2511.03173](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.03173) (November 2025)
  - **Relevance:** Reviews low-energy transfer strategies, cislunar GNSS limitations, and AI/ML-based trajectory refinement — contextually relevant to Artemis II's free-return trajectory design and future autonomous surface missions.

- **Title:** Optimized Trajectory Correction Burn Placement for the NASA Artemis II Mission
  - **Authors:** David Woffinden, Randy Eckman, Shane Robinson (NASA JSC)
  - **Source:** [NASA NTRS 20230000223 (AAS 23-062)](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20230000223)
  - **Relevance:** Defines TCB placement methodology for the Artemis II free-return trajectory; mission controllers will execute these burns on Days 3–4 post-TLI.
