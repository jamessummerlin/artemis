# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-14 17:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II has successfully completed its ~10-day crewed lunar flyby mission, with the crew now in Day 4 of postflight reconditioning at NASA's Johnson Space Center in Houston. The mission set a new human spaceflight distance record of 252,756 miles from Earth and marked the first crewed cislunar mission since Apollo 17 in 1972. Post-mission analysis is underway across multiple fronts: Orion heat shield inspections, AVATAR bone marrow chip RNA sequencing, and planning for the April 16 crew postflight news conference.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — crew reconditioning (Day 4), Orion engineering inspections at KSC, science sample analysis
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026, 22:35:12 UTC from LC-39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown:** April 10, 2026, ~00:07 UTC (April 10, 5:07 p.m. PDT), Pacific Ocean SW of San Diego
- **Mission Duration:** ~9 days, 1 hour, 31 minutes
- **Notable Changes:**
  - Crew returned to JSC in Houston on April 11; all four astronauts in standard postflight reconditioning protocols
  - Orion heat shield formal 30-day inspection period underway; report due ~May 10, 2026
  - Lunar Gateway officially cancelled March 24, 2026; NASA shifting to $20B lunar surface base program
  - Artemis III redefined as crewed LEO test (no lunar landing); Artemis IV now targeted as first crewed lunar landing (~2028)
  - TACHELES CubeSat (Germany/NEUROSPACE) deployed but communications not yet established

## Key Updates

### Official NASA Updates

- **Postflight crew news conference — April 16, 2:30 p.m. EDT (18:30 UTC):** All four crew members (Wiseman, Glover, Koch, Hansen) will answer questions at JSC. Livestreamed on NASA TV and YouTube. This is the first public opportunity for the full crew to speak in detail about the mission since splashdown.
  - [NASA to Host Artemis II Crew Postflight News Conference — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-to-host-artemis-ii-crew-postflight-news-conference/)

- **Mission success summary:** The crew completed all primary test flight objectives: life support system validation, manual Orion piloting, translunar injection burn, proximity operations demonstration with the spent ICPS, lunar flyby, and skip-free direct reentry. The crew captured more than 7,000 lunar surface images and documented six meteoroid impact flashes during the April 6 flyby.

### Technical & Engineering

- **Orion heat shield — modified direct-entry trajectory:** Rather than use the skip-reentry planned originally, NASA modified the trajectory to a steeper direct entry to limit the thermal environment exposure, following the unexpected char loss seen on Artemis I. The capsule re-entered at ~24,664 mph. Initial visual inspections by heat shield experts aboard the recovery ship have begun; detailed thermal scans will follow at KSC. A formal 30-day engineering report is due ~May 10, 2026. This data will directly inform the Artemis IV schedule.
  - [NASA confident Artemis 2 heat shield will protect crew during re-entry — Spaceflight Now](https://spaceflightnow.com/2026/04/10/nasa-confident-artemis-ii-heat-shield-will-protect-crew-during-re-entry/)
  - [Orion splashes down to successfully end Artemis 2 mission — SpaceNews](https://spacenews.com/orion-splashes-down-to-successfully-end-artemis-2-mission/)

- **ESM helium pressurant valve — anomaly flagged:** The European Service Module's helium pressurant valve exhibited a higher-than-expected leak rate during flight. ESA and Airbus Defence and Space are conducting root-cause investigation; design modifications will be required for Artemis IV's more demanding propulsion profile. Notably, the TLI burn was so precise that two planned trajectory correction maneuvers were cancelled entirely.
  - [ESA — Splashdown for Artemis II](https://www.esa.int/Newsroom/Press_Releases/Splashdown_for_Artemis_II)

- **Mission trajectory overview:** After launch, Orion spent ~24 hours in highly elliptical high Earth orbit with crew systems checkout and proximity operations with the spent ICPS. Following mission manager go-ahead, the TLI burn sent Orion on a free-return trajectory around the Moon's far side. Closest lunar approach was ~4,067 miles above the surface on April 6. Maximum Earth distance reached 252,756 miles — 4,111 miles farther than Apollo 13 (previous record).
  - [Artemis II — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artemis_II)
  - [NASA's Artemis II Mission Leaves Earth Orbit for Flight around Moon — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasas-artemis-ii-mission-leaves-earth-orbit-for-flight-around-moon/)
  - [NASA's Artemis II Crew Eclipses Record for Farthest Human Spaceflight — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasas-artemis-ii-crew-eclipses-record-for-farthest-human-spaceflight/)

- **SLS performance — Block 1:** Eight solid rocket boosters and four RS-25 engines generated 8.8 million pounds of thrust at liftoff. The SRBs delivered more than 75% of total thrust during the first two minutes, then separated as planned. The ICPS executed the TLI burn successfully. No major SLS anomalies reported.
  - [Space Launch System Reference Guide — NASA (PDF)](https://www.nasa.gov/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/sls-5558-artemis-ii-sls-reference-guide.pdf)

- **TACHELES CubeSat — comms not established:** The German NEUROSPACE CubeSat TACHELES, designed to study radiation effects on rover electronics in the Van Allen belts, was successfully deployed in high Earth orbit but ground stations have not yet established radio contact. The spacecraft is designed to operate for two years; engineers continue troubleshooting.
  - [Artemis II launches to the Moon – with German and European tech on board — DLR](https://www.dlr.de/en/latest/news/2026/artemis-ii-launches-to-the-moon-with-german-and-european-tech-on-board)

### Science & Instruments

- **AVATAR (A Virtual Astronaut Tissue Analog Response):** Organ-on-a-chip devices containing bone marrow tissue grown from the astronauts' own cells flew alongside the crew throughout the mission, experiencing identical deep-space radiation and microgravity. Now back on Earth, the Harvard Wyss Institute / Emulate team will perform single-cell RNA sequencing to measure gene expression changes in tens of thousands of individual cells. Parallel Earth-based chips serve as controls. Findings will guide protective countermeasures (e.g., antioxidants) for future long-duration missions.
  - [Artemis II AVATAR Science — NASA Science](https://science.nasa.gov/biological-physical/investigations/avatar/)
  - [Harvard Wyss Institute announces AVATAR breakthrough — Wyss Institute](https://wyss.harvard.edu/news/wyss-institute-technologies-enable-breakthrough-in-astronaut-health-research-aboard-nasas-artemis-ii-mission/)
  - [The Artemis II Mission Included a Harvard Space Medicine Experiment — Harvard Magazine](https://www.harvardmagazine.com/science/harvard-wyss-institute-artemis-avatar-space-medicine)

- **DLR M-42 EXT radiation detectors:** Four extended radiation detectors from the German Aerospace Center measured deep-space radiation between Earth and the Moon at 6× the energy measurement range of Artemis I's sensors. This dataset will provide the highest-resolution radiation environment characterization ever collected on a crewed cislunar trajectory.
  - [DLR — Artemis II launches to the Moon with German and European tech](https://www.dlr.de/en/latest/news/2026/artemis-ii-launches-to-the-moon-with-german-and-european-tech-on-board)

- **International CubeSats deployed:** Space agencies from Germany (TACHELES), South Korea, Saudi Arabia, and Argentina flew CubeSats aboard Artemis II in high Earth orbit, each with distinct science and technology objectives. South Korea's CubeSat is actively conducting its radiation mission.
  - [South Korea CubeSat begins radiation mission on Artemis 2 — UPI](https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2026/04/02/satellite-cubesat/5711775174421/)

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** 7,000+ images of the lunar surface captured during the April 6 flyby, plus documentation of six meteoroid impact flashes on the night side — a scientifically rare direct observation from crewed spacecraft. Ground scientists are cross-referencing impact events with Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter data. The Unistellar citizen-science network mobilized ground telescopes during the flyby window to independently observe the same events.
  - [Lunar Flashes with Artemis II — Unistellar](https://science.unistellar.com/planetary-defense/missions/lunar-flashes-with-artemis-ii/)
  - [Artemis II Flight Day 6: Lunar Flyby Updates — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/06/artemis-ii-flight-day-6-lunar-flyby-updates/)

- **ARCHeR framework:** Bio-behavioral and human health data from the ARCHeR (Artemis Radiation and Crew Health Response) framework are in active analysis at JSC, including blood and saliva samples.
  - [PROJECT ARCHeR: Artemis II Update — NTRS 20260002430](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/20260002430)

- **National Academies science study:** A current National Academies study (sponsored by NASA) will produce a report later in 2026 identifying key non-polar lunar landing sites and science goals for future crewed missions, informed by Artemis II observational data and crew geological expertise.
  - [Following the Success of Artemis II, Scientists Will Shape What Comes Next — National Academies](https://www.nationalacademies.org/news/following-the-success-of-artemis-ii-scientists-will-shape-what-comes-next-for-lunar-exploration)

### Crew Updates

- **All four crew members healthy:** Reid Wiseman (Commander), Victor Glover (Pilot), Christina Koch (Mission Specialist), and Jeremy Hansen (Mission Specialist/CSA) are in Day 4 of standard postflight reconditioning at JSC, including cardiovascular, vestibular, musculoskeletal, and human performance evaluations. Standard reconditioning runs 21–45 days total.

- **Historic firsts:** Victor Glover became the first person of color; Christina Koch the first woman; Jeremy Hansen the first non-U.S. citizen; and Reid Wiseman the oldest person to travel beyond low Earth orbit and fly around the Moon.

- **April 16 public appearance:** Full crew will speak at the postflight news conference at 2:30 p.m. EDT at JSC (NASA TV / YouTube).
  - [NASA to Host Artemis II Crew Postflight News Conference](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-to-host-artemis-ii-crew-postflight-news-conference/)

- **First remarks post-splashdown:** Commander Wiseman stated: *"Victor, Christina and Jeremy, we are, we are bonded forever, and no one down here is ever going to know what the four of us just went through. And it was the most special thing that will ever happen in my life."*
  - [What the Artemis II crew shared in first remarks after return — CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/11/science/artemis-2-astronauts-first-remarks-post-splashdown)

### International Partners

- **ESA — European Service Module:** ESM performed all primary functions. The precision of the TLI burn cancelled two scheduled trajectory correction burns. Anomaly: helium pressurant valve leak rate higher than predicted; root-cause investigation and redesign required for Artemis IV. ESA and Airbus Defence and Space are assessing scope of required modifications.
  - [ESA — Europe powers Artemis II mission to the Moon](https://www.esa.int/Newsroom/Press_Releases/Europe_powers_Artemis_II_mission_to_the_Moon)
  - [ESA — Artemis II: around the Moon in 10 days (video)](https://www.esa.int/ESA_Multimedia/Videos/2026/04/Artemis_II_around_the_Moon_in_10_days)

- **CSA — Jeremy Hansen:** Canadian astronaut Hansen completed the mission as a Mission Specialist, becoming the first non-U.S. citizen to travel to the vicinity of the Moon. The CSA's Canadarm3 contribution to the (now-cancelled) Lunar Gateway remains in renegotiation following NASA's pivot to a lunar surface base.
  - [The Artemis campaign — Canadian Space Agency](https://www.asc-csa.gc.ca/eng/astronomy/moon-exploration/artemis-missions.asp)

- **Lunar Gateway cancelled — international fallout:** NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman officially cancelled the Lunar Gateway on March 24, 2026, pivoting to a $20 billion lunar surface base program over seven years. This affects Gateway hardware commitments from ESA (ESPRIT module), JAXA, and CSA. NASA plans to repurpose the Power and Propulsion Element (PPE) and HALO module toward the surface base program. Negotiations with international partners are ongoing.
  - [NASA halts work on Gateway to develop a lunar base — SpaceNews](https://spacenews.com/nasa-halts-work-on-gateway-to-develop-a-lunar-base/)
  - [NASA to spend $20 billion on moon base — CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/24/nasa-to-spend-20-billion-on-moon-base-cancel-orbiting-lunar-station.html)
