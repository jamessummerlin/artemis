# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-13 12:00 UTC

## Summary

No significant new developments have emerged since the 11:00 UTC report. The Artemis II crew (Day 11 post-splashdown) continues post-flight reconditioning and medical evaluations at NASA's Johnson Space Center in Houston. Key pending items — Artemis III crew announcement, White House visit date, IAU crater naming decision, Orion heat shield inspection, and ESM helium valve redesign — remain outstanding with no new information as of this report cycle.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — Day 11 crew post-flight reconditioning, medical/human-performance evaluations, and lunar science debriefs at NASA Johnson Space Center
- **Mission Duration:** 10 days (April 1–10, 2026); splashdown April 10 at 8:07 p.m. EDT (00:07 UTC April 11) in the Pacific Ocean ~96 km off San Diego; recovered by USS *John P. Murtha* (LPD 26)
- **Total distance flown:** 694,481 miles; maximum distance from Earth: 252,756 miles (406,771 km) — a new human spaceflight record
- **Notable Changes:**
  - No new schedule, architecture, or personnel changes since the 11:00 UTC report
  - ESM helium valve leak confirmed ~10× worse than ground-test levels; redesign required for Artemis IV
  - Lunar Gateway cancelled (March 2026); Artemis III redesignated as LEO rendezvous/docking test (mid-2027); Artemis IV (~early 2028) planned as first crewed lunar landing

### Technical & Engineering

*No new engineering or inspection reports published since 11:00 UTC. Standing status:*

- **UWMS (toilet) post-flight investigation:** Leading theory is a chemical-reaction-driven debris clog in the vent line filter; ground-team telemetry review ongoing. Full confirmation expected once Orion "Integrity" is accessed at KSC.
  - [The Artemis 2 space toilet is actually working fine. But there is another problem — Space.com](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/artemis/the-artemis-2-space-toilet-is-actually-working-fine-but-there-is-another-problem)
  - [Let's Talk About the Artemis II Toilet Problems — PJ Media](https://pjmedia.com/rick-moran/2026/04/12/lets-talk-about-the-artemis-ii-toilet-problems-n4951706) *(April 12)*
- **ESM helium propulsion valve leak:** ~10× larger in flight than ground-test levels; internal (no external venting). No flight-safety risk during Artemis II free-return. Redesign required before Artemis IV.
- **Orion heat shield (30-day inspection):** CT-scan of AVCOAT ablative material underway at KSC; full report expected ~May 10, 2026. Modified skip re-entry trajectory (higher sustained heating; two lower-intensity heating events; 5,524-mile landing range) performed as planned. Divers photographed the heat shield immediately after splashdown.
  - [NASA confident Artemis 2 heat shield will protect crew during re-entry — Spaceflight Now](https://spaceflightnow.com/2026/04/10/nasa-confident-artemis-ii-heat-shield-will-protect-crew-during-re-entry/)
- **SLS architecture update:** NASA selected ULA Centaur V (dual RL10 engines) to replace the ICPS/Exploration Upper Stage from Artemis IV onward; Mobile Launcher 2 development cancelled (Feb. 27, 2026).
  - [NASA Overhauls Artemis Program: Scraps SLS Upgrades, Adds 2027 LEO Test Mission — SatNews](https://satnews.com/2026/02/27/nasa-overhauls-artemis-program-scraps-sls-upgrades-adds-2027-leo-test-mission/)

### Science & Instruments

*No new science results published since 11:00 UTC. Standing status:*

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO):** 7,000+ high-resolution images and video from the April 6 lunar flyby (closest approach 4,067 mi / 6,545 km from the far-side surface) being processed at JSC. Six meteoroid impact flashes recorded on the darkened lunar surface; hazard-modeling analysis ongoing.
- **Distance record:** 252,756 miles (406,771 km) from Earth on April 6, 2026, surpassing Apollo 13's 248,655 mi (1970) by more than 4,000 miles.
- **AVATAR Bone Marrow Organ Chips:** Hardware recovered post-splashdown; comparison analysis of flight chips vs. ground controls underway at Wyss Institute (Harvard). Organ chips contained bone marrow tissue derived from crew cells, flown alongside astronauts throughout the 10-day mission.
  - [Why NASA sent 'organ chips' of the Artemis II crew into space — CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/09/science/artemis-2-human-health-experiments)
  - [Wyss Institute technologies enable breakthrough in astronaut health research — Wyss Institute / Harvard](https://wyss.harvard.edu/news/wyss-institute-technologies-enable-breakthrough-in-astronaut-health-research-aboard-nasas-artemis-ii-mission/)
- **ARCHeR (Artemis Research for Crew Health and Readiness):** Wearable sensors tracked crew heart rate, sleep, cognitive function, and team dynamics throughout mission; actigraphy and audiovisual data being processed through machine-learning pipeline. See NTRS 20260002430.
- **Radiation dosimetry (M-42 EXT detectors + personal dosimeters):** Preliminary readings within ~10% of predicted margins; six radiation sensors aboard Orion plus crew personal monitors; full analysis ongoing.
- **International CubeSats:** CubeSats from Germany (*TACHELES*), Argentina (*ATENEA*), South Korea, and Saudi Arabia deployed in high Earth orbit; data analysis ongoing; no new results published.
- **IAU crater naming:** NASA will formally submit proposals for *Carroll* and *Integrity* craters (named by crew during flyby); IAU decision expected ~mid-May 2026.
  - [Artemis II names moon crater Carroll after Reid Wiseman's late wife — NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/07/nx-s1-5776440/artemis-moon-crater-carroll-wiseman)
