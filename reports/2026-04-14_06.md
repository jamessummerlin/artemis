# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-14 06:00 UTC

## Summary

Artemis II has concluded successfully: the crew splashed down April 10, 2026, at ~00:07 UTC (~50 miles off San Diego), returned to Houston on April 11, and all four astronauts are healthy and in post-flight reconditioning at JSC. A postflight crew news conference is scheduled for April 16 at 2:30 p.m. EDT. NASA is now assessing Orion's modified heat shield, processing 7,000+ lunar flyby images and science experiment data, and advancing Artemis III planning after confirming the cancellation of the Lunar Gateway.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — crew reconditioning, engineering data analysis, science debriefs at JSC
- **Launch Date:** April 1, 2026 at 22:35:12 UTC, Launch Complex 39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Splashdown:** April 10, 2026 at ~00:07 UTC (~5:07 p.m. PDT / 8:07 p.m. EDT April 9), Pacific Ocean ~50 miles off San Diego; recovered by USS John P. Murtha (LPD 26)
- **Mission Duration:** ~9 days, 1 hour, 31 minutes (approximately)
- **Notable Changes:**
  - All primary mission objectives achieved; mission declared a complete success
  - NASA shelved the Lunar Gateway (confirmed March 2026); Artemis III redesigned as Earth-orbit lander docking test; Artemis IV now first crewed lunar landing target (~2028)
  - Crew health status: all four astronauts "green" on recovery; Victor Glover walked unaided off the recovery raft
  - Heat shield post-flight inspection and engineering assessment underway; early indications nominal under modified loft-reentry profile
  - Crater names "Integrity" and "Carroll" formally proposed to the IAU; decision expected within ~1 month

### Technical & Engineering

- **Re-entry and splashdown (April 10, 00:07 UTC):** Orion "Integrity" executed a textbook "loft" (non-skip) re-entry to manage heat shield heating; described as a "bullseye landing" by Live Science. Recovery was the first Navy-led crew recovery since the Apollo–Soyuz Test Project in 1975.
  - ['Welcome home, Integrity': Artemis II crew safely returned — Live Science](https://www.livescience.com/space/live/artemis-ii-returns-friday-april-10)
- **Heat shield assessment:** Pre-flight, NASA modified the Artemis II reentry trajectory to use a loft profile (vs. skip entry used on Artemis I) to create more favorable heating conditions and limit Avcoat cracking. Post-mission inspection by engineers is underway at JSC. Early NASA statements express confidence.
  - [NASA confident Artemis 2 heat shield will protect crew during re-entry — Spaceflight Now](https://spaceflightnow.com/2026/04/10/nasa-confident-artemis-ii-heat-shield-will-protect-crew-during-re-entry/)
  - ['It's 13 minutes of things that have to go right' — Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/04/10/artemis-ii-lands-pacific-heat-shield-issues/)
- **All primary objectives achieved:** Life support systems tested; manual piloting by all four crew members (Koch/Hansen on FD4; Wiseman/Glover on FD8/9); lunar flyby trajectory executed; safe re-entry and recovery completed.
  - [Artemis II: NASA's First Crewed Lunar Flyby in 50 Years — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/mission/artemis-ii/)
- **Return trajectory correction burns:** TCB-Return-1 completed April 7 (15 sec, 1.6 ft/s ΔV); TCB-Return-2 confirmed executed; TCB-Return-3 (final) executed prior to entry interface April 10.

### Science & Instruments

- **7,000+ lunar flyby images:** Images released publicly including Earthset and Earthrise from the lunar far side, Orientale Basin (never before seen with human eyes), Vavilov Crater, impact craters, ancient lava flows, surface fractures, color variations, Milky Way, and six observed meteoroid impact flashes on the darkened lunar surface.
  - [New images from Artemis II's historic lunar flyby reveal 'Earthset' and eclipse — CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/07/science/artemis-2-lunar-flyby-images-earthset)
  - [Earthset From the Lunar Far Side — NASA Science/Earth Observatory](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/earth-observatory/earthset-from-the-lunar-far-side/)
  - [Artemis II Science Data 2026: First Results from Vavilov Crater & Radiation Tests](https://www.collegesimplified.in/post/artemis-ii-science-data-downlink-flight-day-7-2026)
- **AVATAR investigation:** Bio-sample collection and analysis underway at JSC; data on human tissue response to deep space microgravity and radiation environment being processed.
- **ARCHER (Artemis Research for Crew Health and Readiness):** Post-mission data collection from crew ongoing per the NTRS 20260002430 protocol. Crew health data will inform long-duration mission planning.
- **CLO (Crew Lunar Observations):** 7,000+ images being analyzed at JSC; post-flight science debriefs scheduled for the crew.
- **Solar energetic particle (SEP) / radiation dosimetry:** No significant solar particle events during the mission; radiation dosimetry data from crew being analyzed.
- **Crater naming — Carroll and Integrity:**
  - *Carroll crater*: Informally named April 6 by the crew after Commander Reid Wiseman's late wife, Carroll Taylor Wiseman (d. 2020). Positioned on the near/far-side boundary, visible from Earth at certain points in the lunar cycle.
  - *Integrity crater*: Named after the Orion capsule; symbolizes values guiding human space exploration.
  - Formal IAU submissions now in progress; a ruling is expected within ~1 month.
  - [Artemis II names moon crater Carroll after Reid Wiseman's late wife — NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/07/nx-s1-5776440/artemis-moon-crater-carroll-wiseman)
  - [Carroll crater — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carroll_(crater))
  - [Integrity crater — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrity_(crater))

### Crew Updates

| Astronaut | Role | Agency | Historic Distinction |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Reid Wiseman** | Commander | NASA | Oldest person to travel beyond LEO; named "Carroll" crater in honor of late wife |
| **Victor Glover** | Pilot | NASA | First person of color to travel beyond LEO and near the Moon; walked unaided off recovery raft |
| **Christina Koch** | Mission Specialist | NASA | First woman to travel beyond LEO |
| **Jeremy Hansen** | Mission Specialist | CSA | First non-U.S. citizen and first Canadian to reach the lunar vicinity |

- All four crew members confirmed "green" (healthy) on recovery raft following splashdown; no medical anomalies reported.
- Victor Glover notably walked unaided for most of his path to the medical bay aboard USS John P. Murtha — a positive sign for post-deep-space crew recovery.
- Crew arrived at Ellington Field, Houston (JSC), on April 11, 2026; met with families and NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman.
- Crew are currently undergoing standard postflight reconditioning, medical and human performance evaluations, and lunar science debriefs.
- First crew remarks post-splashdown shared with media April 11.

### International Partners

- **CSA (Canadian Space Agency):**
  - Jeremy Hansen's participation marked the first time a non-U.S. citizen traveled to the lunar vicinity; Hansen was celebrated in Canada with public and government events.
  - The April 8 call between PM Mark Carney and Hansen (hosted by CSA astronaut Joshua Kutryk at John H. - **ESA (European Space Agency):**
  - European Service Module (ESM) performed nominally throughout the mission; post-flight ESM performance data now being analyzed by Airbus/ESA.
  - ESA released mission begins summary.
  - [ESA — Artemis II mission begins](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Human_and_Robotic_Exploration/Artemis_II_mission_begins)
- **Lunar Gateway — cancelled:** In March 2026, NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman confirmed the Lunar Gateway space station project has been shelved. Commercial partnerships and 30 planned robotic lunar landings in 2027 are the new focus.
