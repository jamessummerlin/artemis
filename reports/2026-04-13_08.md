# Artemis II Mission Update — 2026-04-13 08:00 UTC

## Summary

No major new developments have emerged in the ~3 hours since the 05:00 UTC report. The Artemis II crew (Day 7 post-splashdown) continues post-flight reconditioning at NASA's Johnson Space Center; no new public appearances or White House visit date have been announced. Ongoing analysis of the Orion heat shield, ESM helium valve data, and CLO lunar imagery remains the primary post-mission technical focus.

## Mission Status

- **Current Phase:** Post-mission — Day 7 crew post-flight reconditioning, medical/human-performance evaluations, and lunar science debriefs at NASA Johnson Space Center
- **Mission Duration:** 10 days (April 1–10, 2026); splashdown April 10 at 8:07 p.m. EDT (00:07 UTC April 11) in the Pacific Ocean ~96 km off San Diego; recovered by USS *John P. Murtha* (LPD 26)
- **Notable Changes:**
  - No new schedule or architecture changes since post-splashdown press conference
  - ESM helium valve leak confirmed as ~10× worse than ground-test levels; extensive redesign needed for Artemis IV
  - Lunar Gateway cancelled (March 2026); Artemis III redesignated as LEO rendezvous/docking test (mid-2027); Artemis IV (~early 2028) remains planned as first crewed lunar landing

### Technical & Engineering

- **ESM helium propulsion valve leak (ongoing analysis):**
  - The helium leak in the European Service Module's propulsion pressurization valve system was confirmed as approximately **10× larger in flight** than measured in pre-flight ground testing.
  - The leak was internal (across valves, not venting to space) and posed no flight safety risk for Artemis II, which used a free-return trajectory that did not require full propulsion pressurization.
  - NASA noted the current valve system could likely be used for Artemis III (LEO only; no need for full pressurization), but will require an **extensive redesign** for Artemis IV, which requires full pressurization for lunar orbit insertion.
  - [Orion Helium Leak No Threat to Artemis II Reentry, But Will Require Redesign — Nsane Forums](https://nsaneforums.com/news/general-news/orion-helium-leak-no-threat-to-artemis-ii-reentry-but-will-require-redesign-r34500)
  - [Artemis 3 and Beyond: What's Next for NASA — Space.com](https://www.space.com/space-exploration/artemis/artemis-3-and-beyond-whats-next-for-nasa-after-artemis-2-moon-success)
- **Orion heat shield (30-day inspection underway):**
  - Orion capsule "Integrity" at KSC undergoing CT scanning of AVCOAT ablative heat shield. The modified steeper/shorter-skip reentry trajectory was designed to address the char-loss anomaly seen after Artemis I. Officials noted that heat shield data from Artemis II "could define the timeline of future lunar missions."
  - Full CT-scan and inspection report expected within ~30 days of splashdown (by ~May 10, 2026).
  - [NASA Shares Orion Heat Shield Findings, Updates Artemis Moon Missions — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasa-shares-orion-heat-shield-findings-updates-artemis-moon-missions/)
  - [Heat Shield Data from Artemis II Could Define Timeline of Future Lunar Missions — Scripps News](https://www.scrippsnews.com/science-and-tech/space/heat-shield-data-from-artemis-ii-could-define-artemis-iv-timeline)
- **Manual piloting data analysis (ongoing):** Data from two manual piloting demonstration sessions is being analyzed to refine Orion handling qualities for crew rendezvous/docking training ahead of Artemis III and IV.
- **Artemis III mission profile (confirmed, revised):** Mid-2027 LEO rendezvous and docking demonstration with SpaceX Starship HLS and/or Blue Origin Blue Moon; includes AxEMU spacesuit evaluation. No lunar landing.
  - [Explainer: What's Next for NASA After Artemis II? — U.S. News](https://www.usnews.com/news/national-news/articles/2026-04-08/explainer-whats-next-for-nasa-after-artemis-ii-a-mission-to-test-lunar-docking-and-mars-tech)

### Science & Instruments

- **Crew Lunar Observations (CLO) — analysis underway:** 7,000+ high-resolution images and video from the ~7-hour lunar flyby (April 6; closest approach 4,067 miles / 6,545 km) are being processed at JSC. Targets included Orientale Basin (~600-mile-wide, ~3.8 billion-year-old impact structure), Reiner Gamma swirl, Glushko Crater, Vavilov Crater, and Hertzsprung Basin. Crew color discrimination reports are being cross-referenced with LRO and Kaguya datasets. The crew observed parts of the far side illuminated — views unprecedented in human history.
  - [NASA's Artemis II Crew Beams Official Moon Flyby Photos to Earth — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasas-artemis-ii-crew-beams-official-moon-flyby-photos-to-earth/)
  - [Earthset From the Lunar Far Side — NASA Earth Observatory](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/earth-observatory/earthset-from-the-lunar-far-side/)
  - [Historic Artemis II Moon Fly-by: Nature's Live Coverage — Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-00989-9)
  - [What the Artemis II Astronauts Saw From Space — NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/07/nx-s1-5776824/artemis-astronauts-moon-observation-nasa-human-eye)
- **Distance record set:** 252,756 miles (406,771 km) from Earth on April 6, surpassing Apollo 13's record (248,655 mi / 400,171 km, 1970); total mission distance 695,081 miles.
  - [NASA's Artemis II Crew Eclipses Record for Farthest Human Spaceflight — NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasas-artemis-ii-crew-eclipses-record-for-farthest-human-spaceflight/)
- **Solar corona / meteoroid impacts:** Six distinct meteoroid impact flashes on the darkened lunar surface recorded during the flyby; data being analyzed to refine lunar impact hazard models.
- **AVATAR organ-on-a-chip:** Hardware recovered; Bone Marrow Chips at Wyss Institute (Harvard) for comparison against ground controls. Analysis expected to take months.
  - [Wyss Institute Technologies Enable Breakthrough in Astronaut Health Research — Wyss Institute](https://wyss.harvard.edu/news/wyss-institute-technologies-enable-breakthrough-in-astronaut-health-research-aboard-nasas-artemis-ii-mission/)
- **Radiation dosimetry:** M-42 EXT detectors showing preliminary readings within ~10% of predicted margins. Full analysis ongoing.
